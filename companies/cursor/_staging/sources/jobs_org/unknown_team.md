Blog / company

One of the best parts of working on Cursor is collaborating with such an excellent team. We wanted to take a moment to highlight a representative sample of the great people we get to work with:

Aman Karmani — VP of Infra at GitHub, creator of the GitHub merge button and built some of the world’s best Ruby profilers.

Jeremy Stribling — founding engineer at Keybase building core distributed systems, led development for KBFS. Led security for Zoom as a senior principal engineer.

Jacob Jackson — inventor of Tabnine (the first code completion tool), same.energy, and Supermaven (the first shipped 1M context model). Early researcher at OpenAI, where he independently invented speculative decoding.

Ian Huang — created Voxelize, a high-performance game engine for multiplayer Minecraft in the browser.

Sasha Rush — professor at Cornell, worked on improving efficiency, reasoning and long-context abilities of language models.

Alex Haugland — performance lead for Google Search and built trace search for APM at Datadog as a senior staff engineer.

Ryo Lu — founding designer at Notion.

Lukas Möller — built Cursor’s in-house high performance inference engine in 2 weeks, previously VP of engineering at DeepJudge.

Jack Gallagher — led the pretraining and RL of Midjourney v2–v7.

Michael Feldstein — built prototyping at Figma, founding work on the Figma AI team, and the social VR team at Meta.

If you are interested in building and designing transformational AI products, frontier RL, AI Infrastructure, or distributed systems, please reach out at hiring@cursor.com
