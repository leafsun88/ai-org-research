# Why Disney vs MidJourney Could Rewrite Hollywood's IP Playbook

## Rob Rosenberg 把 AI 版权争议拉回传统娱乐业规则：使用别人的内容要先谈许可

这期和 Rob Rosenberg 的对谈比一般法律新闻更偏产业视角。他的核心立场很直接：娱乐业长期的基本规则是，产品里用到音乐、视频、角色和受保护素材，就要授权；AI 平台过去靠“先用、后解释”的方式训练和商业化，现在正在被权利方要求回到许可逻辑。这个判断对 Midjourney 很重要，因为诉讼背后是 Hollywood 在争夺 AI 时代内容资产的定价权。Midjourney 如果要进入专业内容生产，最终绕不开 consent、credit、compensation 和 guardrails。

“Asking forgiveness instead of permission is not going to fly forever. In entertainment, if you use music or video in a product, you usually license it unless fair use clearly applies.” —— Rob Rosenberg, 2025-09-09

“AI platforms trained models on content, sometimes scraped and sometimes bought from places rights holders would challenge. Now they say starting over is impractical.” —— Rob Rosenberg, 2025-09-09

“The industry is trying to pull AI companies back into the same licensing logic that governs the rest of media.” —— Rob Rosenberg, 2025-09-09

## Disney 的目标不一定是杀死 Midjourney，而是把 fan creation 变成可授权市场

Rob 的有趣观点是，Disney 也可能从 AI fan creation 里赚钱。用户想生成 Star Wars、Marvel、Pixar 风格的短片和图像，这本身是一种强需求；如果被完全禁止，会损失活跃度和新型创作；如果被许可化，反而可能成为新业务。Midjourney 这类平台对 Disney 的威胁在于未经许可使用 IP，对 Disney 的机会在于把 IP 打包成受控、可分成、可审查的生成能力。这个方向会把生成式 AI 从盗版争议推向 licensed playground。

“Disney could turn this into a licensing program. Fans want to make shorts and images with superheroes or Star Wars, and that demand is real.” —— Rob Rosenberg, 2025-09-09

“The question is not only how to stop it. The question is how to permit it, price it, and keep the brand safe.” —— Rob Rosenberg, 2025-09-09

“A licensed fan-fiction environment could attract users and compensate rights holders, if the guardrails are strong enough.” —— Rob Rosenberg, 2025-09-09

## Guardrails 会成为 AI 图像平台的商业功能

Rob 反复提到，权利方最担心的不只是图像被生成，而是品牌被放进不可接受的语境。Disney 不会希望角色出现在暴力、色情、政治或品牌破坏性内容中。由此看，Midjourney 的商业化不只需要版权过滤，还需要品牌语境控制、授权范围控制、输出审计和企业级 policy。未来如果 Midjourney 与权利方达成合作，它要卖的不只是模型能力，还要卖“安全使用 IP 的系统”。这会显著改变产品形态和组织能力需求。

“Rights holders need guardrails so their IP is not used in unspeakable or brand-damaging ways.” —— Rob Rosenberg, 2025-09-09

“Permission is not enough if the platform cannot control context. A character can be licensed for one use and forbidden for another.” —— Rob Rosenberg, 2025-09-09

“For AI platforms, brand safety becomes a product feature. It is not just a legal memo after launch.” —— Rob Rosenberg, 2025-09-09

## Midjourney 的最佳路径可能是 deal-making，因为诉讼不确定性会拖慢专业采用

Rob 的产业判断偏务实：长期诉讼会制造不确定性，让企业客户和权利方都不敢大规模采用 AI 图像工作流；更好的路径是谈判、授权和规则化。这个判断对 Midjourney 的战略选择很关键。继续硬打 fair use 可以保护开放模型空间，但也会让商业客户承受不确定风险；主动与权利方达成 deal，会限制一部分自由，却能打开广告、影视、游戏和品牌资产市场。Midjourney 如果想做 professional creative infrastructure，deal-making 能力会变得和模型能力一样重要。

“The best path forward is to make a deal. Litigation uncertainty stalls business, and everyone needs to know what is allowed.” —— Rob Rosenberg, 2025-09-09

“AI companies want the freedom to build; studios want consent, compensation and brand control. A negotiated framework is where the money can move.” —— Rob Rosenberg, 2025-09-09

“If platforms and rights holders can price the use, creators get a clearer path and customers get less risk.” —— Rob Rosenberg, 2025-09-09

## 这期材料把 Midjourney 的长期天花板拆成两层：模型审美和内容权利网络

很多 Midjourney 分析只看模型审美、社区和订阅收入，这期材料补了第二层：内容权利网络。AI 图像平台要进入 Hollywood 和品牌世界，必须拿到或管理大量 IP 权利；谁能把模型能力和权利网络结合，谁就能把生成能力卖进更高价值场景。Midjourney 的风险是它没有 Disney 的 IP 库，也没有 Adobe 的 stock/creative cloud commercial safety；它的机会是模型审美和用户需求足够强，能迫使权利方坐上谈判桌。

“Creative AI has two assets: the model and the rights it can safely touch. The model makes the image; the rights determine whether the image can be used.” —— Rob Rosenberg, 2025-09-09

“Midjourney has user demand and visual quality. Studios have IP. The market will likely form where those two sides meet.” —— Rob Rosenberg, 2025-09-09

“If there is no licensing path, professional adoption stays cautious. If there is a path, the market becomes much larger.” —— Rob Rosenberg, 2025-09-09
