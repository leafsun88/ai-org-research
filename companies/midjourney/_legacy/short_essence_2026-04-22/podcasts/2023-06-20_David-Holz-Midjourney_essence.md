# David Holz (Midjourney)

## Midjourney 是小团队 AI 公司的反融资样本

这条材料最有价值的地方，是把 Midjourney 放在 OpenAI 式资本竞赛的反面：它有巨大用户影响力、订阅收入和 GPU 成本，却没有把组织做成融资、PR、policy 和大团队共同驱动的机器。Power Law 的叙事口径很清楚：Midjourney 在 2023 年已经像一个 juggernaut business，但产品仍然寄居在 Discord，创始人也没有 Sam Altman 那种公共曝光。这不是一个“低调公司”的性格描述，而是一种组织和资本结构：小团队、低 overhead、靠订阅收入喂 GPU，先把产品和模型跑到 PMF，再决定是否要平台化。

“Midjourney is this awkward product living inside Discord, without the media position of OpenAI, yet already a juggernaut business.” —— Power Law, 2023-06-20

“The company looks almost bootstrapped: a very small team, low overhead, and apparently very profitable.” —— Power Law, 2023-06-20

“They charge subscriptions for AI-generated images in Discord, with tiers around $10, $30, and $60 a month.” —— Power Law, 2023-06-20

“Revenue estimates are scattered, from tens of millions to as high as $300 million, so those numbers should be treated as rumors.” —— Power Law, 2023-06-20

## Leap Motion 的十年解释了 David 为什么不急着做平台

David Holz 的上一次创业 Leap Motion，是理解 Midjourney 的关键反面案例。Leap Motion 融了大量 VC、做硬件、做开发者生态、尝试成为交互平台，最后卡在用户教育、硬件集成和生态落地里。Midjourney 则几乎把顺序倒过来：先不做 API、不做 App Store、不急着搭平台，而是把一个核心产品打穿。这个背景能解释 Midjourney 的组织克制：David 已经见过“平台梦”如何在 killer app 之前吃掉公司，所以 Midjourney 宁愿先把图像生成体验做到极致。

“Leap Motion raised over $120 million, grew into a sizable organization, and still struggled to get the product into everyday use.” —— Power Law, 2023-06-20

“The company built an App Store before the core product had become a repeat-use product.” —— Power Law, 2023-06-20

“Midjourney has not rushed into apps or an API; David appears laser-focused on making the best images and the best product.” —— Power Law, 2023-06-20

“The lesson is product before platform: solve the thing people use again and again before grabbing platform value.” —— Power Law, 2023-06-20

## David 的长期母题是 interaction model

Leap Motion 和 Midjourney 表面上一个做手势识别、一个做文本生图，底层都在解决同一个问题：人的意图怎么进入机器，并被机器放大。Power Law 提到 David 很早就认为技术瓶颈不只是成本、速度或大小，而是 interaction model。这个点很重要，因为它把 Midjourney 从“画图模型”抬到“人机接口”层：prompt、Discord、社区模仿、按钮和 remix 都是新的输入系统，不只是图像生成的包装。

“David saw a bottleneck: the mind can think quickly, but getting intention into the computer is slow.” —— Power Law, 2023-06-20

“He believed one of technology’s biggest limits was the interaction model.” —— Power Law, 2023-06-20

“Leap Motion tried to solve that through hands; Midjourney solves it through language.” —— Power Law, 2023-06-20

“This is why the product is about user learning, not only image quality.” —— Power Law, 2023-06-20

## Discord 是 awkward interface，也是最早的学习飞轮

Midjourney 早期用 Discord，并不是因为 Discord 是最优雅的创作工具，而是因为它把用户学习、社区展示、prompt 传播和产品分发压进了同一个空间。图像生成最难的第一步，是用户不知道该想象什么；Discord 让用户看到别人怎么 prompt、怎么迭代、怎么 remix，学习成本被社区吸收。这是一种很像产品组织的选择：不先造完美前端，把资源集中在核心生成能力；同时让用户互相训练，降低教育成本。

“Discord gave them a scalable front end, mobile app, slash commands, bots, buttons, and image support out of the box.” —— Power Law, 2023-06-20

“People do not become creative alone in a blank box; together they play off each other.” —— Power Law, 2023-06-20

“A user can enter a room, watch others make images, copy prompts, and immediately start creating.” —— Power Law, 2023-06-20

“That community flow solved the old Alan Kay problem: think about how the user learns.” —— Power Law, 2023-06-20

## 小团队不是省钱动作，是 David 对 intellectual labor 的组织判断

材料里最组织化的一段，是 David 对 intellectual labor 的规模判断：知识工作的组织效率不是线性增长，1000 人不一定比 100 人强 10 倍。这个观点能解释 Midjourney 为什么愿意把成本规模放在 GPU，而不是人头上。Midjourney 并没有“小成本”运行，它的推理成本很重；真正被压缩的是组织复杂度。组织小，资源大，方向集中，这才是它和传统软件公司以及大模型 lab 的差异。

“David is focused on keeping the organization small.” —— Power Law, 2023-06-20

“He has argued that intellectual labor scales roughly logarithmically with headcount.” —— Power Law, 2023-06-20

“A thousand people may be only about twice as effective as a hundred.” —— Power Law, 2023-06-20

“Midjourney scales GPU cost, but keeps human coordination cost deliberately small.” —— Power Law, 2023-06-20

## GPU allocation 是 Midjourney 的隐形融资

Midjourney 没有大量融资，不代表它没有资源杠杆。材料里最像投资变量的是 GPU allocation：David 用自己在 Silicon Valley 的信用，和云厂商谈 GPU 供给，把稀缺资源锁住。AI 应用公司早期最稀缺的可能不是钱，而是推理供给、成本控制和能否在用户增长时不崩。Midjourney 的订阅收入能喂 GPU，GPU 又支撑更多生成，形成一个比“融资换增长”更干净的闭环。

“For Midjourney, the scarce input was not only money; it was GPU allocation.” —— Power Law, 2023-06-20

“David did not raise a big round; he used credibility to broker supply with cloud providers.” —— Power Law, 2023-06-20

“The team built infrastructure to use GPUs across geographies, rendering where capacity was available.” —— Power Law, 2023-06-20

“Because image generation already takes time, a few extra network fractions mattered less than GPU utilization.” —— Power Law, 2023-06-20

## Midjourney 的商业本质是第一批大规模 inference PMF

这条材料把 Midjourney 说成“first AI model with product-market fit”，这个判断值得保留。很多 AI 公司早期烧钱主要在训练，产品使用本身还没有大规模收入；Midjourney 的成本重心则很早进入 inference，因为用户真的在大量生成图像。这会带来一个很不同的商业结构：收入、GPU 利用率、用户体验和排队时间直接耦合。它的订阅不是 SaaS seat，而是围绕生成能力的消费税。

“Midjourney may be the first AI model with real product-market fit.” —— Power Law, 2023-06-20

“The GPU cost is not only training; it is the actual inference for customer image generation.” —— Power Law, 2023-06-20

“A user sends a prompt and expects an image back in roughly a minute.” —— Power Law, 2023-06-20

“Subscription revenue pays for the GPU usage that keeps the product scaling.” —— Power Law, 2023-06-20

## David 站在 scale camp，但他的产品路线没有变成纯技术崇拜

材料把 David 放在 AI 的 scale camp：更多 compute、更多 data、继续把已有方法放大。但 Midjourney 的成功不只是 scale；它同时抓住了用户学习、Discord 分发、prompt 社区和美学输出。这点很关键：Midjourney 的模型强，但它没有用 benchmark 叙事去卖产品。它更像把 scale 出来的能力包装成普通人能感知的创作体验。

“Around GPT-2, David saw the debate between scaling the existing techniques and doubting whether we understood intelligence at all.” —— Power Law, 2023-06-20

“He clearly falls closer to the camp of scaling the technology and making something people want.” —— Power Law, 2023-06-20

“For Midjourney, scaling only mattered because it became visible through better images.” —— Power Law, 2023-06-20

“The product translated model progress into user surprise.” —— Power Law, 2023-06-20

## David 对增长的警惕，是 Midjourney 内容治理的底层气质

Power Law 结尾引用 David 对 growth 和 optimization 的讨论：他不反对增长，但警惕为了优化而牺牲 humanity。这个观点能解释 Midjourney 的一些产品和治理选择：它在推动非常强的视觉技术，但创始人并不把“最大化 engagement / 最大化生成量”当成唯一目标。对一个图像模型公司来说，这种价值观不是软文化，而会影响 NSFW、deepfake、版权、艺术家关系和社区规则。

“David argued the bedrock disagreement is not growth itself, but optimization that sacrifices humanity.” —— Power Law, 2023-06-20

“He called some forms of optimization sociopathic, because they make products more profitable but less empowering.” —— Power Law, 2023-06-20

“He is building powerful AI while still asking what kind of human relationship with machines is worth preserving.” —— Power Law, 2023-06-20

“That matters for a company sitting directly inside image authenticity and copyright conflict.” —— Power Law, 2023-06-20
