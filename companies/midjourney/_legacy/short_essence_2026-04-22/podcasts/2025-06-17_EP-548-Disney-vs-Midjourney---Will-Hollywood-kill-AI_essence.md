# EP 548: Disney vs Midjourney - Will Hollywood kill AI?

## Disney 诉 Midjourney 的核心压力来自输出端直接撞上角色资产

这期法律讨论对诉讼主线抓得很准：很多 AI 版权案争的是训练阶段是否 fair use，但 Disney/Universal 对 Midjourney 的攻击点更直观，直接拿输出图像和角色形象对比。对 Midjourney 来说，这比一般文本训练案更危险，因为图像模型的商业价值恰恰来自可视化相似性，一旦输出高度接近米老鼠、Darth Vader、Minions 这类强 IP，法官和公众都更容易理解原告损害。这个案子会逼 Midjourney 在模型自由生成、版权过滤、品牌安全和用户责任之间重新划线。

“This one looks visually cut and dry. The evidence is hard to argue against when the output looks like the protected character.” —— EP548, 2025-06-17

“The complaint is not only about training data. It is also about users being able to generate characters that clearly resemble Disney and Universal properties.” —— EP548, 2025-06-17

“Images are different from abstract model weights. When the output looks like Darth Vader or a Minion, the legal and PR problem becomes much easier to see.” —— EP548, 2025-06-17

## Midjourney 已经不是实验室小工具，收入和用户规模让它必须承担平台责任

播客里提到 Midjourney 已经有数亿美元订阅收入、数千万用户和继续增长预期。这个事实会改变法律和商业语境：一家小实验室做 demo 可能被视为技术探索，数亿美元收入的平台则会被要求承担更清晰的版权治理责任。Disney/Universal 选择此时起诉，不只是为了赔偿，也是在 AI 图像平台商业化到足够大之后，争夺规则制定权。Midjourney 的下一阶段不能只依赖社区和订阅，它必须像平台一样处理 IP、过滤、申诉、授权和企业风险。

“Midjourney is reportedly around $300 million in subscription revenue, with projections much higher in 2025. This is not a basement startup anymore.” —— EP548, 2025-06-17

“Nearly 21 million users generated images in a recent month. At that scale, the output is no longer a niche experiment.” —— EP548, 2025-06-17

“Once a product is this large and this commercial, studios will expect platform-level responsibility, not just model-level excuses.” —— EP548, 2025-06-17

## 原告会抓住 Midjourney 已有安全过滤能力，追问为什么能过滤成人暴力却不过滤版权角色

一个很有杀伤力的诉讼点，是原告可以指出 Midjourney 已经能对成人、暴力和敏感内容做过滤，却没有同等强度地处理版权角色。这让“技术上做不到”变得更难成立。版权过滤当然比裸露或暴力更复杂，因为角色可能有变体、风格迁移和语义描述，但平台已经证明自己能做某些输出治理，法院可能会要求它解释为什么 IP 保护不在同等优先级。这会把 Midjourney 从 creative freedom 的叙事拉回平台治理叙事。

“The studios point out that Midjourney has filters for nudity and violence. Their question is simple: if you can filter that, why not copyrighted characters?” —— EP548, 2025-06-17

“Midjourney will say copyright is harder to define than safety categories. The plaintiffs will say difficulty is not a free pass when the business is built on these outputs.” —— EP548, 2025-06-17

“The case becomes about willful disregard if the company knew the issue and chose not to build guardrails.” —— EP548, 2025-06-17

## 用户风险开始上移，Midjourney 的 ToS 会影响专业采用

播客提醒 Midjourney 曾调整 ToS，让用户在版权问题上承担更多责任。这个点对专业 adoption 很关键：个人玩图可以接受模糊风险，品牌、广告公司、影视工作室和企业客户无法把核心视觉资产建立在不确定版权责任上。如果 Midjourney 想从 C 端订阅走向更大商业市场，它必须给专业用户更明确的 indemnity、版权保护、输入输出合规和授权路径。否则，Adobe Firefly 这类强调 commercial safety 的工具会在企业场景里拿走一部分需求。

“Midjourney changed the terms, and if copyright trouble happens, the user may carry more of that risk.” —— EP548, 2025-06-17

“That matters for professionals. A hobbyist can experiment; a brand cannot build a campaign on uncertain IP exposure.” —— EP548, 2025-06-17

“The business question is whether users trust the platform enough to use outputs commercially, not only whether the images look good.” —— EP548, 2025-06-17

## 如果 Disney 赢，最重后果会落在 injunction 和模型重训

播客把结果拆得很清楚：赔偿是一层，真正可能改变行业的是 injunction、过滤要求、模型清理或重训。如果法院要求 Midjourney 停止生成特定角色，或者要求对模型训练和输出做结构性调整，其他权利方会立刻跟进。这会把生成式图像平台带进一个更许可化的世界，Midjourney 可能需要从开放探索转向带 guardrails 的专业创作环境。这个案子会影响它的产品边界、成本结构、合作策略和长期估值。

“The studios are not only asking for damages. They want an injunction that could halt infringing outputs.” —— EP548, 2025-06-17

“If the studios win, model purges or retraining could be forced. If that precedent lands, other studios will follow.” —— EP548, 2025-06-17

“Midjourney will argue transformative fair use. But if the outputs keep looking like protected characters, the court may focus on the market effect and platform behavior.” —— EP548, 2025-06-17
