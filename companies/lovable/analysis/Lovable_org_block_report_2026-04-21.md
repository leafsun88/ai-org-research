# Lovable 组织报告：当更多人可以先把想法做成软件

## 读法

这份报告只讨论一个问题：Lovable 的组织为什么能支撑它的产品速度，以及这种组织会不会继续放大业务。商业模式、收入、竞争都会写到，但都放在组织主线下面。

Lovable 做的事可以讲得很简单：过去一个想法进入公司，需要先写需求、排工程、等设计、等开发、等测试。Lovable 把第一步提前了。产品经理、设计师、市场、运营、创始人，甚至完全不会写代码的人，都可以先做出一个能运行的版本，再拿这个版本去讨论、验证、交给工程团队接管。

这件事会改变客户公司的工作方式，也反过来要求 Lovable 自己变成一种很特殊的公司。它不能只靠工程团队快；增长、销售、产品、支持、员工个人表达，都要贴着产品跑。它也不能只靠 Anton 一个人快；公司变大后，必须把 founder 判断、用户反馈、产品质量和企业客户需求，变成可以持续运转的系统。

## 一、Founder 认知：Lovable 的核心用户，是那些过去没有软件生产权的人

### A01 — Anton 看到的机会，是把软件表达权交给更多人

Anton 最早的判断没有停在“让工程师更快写代码”。他看到的是另一件事：世界上有大量人有产品想法、行业经验、业务判断，但他们无法把这些东西变成软件。过去这些人只能写文档、画原型、找工程师排期。Lovable 想把这一步改掉，让这些人先自己做出一个可运行版本。这个判断决定了 Lovable 的产品不能长得太像 IDE，也决定了组织必须长期服务“普通人能不能完成一次真实 build”这件事。

“Only a tiny fraction of the world can code, and even fewer can build a great product. I decided: let’s build something for the 99%, not a productivity boost for developers. It has to be a completely new type of interface where you can bring your idea for a technical AI product into existence.” —— Anton Osika, Long Strange Trip, 2025-12-11

“After thinking about how many people are making engineers more productive with AI, I realized the long-term outcome is that anyone will be able to build software thanks to AI. Going from zero to one for so many more people is going to have much more impact. Many of the unique ideas, the best ideas, don't come from engineers.” —— Anton Osika, Spotlight On, 2025-07-17

### A02 — Lovable 的“简单”，本质上是公司替用户做技术判断

Anton 反复讲 simplicity，这个词不能理解成界面少几个按钮。Lovable 的用户很多并非专业开发者，如果让用户自己选快模型、慢模型、不同后端、不同代码路径，产品很快就会把技术门槛还给用户。所以 Lovable 的组织要替用户做一层判断：什么时候该快，什么时候该慢，什么默认方案最稳，哪些复杂选择应该藏起来。这要求产品团队有很强的判断力，也要求工程和产品能持续做实验；复杂性要被平台吸收，不能丢回用户手里。

“Should the user choose a fast response or a slow response? Should they choose between different AI models and capabilities? We fundamentally think it is on our side to choose what is best for users at large. Because we simplified that much, we can run many more experiments where we switch out the AI, make it faster or slower, and see what users prefer.” —— Anton Osika, Building One, 2026-03-10

### A03 — Depict 的教训让 Anton 对分心很敏感

Anton 在 Depict 的经历，给 Lovable 留下了一个很具体的组织教训：早期什么都试，可能有用；人多之后，过去随手答应的每一条线都会变成维护成本。Lovable 面对的是一个每天都有新模型、新竞品、新客户请求的市场，机会会一直涌来。Anton 现在总讲瓶颈和 focus，背后就是这个经验。Lovable 的速度能不能保住，很大程度取决于它能不能持续识别“这周真正限制公司的那件事”，然后把其他诱惑压下去。

“At Depict, we scaled super fast as well, and we did moving fast and scrappy really well. We did well on high-potential talent, junior talent with high potential; you can see that from the companies coming out of the Depict mafia. But what works in the beginning is to say yes to a lot of opportunities and try out what works. Once you become more people and you have to follow up and maintain everything that you start, you have to be much more focused.” —— Anton Osika, 20VC, 2025-03-05

“We said yes to too many things at Depict, and we didn't take this one thing that we could do ten times better than anyone else.” —— Anton Osika, 20VC, 2025-03-05

### A04 — AI 产品的规划周期被压到 24 小时

Lovable 的产品节奏很难用传统 roadmap 理解。Anton 说，研究团队或外部模型一旦出现新能力，产品团队最好在 24 小时内开始接入。重点不在于所有功能都一天做完，而在于组织要极快感知新能力，并且敢于马上试。AI 应用的用户会持续比较“哪个产品今天更强”，所以 Lovable 必须让团队保持小、快、能直接行动。太多审批和排期，会让产品错过能力刚出现时的窗口。

“For research initiatives, you do not really know what will come out, so you need to stay nimble. The moment something comes out internally or from another team, you have 24 hours to start integrating it into your product. That is the most important time to stay ahead.” —— Anton Osika, Long Strange Trip, 2025-12-11

## 二、招聘与人才：Lovable 要找能快速长大的人

### B01 — Anton 招人时更看成长斜率

Lovable 的招聘逻辑很清楚：它不只找履历最像的人，更看一个人能不能在高速变化中继续变强。Anton 讲 ambition、open-mindedness、slope，这些词背后是一个很现实的问题：AI 应用公司没有成熟说明书，很多旧软件公司的经验会带来慢流程和错误默认值。Lovable 需要的人，既要能自己判断，也要能快速放弃旧方法。一个人如果学习速度很快，就算经验没那么“标准”，也可能更适合这个阶段。

“I think experience can be a negative thing in some cases. You often want people who are super ambitious, who have a lot to prove, and who are more open-minded towards how you should work together in the team.” —— Anton Osika, 20VC, 2025-03-05

“I like to think a little about slope. If I talk to someone and I learn a lot of things from them, and I notice that my conversation is very dynamic and exciting, that is usually a very good indicator. It means they are going to adapt to the organization, and their slope will be very high.” —— Anton Osika, 20VC, 2025-08-18

### B02 — Senior 人才要进来，但作用是放大年轻人的判断

Anton 并不排斥 senior。更准确地说，他警惕的是那种把年轻人变成执行层、把公司拖回旧流程的 senior。Lovable 早期有很多年轻、高斜率的人，优势是冲得快，风险是经验不足。好的 senior layer 应该提供标准、反馈和判断，帮助年轻人更快长大，同时保住他们的 owner 感。这个配比很关键：如果 senior 太弱，公司会犯低级错；如果 senior 太重，公司会慢下来。

“Now I've met more people who share the traits I'm looking for, and they have impressive backgrounds. I'm more positive towards adding select very senior people who care a lot about culture and understand the benefit of a team where young individuals have a lot of influence and are not just told what to do.” —— Anton Osika, Spotlight On, 2025-07-17

“We have quite a bunch of very young people and also a lot of very senior people. That mix is very good, because if you have a tight feedback loop between young people and very senior people, the young people learn much faster.” —— Anton Osika, Spotlight On, 2025-07-17

### B03 — Stockholm 的真实优势，是低流失带来的组织记忆

Lovable 的欧洲优势不能写成情绪。Anton 自己也说，欧洲没有硅谷那种成熟网络，这是 hard mode。但 Stockholm 给 Lovable 一个很实际的好处：它更容易成为当地第一人才目的地，人才也没那么频繁跳槽。对这种产品速度很快的公司来说，低流失很重要。用户理解、产品 taste、AI 工具使用方式，这些东西都需要在团队里沉淀。如果人一直被更高 package 挖走，公司每隔几个月就要重建一遍默契。

“Europe is hard mode because the network around you is not as mature as Silicon Valley. But it has been helpful for us to be the number one talent destination in Stockholm. There is much more available talent that does not jump around, people are less spoiled, and they are yearning for a possibility to literally change the world.” —— Anton Osika, Long Strange Trip, 2025-12-11

“We are the biggest talent magnet in Stockholm right now, which is amazing. It's much more difficult to be that in San Francisco or New York. We can pick up all the underutilized talent and 10x their performance by being in a 10x better culture and ways of working, with amazing colleagues.” —— Anton Osika, 20VC, 2025-08-18

### B04 — Anton 和 Fabian 是一快一稳的搭配

Lovable 的创始人组合很有意思。Anton 更外向，愿意试新东西，也承担更多外部叙事；Fabian 更安静，更专注 build，更倾向于把事情简化之后再推进。这种搭配让 Lovable 不容易只剩一种气质。AI 公司如果只有冲动，会到处试新功能；如果只有谨慎，又会错过窗口。Anton 和 Fabian 的分工，把“开新局”和“收敛成产品”放在同一个 founding team 里。

“From working with Fabian, I knew he was extremely pragmatic, high urgency, focused on just building and doing that as fast as possible. I ended up on my bicycle outside where he lived, called him up, and said, Fabian, let's go on a walk.” —— Anton Osika, Spotlight On, 2025-07-17

“Fabian is quiet until he has really shaped an opinion; I'm on the other side, saying we should use this new crazy thing, and that polarity is productive.” —— Anton Osika, 20VC, 2025-08-18

## 三、工作方式：Lovable 要求员工自己也成为 builder

### C01 — “每个人都要能 ship”把岗位边界打开了

Elena 对 Lovable 员工的要求说得很重：每个人都要能把代码发到生产环境，每个人都应该用 Lovable 做自己的小产品，同时还要做好本职工作。这句话很硬，因为它直接改了岗位边界。Lovable 的产品承诺是“非工程人也能把想法做成软件”，那它自己的增长、人力、市场、产品团队也必须亲自经历这件事。否则员工讲不出用户真实的卡点，也很难判断产品哪里还不够简单。

“Every single employee at Lovable is expected to ship code to production. Every employee is expected to build their own satellite apps or products, even have a side gig running on Lovable. Every employee is expected to do their own marketing, post on social, build their brand, and talk about what they are building, while still doing their day job and specialty.” —— Elena Verna, 20Growth, 2026-03-14

### C02 — 工程师每周亲自跑完整用户流程

Lovable 的工程师不能只接 ticket。Anton 要他们每周坐下来，完整走一遍用户从输入想法到生成产品的流程，主动找哪里可以更顺、更简单、更令人惊喜。这是一个很具体的防熵增动作。AI builder 产品的复杂性很容易从后台漏到前台：错误提示、模型选择、权限、部署、数据库、支付，每一处都可能把非技术用户劝退。让工程师持续回到用户视角，是 Lovable 保持产品简单感的办法。

“Every week, engineers don't just take the planned tasks and do them. They sit down and go through the entire flow of using the product and think: is this actually the best way to do it? They try to polish, simplify, and make it as delightful as possible.” —— Anton Osika, Spotlight On, 2025-07-17

### C03 — “Not lovable”是一句内部质量暗号

Elena 讲到一个非常有用的细节：在 Lovable 内部，说一个东西 “not lovable”，大家会立刻跳起来修。它把品牌名变成了内部验收语言。AI 产品的问题很多时候不容易写成清单，有些是模型回答不好，有些是等待太久，有些是用户不知道下一步怎么做。用一句全员能理解的话，把这些体验问题快速升级，比写一大套质量流程更有效。

“We are trying to create absolutely lovable experiences. That is a mentality internally: if it is not lovable, we are not going to ship it. The best way to fix a bug at Lovable is to say, ‘this is not lovable,’ and everybody jumps on it. Sprints or no sprints, it is getting fixed right now.” —— Elena Verna, Lenny's Podcast, 2025-12-18

### C04 — Founder mode 需要保护层，否则 Anton 会被机会淹没

Anton 没有把公司规模化理解成自己继续管所有事。他承认自己最大的影响力仍来自 founder mode，但公司越大，投融资、客户、候选人、媒体、产品机会都会同时涌过来。Lovable 现在需要一层贴近他的 generalist，帮他筛掉噪音、排序机会、把反馈迅速传给相关团队。这层人不像传统中层，更像 founder 判断的放大器。Lovable 能否从“Anton 很快”变成“公司整体很快”，这里是关键。

“I'm going to always operate with most of my impact coming from founder mode. But given that there are so many things thrown at me from all different directions, I do need a protective layer that introduces order. How do we prioritize all these incoming things? The team is made up of previous founder-type generalists working closely with me, and I work with them through quick feedback: this is not what you should be doing, this is what you should be doing.” —— Anton Osika, 20VC, 2025-08-18

### C05 — Lovable 的 focus 机制连 founder 自己的想法也能压住

Mindaugas 讲过一个小故事：用户提了一个功能，他发到内部 crazy ideas；Anton 立刻说自己很早就想要这个功能，下一句又说这件事不排在优先级里，所以不做。这个片段很有含金量。很多公司所谓聚焦，只能压住普通员工的想法，压不住 founder 的灵感。Lovable 如果想保持速度，就必须让最有诱惑力的想法也接受同一套优先级判断。

“A user DM'd me an idea or feature request, and I posted it in crazy ideas. Anton responded very quickly. The first thing he wrote was: I have wanted this for such a long time. And instantly he followed up with: but it's not priority; we're not doing it. To me that showed: if the founders say, hey, I think we should have this, but it's not a priority, then we're not doing this.” —— Mindaugas Petrutis, AI Giants, 2025-09-11

### C06 — 每日发布是一种留存机制

Lovable 的 daily launch 不能只理解成“很勤奋”。AI 应用的用户迁移成本很低，竞品又多，用户很容易今天试一个、明天换一个。Lovable 需要让用户持续感到产品每天都在变强。Elena 说他们每天发布，制造持续存在感；每一两个月再用一次大版本讲一个更大的故事。发布节奏在这里同时承担产品迭代、市场传播和用户留存三件事。

“At Lovable, product launches are very different from any other company I have seen. We are committed to launching every day, so there is ongoing buzz: Lovable is evolving every single day. On top of that, every one to two months we make big T1 launches that bundle functionality around a story and a step-function change.” —— Elena Verna, Lenny's Podcast, 2025-12-18

“At Lovable, we are committed to launching every day. There is ongoing buzz: Lovable is evolving every single day, getting better every day. Every one to two months we make big T1 launches around a story and a step-function change. The constant noise is part of our retention strategy.” —— Elena Verna, 20Growth, 2026-03-14

## 四、增长组织：Lovable 的增长团队要贴着产品发明

### D01 — Elena 发现，老增长经验只能迁移一部分

Elena 说过去 15-20 年的增长经验，只有 30-40% 能用在 Lovable。这个判断很重要。传统增长通常是产品已经稳定后，去优化转化率、渠道、漏斗和留存。Lovable 所在的市场还在快速变形，用户不知道自己能做什么，产品边界也每周都在变。增长团队不能只站在产品外面调按钮，它必须参与发明新的使用场景、新的传播方式和新的留存理由。

“Only 30 to 40% of what I learned in the last 15 to 20 years of growth transfers here. Everybody is starting a vibe coding business, and to be ahead is not optimization of the problem, it is reinvention of the solution. In previous roles I might spend 5% innovating on growth; here I spend 95% innovating on growth and 5% optimizing.” —— Elena Verna, Lenny's Podcast, 2025-12-18

### D02 — Activation 被产品体验吃掉了

Elena 加入 Lovable 后发现，自己两个月几乎没碰 activation。原因很具体：Lovable 的 aha moment 就在用户和 AI 的第一次互动里，输入想法，看到东西被做出来。传统增长团队可以设计 onboarding、提醒、邮件和漏斗；Lovable 的增长必须进入核心产品体验本身。增长负责人如果不懂 agent 体验，只会做渠道和转化，很难真正拉动这种产品。

“I spent so much time writing that growth should be responsible for activation: setup moment, aha moment, first habit formation. Then I came to Lovable and realized I had not touched activation for two months. In this product, the core interaction is the AI magic itself, so activation is being built by the product team that owns the agent experience.” —— Elena Verna, The Product Podcast, 2025-11-26

### D03 — Founder 和员工的真人声音，是早期信任来源

Lovable 的传播并不靠一个公司账号发公告。Anton 早期亲自讲产品，后来团队成员、用户和社区一起扩散。AI 产品有不透明感，用户会担心工具靠不靠谱、会不会持续进化、背后的人是否真的听反馈。真人声音可以降低这种不确定性。对 Lovable 来说，员工会 build、会讲自己 build 的东西，也是一种增长资产。

“Word of mouth is the way to cut through the noise, and it comes back to brand being your product. The second piece is founder social. CEOs, cofounders, leadership teams, and employees need to be vocal about what they are building. Customers want to connect with the people behind the product; Anton being very active on social has been a gift that keeps on giving.” —— Elena Verna, The Product Podcast, 2025-11-26

“Lovable is where it is right now because of the founder-led socials Anton leaned into at the beginning. His brand is less important to current success because we diversified: users are active, community is strong, there is a lot of user-generated content, and other people on the team, including me, are pushing the brand as well.” —— Elena Verna, 20Growth, 2026-03-14

### D04 — 社区的价值，是让用户看到别人还能做什么

Elena 反对把社区当客服泄洪口。很多公司建社区，是因为 support 队列太长，结果社区里全是问题和抱怨。Lovable 需要的是另一种社区：用户展示自己做出的东西，互相学习，看到更多可能性。对 AI builder 产品来说，很多需求是被别人做出来之后才被看见的。社区承担的是想象力扩散，价值远大于省客服成本。

“The biggest mistake is treating community as a support outlet. Support cannot get through the queue, so companies create a community for users to help each other, and it becomes a dumping ground of negative sentiment. It is not filled with inspiration, positivity, or accomplishment; it is filled with problems.” —— Elena Verna, 20Growth, 2026-03-14

“Community is really important because you need to bring people together as they explore these capabilities and see what is possible. They can bounce off each other and help each other out. Community amplifies word of mouth, social posting, and retention. Ours started very early, runs on Discord, and has hundreds of thousands of members.” —— Elena Verna, Lenny's Podcast, 2025-12-18

## 五、企业化：补销售和安全，但要守住产品速度

### E01 — 企业客户买的，是工程介入前的验证速度

Lovable 在大公司里的第一层价值，放在工程介入之前。产品经理、设计师、市场、运营可以用 Lovable 把想法做出来、试出来、讲清楚，再拿给老板、用户或工程团队看。这样工程团队接到的材料，会从模糊 PRD 变成更接近可执行规格的东西。Anton 说这能节省工程介入前超过一半时间，这也是 Lovable 进入企业预算的真实路径。

“If I talk to large companies using Lovable, there is excitement for many different use cases: finance, operations, marketing moving very fast, and product, engineering, design. Even though you're not using Lovable to go all the way to production in most cases, you're saving more than half of the time spent, because you only loop in engineering resources with context on the existing system when you're fully certain of what you want to build and you've validated that this is actually the right thing.” —— Anton Osika, Sifted Podcast, 2025-09-03

“Product managers, designers and marketers can move much faster. Lovable is the best tool for them to prove out: here's my great idea. I'm not just going to talk about the idea; I'm going to build it in a few hours, show it to my manager, or try it with customers and say, let's launch this. Then you've saved 60% of the time, and engineers have this perfect specification to go implement in the system.” —— Anton Osika, This Week in Startups, 2025-09-19

### E02 — Enterprise sales 要补，但销售文化不能反过来改造公司

Anton 知道 Lovable 会有企业销售团队。他同时很清楚，这条路有风险。传统 enterprise sales 容易把公司带向大客户定制、长周期谈判、关系型销售和内部流程加厚。Lovable 需要销售能力，但销售应该服务产品扩散：理解客户怎么用、哪里卡、需要什么权限和治理，再把这些反馈带回产品。第一个 sales leader 很重要，因为他会复制出整支销售团队的工作方式。

“Will Lovable have an enterprise sales team? Yes. Will it become an enterprise company? It will not become an enterprise company, but it will have its enterprise sales team. I'm not so nervous about it. To me, it is just talking to and understanding your customers, and making sure they have the tools to get value from the product.” —— Anton Osika, 20VC, 2025-08-18

“For sales, you are hiring a great individual to create the sales playbook and help sell into enterprises. But the person you hire will also set the culture for the sales team. The people they hire will be similar in their approach to work, and I did not appreciate that until after the fact.” —— Anton Osika, Long Strange Trip, 2025-12-11

### E03 — GTM engineer 是一种更轻的企业化组织

Elena 提到 GTM engineer，基本说出了 Lovable 企业化的方向。传统销售组织会把 BDR、SDR、AE、客户成功、续约、RevOps 分成很多人和很多流程。AI 工具公司可以把这些链条压短：一个人既懂客户，也能用工具自动化流程，还能把问题反馈给产品。Lovable 如果能用这种角色服务企业客户，就能补上商业能力，同时不把组织变慢。

“I see GTM engineer materializing in two ways. One version replaces a lot of sales roles: BDR, SDR, AE, AM, success, all wrapped into one person who can prospect, have conversations, close, renew, expand, and handle adoption issues. The other version replaces RevOps by piping systems together so GTM can run faster.” —— Elena Verna, The Product Podcast, 2025-11-26

### E04 — Security 是 Lovable 从玩具走向真实业务的门票

Lovable 如果只生成 demo，安全问题不会立刻决定公司上限；如果它要承接企业内部工具、客户项目和真实业务，安全就会变成门票。Anton 说 security 是 top priority，并且 Lovable 已经上线扫描 AI 生成代码的安全功能。这件事很关键：非技术人能 build 之后，影子 IT、权限、数据和发布风险都会变大。Lovable 必须把安全做成平台能力，企业客户才敢继续往里放更重要的工作。

“We take security as the top priority, because if you're building something that is not secure, you should not have built it at all. In the 60s, sending people to the moon, you could have put a human in the cockpit steering the rocket ship, but you realized you need a computer to run that mission because it is much more reliable in a certain scope.” —— Anton Osika, This Week in Startups, 2025-09-19

“Over the last few months, we've launched several security features that scan all the clients and code that the AI has written for you. I cannot say it is more secure than a security expert today, so I recommend looping in a security expert for life-and-death applications or very sensitive data.” —— Anton Osika, This Week in Startups, 2025-09-19

## 六、业务结果与风险：速度已经验证，留下来还要继续验证

### F01 — ARR 很快，但收入质量要看持续使用

Lovable 的增长数字很强，但私有公司 ARR 要看口径。Anton 在 Sifted 里说，他们就是把 MRR 乘以 12；他也承认会有用户取消，只是新增订阅更多。这个说法并不削弱 Lovable 的爆发力，但提醒我们看两个指标：一是付费用户续不续，二是用户是否把更长期的产品经营交给 Lovable。如果用户只来生成一次 demo，收入质量会弱；如果用户持续 build、发布、增长和沟通，Lovable 才会变成长期工作流。

“We just take the monthly recurring revenue and multiply by twelve. Will a lot cancel? Some will cancel, but today there have just been more people subscribing. People want to try out AI tools a lot, and some are not ready to pay a monthly fee for the long term, but we're seeing who loves the product and we're seeing healthy churn numbers.” —— Anton Osika, Sifted Podcast, 2025-09-03

“I look at retention in two ways. Subscriber retention is how many paid subscribers we get, how many renew, and whether we can expand them. Engagement retention is also important because it is the leading indicator for what paid retention will look like. If you can show net dollar retention above 100%, that is a superpower for investors.” —— Elena Verna, Lenny's Podcast, 2025-12-18

### F02 — 付费用户毛利为正，说明模型成本暂时可控

Anton 说 paying users 单独看已经是 gross margin profitable，这个信息很有用。很多人担心 AI 应用公司收入越多、模型成本越高，最后毛利被吃掉。Lovable 现在至少证明，付费用户这边可以先跑通。但长期更重要的是收入结构能不能升级：早期用户为“生成和搭建”付钱，后面最好为“平台持续承载业务”付钱。这样模型调用才不会只是一次性成本，而会嵌进更高价值的订阅和平台服务里。

“We want to make it as cheap as possible to build and create things. Today we're in a very good place where we can very easily become profitable without big changes to how our business works. If you split up free users and paying users, we have gross margin profitability on the paying ones. That means we can acquire and grow users very rapidly without freaking out about our bank account.” —— Anton Osika, This Week in Startups, 2025-09-19

“As our business develops, we're looking to get most of our revenue once you, as a user, say: I love this platform, I'm never leaving. Today, in the beginning, you're mostly paying to build. Over time, we want to create so much value that you stay on the subscription, and only a small part of the cost goes to AI compute.” —— Anton Osika, 20VC, 2025-08-18

### F03 — OpenAI 和 Google 的威胁在入口和全栈产品

Lovable 很难靠某一个模型形成护城河。Anton 自己也说，它会用不同模型，Claude 常用于写代码，OpenAI 在用户体验和入口上更危险。Google 的威胁更大，因为它有模型、云、数据、Workspace 和集成。Lovable 要守住的东西，是用户习惯：当一个人想到“我要把想法变成软件”，第一反应会不会是打开 Lovable。模型会换，入口和工作流更难换。

“What we're betting on is to be the gateway for humans and to be the best user experience for AI. So far, OpenAI is doing that better than Anthropic, so I see them as a more serious competitor. We usually use Anthropic for code writing.” —— Anton Osika, 20VC, 2025-08-18

“Google, by far, is the one I worry about. If Gemini wins the model race, they have the entire stack: Google Cloud, data, integrations, Google Suite, and everything they need to build an incredible empire. I am hoping for a tough race, and right now it looks like one.” —— Maor Shlomo, 20VC, 2025-11-24

### F04 — Lovable 的上限，是从 idea-to-product 走到 idea-to-company

Lovable 现在最有想象力的地方，在于它不想停在“帮你生成一个 app”。Mindaugas 讲到 analytics、SEO、selling、marketing，Anton 讲到 2026 年成为 perfect cofounder。这条路如果成立，Lovable 会从开发工具变成早期公司和企业内部创新团队的操作系统。它承接的预算也会变大：工程、产品、增长、内部工具、创新项目和创业团队，都可能被纳入同一个工作流。

“Building the product is only part of the challenge. We recently launched analytics, so now it starts to tell you who's arriving at your product and who's visiting, and we're going to go deeper there. I'm pretty sure SEO is being worked on heavily. There will be other things that give you the end-to-end suite of features and tools, not just from idea to product, but from idea to company.” —— Mindaugas Petrutis, AI Giants, 2025-09-11

“At the end of 2026, Lovable is your perfect cofounder. You go to it with your idea from the idea stage, but also all the way up to growing your business once you have customers. It helps optimize the product for growth, optimize the product itself, and optimize your communication with customers through email or different marketing channels.” —— Anton Osika, 20VC, 2025-08-18

## 结论

Lovable 最重要的组织特点，是它自己也在按产品承诺工作。它要求用户成为 builder，所以也要求员工成为 builder；它让客户公司先用可运行产品讨论想法，所以内部也要求工程师、增长、销售、创始人保护层都围绕真实产品反馈运转。

这家公司最值得继续跟的，是几个组织动作能不能守住：招聘是否继续看成长斜率，senior 是否继续放大年轻人才，工程师是否继续贴着完整用户流程，增长团队是否继续在产品里发明新回路，企业销售是否补能力但不拖慢节奏，安全和治理是否能跟上真实业务。

如果这些动作继续成立，Lovable 有机会从 AI app builder 走向软件生产方式变化里的核心入口。如果这些动作被规模、企业客户和竞争压力冲散，它就会变成一款很好用但难以长期防守的生成工具。当前更值得押的，是前一种可能性：组织和业务本质还对得上，而且创始人已经意识到规模化本身会带来的反作用力。
