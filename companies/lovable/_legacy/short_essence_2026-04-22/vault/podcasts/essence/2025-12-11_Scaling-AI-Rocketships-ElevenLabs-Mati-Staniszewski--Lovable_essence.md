# Scaling AI Rocketships: ElevenLabs’ Mati Staniszewski & Lovable’s Anton Osika

## Anton 的原点是 99% 的 creator

这条 CEO-to-CEO 访谈里，Anton 把 Lovable 和 developer productivity 工具切得很开：他看到的是世界上只有很少人会写代码，更多人有想法但没有工程资本。Lovable 的界面选择、用户定位和组织优先级都从这里来。它没有先做“让工程师快一点”的 IDE，主线是一种新 interface，让普通人把技术型 AI 产品“grind into existence”。这能解释 Lovable 为什么一直强调 simplicity、why anyone can build、technical cofounder，也解释了为什么模型或 IDE power feature 没有成为全部叙事。

“Only a tiny fraction of the world can code, and even fewer can build a great product. I decided: let’s build something for the 99%, not a productivity boost for developers. It has to be a completely new type of interface where you can bring your idea for a technical AI product into existence.” —— Anton Osika, Long Strange Trip, 2025-12-11

## AI 产品规划不能等年度节奏，模型突破出来后 24 小时内要接进产品

Anton 和 Mati 都在讲 AI 公司里的 planning 被研究不确定性改写。最有价值的是“24 hours”这一句：一旦内部研究或外部模型出现能力突破，产品团队需要在一天内开始集成，否则就会失去边界上的体验优势。这个 cadence 不适合传统年度规划，也解释了 Lovable 的组织为什么要小队、少流程、高 owner。AI 应用公司的 product planning 更像 response system：有方向，但不把路线图当成确定世界。

“For research initiatives, you do not really know what will come out, so you need to stay nimble. The moment something comes out internally or from another team, you have 24 hours to start integrating it into your product. That is the most important time to stay ahead.” —— Anton Osika, Long Strange Trip, 2025-12-11

## Founder mode 的难点变成“既让 leader own，又不丢细节”

Anton 对 CEO 角色的描述很具体：公司进入 hypergrowth 后，他要从自己做事转向 empowering 和 unblocking leaders，但又不能完全脱离细节。这里是 Lovable 从早期 founder mode 走向规模化组织的核心张力。Lovable 的速度来自创始人近距离判断产品和用户，如果授权过度会丢掉 taste；如果创始人抓太细，组织会被 founder bandwidth 卡住。Anton 还在学习的正是这个 balance。

“I run the company in founder mode, but at this stage there is a big shift toward empowering and unblocking the leaders in the company. I held on too long to doing things myself rather than delegating. You want to give a lot of work to the leaders you hire, but still understand the details and remain in founder mode enough to jump in when needed.” —— Anton Osika, Long Strange Trip, 2025-12-11

## Stockholm 的价值是低流失和高饥饿感，Lovable 把地理劣势转成组织稳定性

Anton 承认欧洲的网络、投资人密度、enterprise playbook 都不如 Silicon Valley，但他也给了一个很有投资含义的反面优势：在 Stockholm，Lovable 更容易成为第一人才目的地，人才没有那么频繁跳槽，也更渴望参与一次能改变世界的机会。对 Lovable 这种需要长期积累产品 taste、AI workflow 和用户理解的公司，低流失本身就是组织复利。这里谈的是人才市场相对位势，欧洲叙事本身反而没那么重要。

“Europe is hard mode because the network around you is not as mature as Silicon Valley. But it has been helpful for us to be the number one talent destination in Stockholm. There is much more available talent that does not jump around, people are less spoiled, and they are yearning for a possibility to literally change the world.” —— Anton Osika, Long Strange Trip, 2025-12-11

## 第一个 sales leader 会复制销售文化，Lovable 补 enterprise muscle 时最怕招错人

Anton 对销售招聘的反思很细：招企业销售负责人不只是找一个能做 playbook 的 individual，这个人还会定义后续整个 sales team 的行为风格。Lovable 从 PLG 和 founder-led traction 走向 enterprise 时，这个点非常关键。销售组织一旦被传统大客户节奏带偏，可能吞掉产品速度；但如果销售文化和公司价值一致，enterprise motion 可以成为产品反馈和大单落地的加速器。

“For sales, you are hiring a great individual to create the sales playbook and help sell into enterprises. But the person you hire will also set the culture for the sales team. The people they hire will be similar in their approach to work, and I did not appreciate that until after the fact.” —— Anton Osika, Long Strange Trip, 2025-12-11
