# Lovable’s Anton Osika on churn, calculating ARR and his newfound celebrity status

## ARR 的算法很朴素，争议来自增长太快后 churn 还没充分显影

Sifted 直接追问 ARR 计算和 churn，这条很适合作为反方材料。Anton 说 Lovable 就是 MRR 乘 12，也承认会有用户取消，但当前新增订阅覆盖了取消。这里要保留的不是“ARR 是否真实”的简单争论，而是 AI 应用早期收入的验证窗口：用户试用和短期爆发很快，长期留存还需要时间沉淀。Lovable 的收入质量最终要看用户是不是把更重要、更长期的 workflow 放进平台。

“We just take the monthly recurring revenue and multiply by twelve. Will a lot cancel? Some will cancel, but today there have just been more people subscribing. People want to try out AI tools a lot, and some are not ready to pay a monthly fee for the long term, but we're seeing who loves the product and we're seeing healthy churn numbers.” —— Anton Osika, Sifted Podcast, 2025-09-03

## Anton 承认应该更早更快招人，但仍然警惕 headcount 过快稀释文化

这条访谈对组织 scaling 的说法很实在：Lovable 没有现成 playbook，因为很少有公司以这种速度增长收入；Anton 事后判断“应该更快 hiring”，但他的谨慎来自对文化稀释的担心。这里的矛盾是 Lovable 组织最核心的问题：AI 放大了小团队，但增长把 support、enterprise、security、product depth 都压上来，不补人会卡住，补太快会冲淡早期 operating style。

“You don't have a playbook to follow because so few companies have grown revenues as fast as we have. I think it's always about figuring out what the business needs. We should have hired faster. I'm cautious about scaling the number of people quickly, but in hindsight I think we could have moved faster on hiring.” —— Anton Osika, Sifted Podcast, 2025-09-03

## Lovable 的企业化不是直接替代工程，而是把工程前的探索时间砍掉一半以上

Anton 对 enterprise use case 的表述很清楚：大公司里 finance、operations、marketing、product、engineering、design 都在用 Lovable 更快探索；即使不直接进 production，也能在工程师介入前验证方向，减少工程组织 wasted time。这比“非技术人替代工程师”更准确。Lovable 在企业里的真实角色，是把模糊 idea 变成可运行 specification，让 engineering resource 被用在更确定的工作上。

“If I talk to large companies using Lovable, there is excitement for many different use cases: finance, operations, marketing moving very fast, and product, engineering, design. Even though you're not using Lovable to go all the way to production in most cases, you're saving more than half of the time spent, because you only loop in engineering resources with context on the existing system when you're fully certain of what you want to build and you've validated that this is actually the right thing.” —— Anton Osika, Sifted Podcast, 2025-09-03

“Large companies save weeks of work every week because so many people can move faster. Their workforce becomes more AI literate and has a greater intuition for what is possible with AI.” —— Anton Osika, Sifted Podcast, 2025-09-03

## 下一阶段的产品里，Lovable 想让公司把 engineering / product / design 跑在它上面

Anton 对 product milestone 的描述很大：未来整个公司可以在 Lovable 上运行 engineering、product、design，任何人发现 friction point 都能提出软件改动、流转提案、再进入生产。这里的关键词是 proposal flow。Lovable 不是要让每个人直接改 production，而是要把 idea -> proposal -> validation -> engineering handoff 变成企业系统。它卖的是新的产品组织工作方式。

“In the future, entire companies are going to run their engineering, product and design department on top of Lovable. Anyone in the company can find something that can be improved in the product, propose a change to the software, circulate that proposed change, and then it can go into production. Being able to have real complex businesses relying solely on Lovable for evolving most product features is a milestone we have in mind.” —— Anton Osika, Sifted Podcast, 2025-09-03

## 欧洲叙事对 Anton 来说是人才和野心问题，不是地理标签

Anton 在 Sifted 里把 Europe 讲得很硬：他想证明欧洲也能建全球 category leader，甚至 trillion-dollar company。这个叙事不是公关装饰，它能帮助 Lovable 召集一批想反证旧共识的人。AI 应用层早期拼人才和速度，founder 的公共叙事会变成招聘工具。Lovable 的欧洲身份如果能持续吸引 ambitious builders，就不只是 location，而是组织磁场。

“What I want to do is inspire European founders to try to become a global world leader in an interesting category. Europe has had this belief that all the large tech companies are built in the US. I don't think that has to be the case. It's about being thoughtful about what team you put together, how you work together, and being ambitious enough to operate at the global stage.” —— Anton Osika, Sifted Podcast, 2025-09-03

“I'm putting together a team where we are determined to prove that you can create a trillion-dollar company from Europe. That means we have to work pretty hard and be very focused on what we spend our time on.” —— Anton Osika, Sifted Podcast, 2025-09-03

## Founder celebrity 是双刃剑：它带来 partnership，也切走 founder 注意力

这条访谈开头问 Anton 变成欧洲 tech celebrity 的感受。他的回答有用：会议上被大量人拦住很烦，但有些合作伙伴已经知道 Lovable story，这对 partnership 有帮助。对 Lovable 这种 founder-led 公司，外部叙事是增长和招聘资产，也可能变成 founder bandwidth drain。后续 protective layer 和 founder-type generalists 的必要性，就能从这里看出来。

“At conferences it's fun and a bit frustrating how many people come up to me. It is useful that a lot of people I want to have a partnership with already know a lot about the Lovable story. That's fun. But a lot of investor attention is annoying, because I have to not answer all these nice people who want to talk with me.” —— Anton Osika, Sifted Podcast, 2025-09-03
