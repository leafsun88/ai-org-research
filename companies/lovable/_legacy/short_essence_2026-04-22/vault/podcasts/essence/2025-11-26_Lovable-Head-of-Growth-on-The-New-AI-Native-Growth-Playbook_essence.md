# Lovable Head of Growth on The New AI-Native Growth Playbook

## AI 应用的 PMF 会反复失效，Lovable 把 retention 放到增长模型中心

Elena 对 AI-native growth 的判断很有价值：AI 产品不同于传统 SaaS 的“找到 PMF 后开始规模化”线性过程，模型能力、用户期待和竞争供给每周都在变化，PMF 会不断被重新定义。Lovable 即使已经超过 100M ARR，她仍然说“不觉得已经稳了”。这句话比增长数字更重要，因为它解释了 Lovable 的组织为什么要保持高 cadence：产品体验、激活和留存都必须持续重新验证，不能把早期爆发当成稳定 moat。

“In AI, product-market fit is an elusive concept. Your place is not guaranteed, even if you are making hundreds of millions of dollars in revenue. We are well over $100 million in revenue, but I do not feel like we have it in the bag. The definition of product-market fit in the vibe coding category is changing every single week, and retention in our world of AI is going to make or break companies.” —— Elena Verna, The Product Podcast, 2025-11-26

## Activation 从 growth team 转移到 agent/product team，Lovable 的增长被产品核心体验吞进去

Elena 讲自己加入后两个月几乎没有碰 activation，这个反常点很关键。传统 PLG 公司里，growth team 通常负责 setup moment、aha moment、habit loop；Lovable 的 aha moment 发生在用户和 agent 的第一轮对话里，激活本身被产品和 AI 能力吸收了。换句话说，Lovable 的增长组织不能再靠外部漏斗修补产品，而要和 product/agent team 一起改核心交互。这也解释了为什么 AI 应用的增长 leader 要懂 product taste 和 model boundary。

“I spent so much time writing that growth should be responsible for activation: setup moment, aha moment, first habit formation. Then I came to Lovable and realized I had not touched activation for two months. In this product, the core interaction is the AI magic itself, so activation is being built by the product team that owns the agent experience.” —— Elena Verna, The Product Podcast, 2025-11-26

## GTM engineer 是 AI-native 公司把 sales、success 和 RevOps 压成 generalist 的雏形

Elena 对 GTM engineer 的拆解很实：这个角色吸收了销售、客户成功、续约、扩张、RevOps 自动化，杠杆高于一个新 title。Lovable 这类公司如果要维持高速度，不能把 GTM 切成 BDR、SDR、AE、AM、CS、RevOps 多段流水线；它需要懂客户、懂工具、会自动化、能闭环的 generalist。这个信号对 Lovable 企业化很重要：它会补 enterprise muscle，但如果能用 GTM engineer 压缩传统销售组织，仍有机会保留 AI-native 的组织速度。

“I see GTM engineer materializing in two ways. One version replaces a lot of sales roles: BDR, SDR, AE, AM, success, all wrapped into one person who can prospect, have conversations, close, renew, expand, and handle adoption issues. The other version replaces RevOps by piping systems together so GTM can run faster.” —— Elena Verna, The Product Podcast, 2025-11-26

## Founder social 和 employee voice 是 Lovable 早期 trust channel

Elena 把 founder social 放在 word of mouth 后面讲，说明 Lovable 的增长靠团队人格站到产品前面，公司账号发内容只占很小一部分。Anton 的个人社交影响力帮助用户看到“产品背后是谁在建”，降低了新工具的信任成本。这里和传统合规大公司形成反差：越是 AI-native、越快迭代的产品，越需要用真人叙事让用户相信团队会持续修、持续 ship、持续听反馈。

“Word of mouth is the way to cut through the noise, and it comes back to brand being your product. The second piece is founder social. CEOs, cofounders, leadership teams, and employees need to be vocal about what they are building. Customers want to connect with the people behind the product; Anton being very active on social has been a gift that keeps on giving.” —— Elena Verna, The Product Podcast, 2025-11-26

## Brand 被产品体验吃掉，Lovable 没有 brand team 反而更像 AI 公司

Elena 说 Lovable 没有 brand marketing team，也没做品牌 campaign，品牌主要来自产品里的 lovable moments。这个判断比“品牌重要”更具体：AI 产品里的 brand 已经超出视觉和 slogan，落在用户第一次成功生成、修复、发布时得到的情绪反馈上。Lovable 的名字本身把质量标准写进公司语言里，产品 bug 可以被一句“this is not lovable”立刻升级处理。品牌在内部先变成质量约束，再外溢成传播资产。

“People say Lovable has a strong brand, but we do not have brand marketing. We have not had brand campaigns or brand channels. Brand has become almost a product exercise. You need to stand out through product experience, and every interaction needs to feel lovable.” —— Elena Verna, The Product Podcast, 2025-11-26
