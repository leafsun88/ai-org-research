# 20Growth: Inside Lovable's Growth Machine

## Lovable 的 AI-native employee 要会 ship code、做 marketing，还保留自己的 specialty

Elena 在 20Growth 里把 Lovable 的组织要求讲得很锋利：每个员工都要能 ship code to production、做自己的 satellite app 或 side project、在社交平台 build in public，同时还要完成自己的专业岗位。这套要求比“人人都会一点 AI”更硬，岗位边界被 AI 放宽后，公司要求每个人把 execution、product sense、distribution 合在一起。Lovable 的人效和速度来自这种 T-shaped 变体：专业深度还在，通用产出能力被 AI 抬高。

“Every single employee at Lovable is expected to ship code to production. Every employee is expected to build their own satellite apps or products, even have a side gig running on Lovable. Every employee is expected to do their own marketing, post on social, build their brand, and talk about what they are building, while still doing their day job and specialty.” —— Elena Verna, 20Growth, 2026-03-14

## Founder-led social 先点火，Lovable 再把信任扩散成 employee-led social

Elena 明确说 Lovable 早期爆发很大程度来自 Anton 的 founder-led social，但后续不能继续只靠 founder 一个人。Lovable 现在把用户内容、community、团队成员个人声音一起变成品牌资产。这个机制对 AI 公司尤其重要：用户面对黑箱模型和快速变化工具，需要知道谁在背后 build。公司用 employee voice 替代公司账号，可以更快建立信任，也更容易把产品迭代变成市场叙事。

“Lovable is where it is right now because of the founder-led socials Anton leaned into at the beginning. His brand is less important to current success because we diversified: users are active, community is strong, there is a lot of user-generated content, and other people on the team, including me, are pushing the brand as well.” —— Elena Verna, 20Growth, 2026-03-14

## 每天 launch 是 retention 策略，AI 应用需要让用户持续感觉产品在变强

Elena 对 launch cadence 的拆解和 12-18 访谈互相印证，但这里更明确地把 daily launch 和 retention 连起来。Lovable 每天都有变化，让用户不断感到“明天再回来会更好”。这个节奏在 AI builder 赛道很重要，因为用户切换成本低、竞品多、模型能力迭代快。持续 launch 既拉新话题，也让已有用户维持期待。

“At Lovable, we are committed to launching every day. There is ongoing buzz: Lovable is evolving every single day, getting better every day. Every one to two months we make big T1 launches around a story and a step-function change. The constant noise is part of our retention strategy.” —— Elena Verna, 20Growth, 2026-03-14

## Freemium 被 Elena 当成 owned marketing channel

Elena 对免费用户的看法很关键：Lovable 的成本很大一部分来自 freemium，但她把免费用户看成营销渠道，因为被 delight 的免费用户会发社交、推荐朋友、带来口碑。她还提到 Lovable score，用来衡量用户 referral。这个视角可以解释为什么 Lovable 愿意承受推理成本和免费使用：免费用户不能简单视为漏斗顶端噪音，他们是产品体验驱动的 earned distribution。

“At Lovable, a lot of our costs are in freemium. We view freemium as a marketing channel. When free users get delighted by what they accomplish, they do marketing on our behalf: they talk on socials and refer friends. We measure something called Lovable score, how often people actually refer us to somebody else.” —— Elena Verna, 20Growth, 2026-03-14

## 社区如果只承接 support，会把负面情绪 SEO 化；Lovable 要的是 inspiration 和 accomplishment

Elena 对 community 的反面案例很有洞察：很多公司把社区当 support queue 的泄洪口，用户进来只会抱怨问题，最后社区变成负面情绪的索引页面。Lovable 需要的社区功能不同，它要让用户互相激发、展示成果、帮助更多人看到“原来这个也能做”。这个差异决定 community team 的工作重心在正向创作氛围和作品传播，而非客服替代。

“The biggest mistake is treating community as a support outlet. Support cannot get through the queue, so companies create a community for users to help each other, and it becomes a dumping ground of negative sentiment. It is not filled with inspiration, positivity, or accomplishment; it is filled with problems.” —— Elena Verna, 20Growth, 2026-03-14

## Paid marketing 是最贵的路，Lovable 更看重无法被购买的 demand engine

Elena 对 paid marketing 的态度很硬：如果还不知道 LTV、activation、conversion 和 retention，就靠 paid growth 会变成最贵且不可防守的路径。Lovable 的 growth philosophy 更偏 owned/earned：产品惊喜、freemium referral、founder/employee social、community、launch cadence。这个组合比广告慢一些，但更难被复制，也更符合 AI 应用高流动、低锁定的市场。

“Paid marketing is the most expensive way out, and it is not competitively defensible or sustainable. You are paying somebody else to generate demand for you. If you have not figured out your unique, defensible, sustainable way to generate demand without relying on another platform, you are buying time before the collapse is imminent.” —— Elena Verna, 20Growth, 2026-03-14
