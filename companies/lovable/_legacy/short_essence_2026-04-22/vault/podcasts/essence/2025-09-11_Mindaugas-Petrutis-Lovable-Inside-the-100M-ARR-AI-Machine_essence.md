# Mindaugas Petrutis: Inside the $100M ARR AI Machine

## Lovable 招进来的 growth 人，本身就是 super user，这让 GTM 和产品反馈天然贴得很近

Mindaugas 的身份很有代表性：他不是传统 SaaS growth hire，而是 Lovable 的 super user，先用产品把自己的 idea 做出来，再进入公司做 growth/marketing。这种路径说明 Lovable 的 GTM 很大一部分来自用户社区内部，早期增长人能直接理解产品魔法、用户障碍和使用场景。对 AI 应用公司，这类“user turned operator”很值钱，因为他们不是只会拉渠道，而是知道产品在哪个瞬间让人从怀疑变成信任。

“For context, I was a super user already before joining Lovable. I was obsessed with the product I was building with it. I'm not technical; I picked it up because I had a product idea last year with a small team, but then found myself without an engineer. I started testing all these tools thinking maybe I can become my own engineer and rebuild this product myself.” —— Mindaugas Petrutis, AI Giants, 2025-09-11

“I set myself a goal: 30 minutes every day to just make something. Honestly, nothing anybody needed; it was more to test the capabilities, the limitations, and see what was possible. It took me down this really cool path of using it for play and curiosity, and then for real things.” —— Mindaugas Petrutis, AI Giants, 2025-09-11

## Mindaugas 的四天付费客户 case，是 Lovable 最强的 adoption 叙事

Mindaugas 讲了一个很具体的 micro-case：周日晚上想到一个自己熟悉的问题，四天后拿到第一个付费客户，第二天又自己接 Stripe。这个故事的价值不在“又一个 vibe coding 成功案例”，而在它把传统产品团队里的多个环节压缩了：工程排期、支付集成、上线验证、收费试错。Lovable 的增长来自这种体验被用户反复讲给别人听，比广告更像 product-led proof。

“I thought, before I try to rebuild my bigger product idea, let me solve a problem I know deeply. In that scenario, I went from idea on a Sunday night to first paying customer four days later on a Thursday. I just took a step back and thought: this is wild. This was not possible before, and I'm not technical.” —— Mindaugas Petrutis, AI Giants, 2025-09-11

“More people wanted access to it, so the next day I built the Stripe integration so people could pay for it while drinking coffee over breakfast. I've worked in teams where building the Stripe integration required engineering time. I just did it myself and it worked.” —— Mindaugas Petrutis, AI Giants, 2025-09-11

## Lovable 的 focus discipline 很硬：founder 想要的功能也可以被当场拒绝

Mindaugas 提到一个内部 Slack / idea 例子：用户提出 feature request，他发到 crazy ideas，Anton 立刻回复自己很久以来也想要，但不优先，不做。这个片段非常有组织含量。它说明 Lovable 的 focus 不是嘴上说说，而是能压住 founder 自己喜欢的 idea。AI 应用公司每天都会冒出大量“看起来很棒”的功能，如果 founder 不能亲自示范 no，组织很快会被机会淹没。

“A user DM'd me an idea or feature request, and I posted it in crazy ideas. Anton responded very quickly. The first thing he wrote was: I have wanted this for such a long time. And instantly he followed up with: but it's not priority; we're not doing it. To me that showed: if the founders say, hey, I think we should have this, but it's not a priority, then we're not doing this. There is clearly a well-thought-out focus and priorities, and we need to stick to those.” —— Mindaugas Petrutis, AI Giants, 2025-09-11

## Voice mode 对 Lovable 不是小功能，而是降低 prompt literacy 门槛

Mindaugas 对 voice mode 的兴奋点很具体：很多人不知道什么是 prompt，也不擅长把背景写清楚；语音让用户直接 brain dump，把用户、竞品、想法、约束说出来，再交给 Lovable。这个功能对 Lovable 的 TAM 很关键，因为它把“会不会 prompt”这个新门槛继续往下压。AI 应用要走向大众，不只是模型更强，还要不断降低人把真实意图输入系统的成本。

“I've been wanting and waiting for voice mode. My workflow with AI products is that I get better results if I just speak what's in my mind versus trying to type it out. AI works best when you give it lots of context. My workflow used to be: open Granola, speak and brain-dump as much context as possible, here's the idea, here's what I know about the users, here's what other solutions exist, then refine it and pop it into Lovable. Now I can just speak directly and I'm off to the races much faster.” —— Mindaugas Petrutis, AI Giants, 2025-09-11

“There are hundreds of millions, billions of people that don't know what a prompt is or haven't used it in different contexts. Voice removes that or bridges that gap. When you arrive at Lovable, you don't need to think, what is a prompt? You can just speak it, get better results faster, see the utility of the product and stick around.” —— Mindaugas Petrutis, AI Giants, 2025-09-11

## Lovable 的产品扩张方向是 idea-to-company，而不是 idea-to-product

Mindaugas 在访谈里把 Lovable 的后续功能讲成 idea-to-company：analytics、SEO、selling、marketing、incorporation、user feedback、feature iteration 都会进入同一条链路。这个视角延续 Anton 的“cofounder”叙事，也能解释增长和企业方向：用户建完产品后，真正的工作才开始。Lovable 如果只停在 product creation，会被同类工具挤压；如果能承接公司生命周期，迁移成本和付费深度都会提高。

“Building the product is only part of the challenge. We recently launched analytics, so now it starts to tell you who's arriving at your product and who's visiting, and we're going to go deeper there. I'm pretty sure SEO is being worked on heavily. There will be other things that give you the end-to-end suite of features and tools, not just from idea to product, but from idea to company. You need to know how to sell, market, incorporate, iterate on features, talk to users, and get feedback.” —— Mindaugas Petrutis, AI Giants, 2025-09-11

## Lovable 在企业里的早期角色，是让已有团队更快 launch 新产品和新 feature

Mindaugas 对 enterprise 的回答有边界感：Lovable 目前不一定深入 legacy code，但已有产品团队、工程团队和企业客户正在用它推出新 feature、新 product，甚至给现有客户试。这里可以看到 Lovable 的现实落点：不是替代大型企业主系统，而是进入新功能探索和新业务孵化。这个位置如果做深，会成为企业内部 idea funnel 的前端。

“Companies with existing products, teams and engineers are using Lovable to launch new features and new products to their existing customer base on top of the company, business and product they already have. How we get into legacy software, I don't know personally. We have to stay close to customers and understand what people are looking for as we embed ourselves deeper into larger companies.” —— Mindaugas Petrutis, AI Giants, 2025-09-11

“Initially the use case was people launching new businesses, giving opportunity to those blocked by technical skills or access to technical resources but with great ideas. Now we have large enterprise clients and companies using us, and different requests and use cases will stem from that.” —— Mindaugas Petrutis, AI Giants, 2025-09-11

## Lovable 的社会叙事是把被替代的非技术人群重新变成 builder

Mindaugas 的一个观点很值得进入报告：如果 AI 替代很多非技术岗位，Lovable 至少给这些人新的生产工具，让他们可以做 side project、创业、创造收入或新工作。这个叙事是 Lovable brand 的重要部分：它不是“工程师效率工具”，而是“让非技术人拥有构建能力”。这会增强用户情感认同，也会帮助 Lovable 吸引 community 和教育内容。

“Why I was excited to join was to be part of the solution, not the problem. If foundational LLMs or products built on top of them start replacing humans and jobs, those humans will need something to do. The people being replaced are often the non-technical ones. We're giving them something: maybe a side project that generates income, maybe a company they couldn't start before that creates jobs for others.” —— Mindaugas Petrutis, AI Giants, 2025-09-11

“I'm discovering millions and millions of untapped, unsolved problems that larger companies are not going to solve. When artists, musicians, DJs, producers, talent managers who are deep in the problems but cannot solve them are given the ability to do so, they build products for their industry.” —— Mindaugas Petrutis, AI Giants, 2025-09-11

## European company 的气质在 Mindaugas 看来体现为少炫耀、重产品、低浪费

Mindaugas 对 Lovable 欧洲属性的描述不是宏大叙事，而是一个具体场景：$200M 融资发布后，Stockholm 的庆祝很朴素，更多能量放在产品和工作上。这种观察和 Anton 的“talent and culture”呼应：Lovable 想把欧洲低 ego、长期主义和工程认真转成竞争力。对高速 AI 公司来说，这种气质如果能保留下来，会帮助它避免硅谷式高流动和过度表演。

“When we announced the $200 million fundraise, that's a lot of money. I know companies on the other side of the world that would have spent half of that on the announcement and party. What I loved when I was in Stockholm for a few days was that there was work behind the scenes to blow it up, but the party still felt simple. It could have been a cool person's party, not a massive corporate show.” —— Mindaugas Petrutis, AI Giants, 2025-09-11
