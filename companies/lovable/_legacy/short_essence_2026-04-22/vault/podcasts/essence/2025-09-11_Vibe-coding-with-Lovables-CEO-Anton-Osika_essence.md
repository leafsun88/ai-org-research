# Vibe coding with Lovable's CEO Anton Osika

## Lovable 这个名字本身代表 Anton 对软件的审美判断：产品要制造情绪反应

Anton 在 Times 访谈里解释公司名字，很值得留：他不满足于软件只是“满足需求”，而是认为最好的软件会让用户产生喜欢、惊喜、情绪反应。这个判断连接了 Lovable 的产品设计和增长机制：如果用户第一次用就 mind blown，他们会自然传播；如果软件只是功能完成，增长会更像传统 SaaS。Lovable 的 brand 从第一天就不是“生成代码”，而是“让人觉得自己能创造漂亮产品”。

“Software to too many people has been something that satisfies a set of requirements. The best software products are something that you try and you like; you feel emotions. That's why we call our company Lovable. Our users should be able to create software products that create that positive emotional response.” —— Anton Osika, The Times Tech Podcast, 2025-09-11

## $100M revenue 的解释是 product-led word of mouth，而不是先融钱再买收入

Anton 对“AI bubble”的回应很有投资意义：Lovable 先有 self-serve revenue growth，再拿高估值，而不是基于未来预期融资。他把 $100M revenue after eight months 归因于用户体验后自发传播。这不能消除留存和成本问题，但能把 Lovable 和那些靠大单预期、AI 概念融资的公司区分开。它的早期增长至少来自真实试用和付费行为。

“When people try the product, they have their minds blown and they start telling others that you should try Lovable if you want to build a software product. We've been growing on that word of mouth. In July, we were named the fastest-growing startup ever, hitting $100 million in revenue after eight months. It's the empowerment that has been fueling our growth.” —— Anton Osika, The Times Tech Podcast, 2025-09-11

“A bubble is often when you invest in something that might materialize in the future. Lovable's growth is purely self-serve: you come, use the tool for free, and if you see that it is helpful and will create value for you, then you pay. We led with revenue growth, and then we got the high valuation from that.” —— Anton Osika, The Times Tech Podcast, 2025-09-11

## Lovable 的边界讲得很清楚：个人和简单 CRM 可以端到端，复杂业务仍要技术人把关

Anton 在 Times 里对非技术用户能力边界说得很实：简单网站、个人/小商业产品、简单 CRM 可以 100% 端到端；复杂业务、Salesforce 级别系统或安全敏感场景，还需要 technical person。这个边界感很重要，因为它让 Lovable 的 enterprise positioning 更可信。它不是把所有工程判断都交给非技术人，而是逐步扩大 AI 能可靠完成的 scope，同时把安全扫描和 human review 放在关键位置。

“If you take a personal website or business website with stunning pictures, that takes you one prompt, then one click and put it on any custom domain. There is no last 5%. If someone wants to build an entire software product on Lovable, to be 100% sure there is no security vulnerability, I recommend having a technical person if you're completely non-technical.” —— Anton Osika, The Times Tech Podcast, 2025-09-11

“For simple applications, you can build them 100% end to end. With recent product updates, we've added a lot of security scanning features. You cannot build Salesforce as a non-technical person, but you can build a simple CRM, a place where you track your customers and what to do next with them, without writing a single line of code.” —— Anton Osika, The Times Tech Podcast, 2025-09-11

## 企业里的最快 use case 是 demo, not memo，把资源决策从 PPT 改成可运行原型

Anton 在 Times 里把 Lovable 的企业价值讲得非常具体：很多大公司领导不想再看 presentation，而是希望员工 demo idea。Lovable 让 product managers、designers、marketers 在工程资源投入前先做 working prototype，让团队和客户真实感受方向。这里是 Lovable 进入企业的核心组织价值：压缩需求传话链，把 PRD / slide 变成可点击产品。

“Lovable fits in as a communication tool. Many large companies, product leaders at Google, Sebastian at Klarna, are clear to their staff: I don't want to keep seeing presentations to communicate ideas; I much prefer you to demo. Large companies ask staff to propose what to do next in their product or internal processes by building a working prototype that could be put in production, so they can try it and feel whether this is where they should go before committing engineering resources.” —— Anton Osika, The Times Tech Podcast, 2025-09-11

## Stockholm 对 Lovable 的价值是新一代 AI product company 的 talent magnet

Anton 被问到伦敦是否会吸引 Lovable 迁移时，回答里保留了对 Stockholm 的判断：Stockholm 可能正在追上甚至超过伦敦成为 unicorn minting city，有 super ambitious talent，也有“Silicon Valhalla”的势能。这不是地缘政治，而是招聘和心智竞争。Lovable 的欧洲叙事越强，它越能吸引那些想在欧洲建全球公司的工程师、产品人和 founder-type generalists。

“I'm here in London to meet and calibrate what type of talent exists here and bring them onto the team to build the last piece of software with us. Number 10 made a compelling pitch on why we should build Lovable mainly in the UK. But Stockholm is catching up, and might overtake London as the place where the most unicorns get minted. There is a lot of super ambitious talent in Stockholm and moving to Stockholm. Silicon Valhalla has a good future.” —— Anton Osika, The Times Tech Podcast, 2025-09-11

## Anton 认为欧洲这一轮机会在于把 AI 翻译成有价值的产品

Anton 对欧洲 comeback 的判断很具体：不是欧洲要赢模型训练，而是欧洲公司可以擅长把 AI 转译成 real products that empower humans。Lovable 在这条链上代表的是 AI application layer 的样本。这个判断也帮助分析 Lovable 为什么把 user experience / interface 放在模型之上：模型能力全球扩散后，谁更会把它产品化，谁能捕获下一层价值。

“I think this is the decade where Europe can make a comeback and be very good at the translation from AI to creating products that create a lot of value. What we want to do at Lovable is change how software is created and who gets the chance to do that.” —— Anton Osika, The Times Tech Podcast, 2025-09-11

## 主流媒体主持人的体验反馈说明 Lovable 已经越过早期 AI 圈，进入普通知识工作者

Times 主持人用 Lovable 做了一个视频游戏，虽然质量离 Super Mario 很远，但这个体验证明 Lovable 已经进入非技术媒体人的日常试验。对 Lovable 来说，这类 low-stakes creation 很重要：它不一定马上贡献 enterprise revenue，却在培养“我也能 build”的心理路径。AI 应用扩散往往先从个人玩具和 side project 开始，再进入工作流程。

“I am not technical, even though I write about tech. I signed up for Lovable and created a video game called Meatball Mania. The quality of the graphics needs work, but the idea that my soon-to-be nine-year-old could probably create his own video game by asking a machine to do it is living in the future.” —— The Times Tech Podcast, 2025-09-11
