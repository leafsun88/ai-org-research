# 20VC: Lovable on Hitting $17.5M in ARR in 3 Months

## Depict 给 Anton 的组织教训，是早期可以广撒网，规模起来后必须收窄到一个十倍点

Anton 对上一家公司 Depict 的复盘很有组织含量：早期多试机会有价值，因为你还不知道哪条线会跑出来；一旦团队变大，所有被打开的机会都需要维护，组织就会被自己制造的 option 拖住。Lovable 早期的极简产品和高速度，背后有这条反向经验：不要让一个小团队同时维护过多承诺，先找到自己能比别人强十倍的点，再把所有节奏压到这个点上。这个判断比“focus 很重要”更具体，它来自一个曾经因为说 yes 太多而失速的 founder。

“At Depict, we scaled super fast as well, and we did moving fast and scrappy really well. We did well on high-potential talent, junior talent with high potential; you can see that from the companies coming out of the Depict mafia. But what works in the beginning is to say yes to a lot of opportunities and try out what works. Once you become more people and you have to follow up and maintain everything that you start, you have to be much more focused. We said yes to too many things at Depict, and we didn't take this one thing that we could do ten times better than anyone else.” —— Anton Osika, 20VC, 2025-03-05

## Lovable 的招聘底色是高斜率和可塑性，经验有时反而会带来旧工作方式

Anton 对 talent 的排序很直接：高潜、ambition、open-mindedness，比“曾经做过同样岗位”更重要。这里不是反经验主义，工程、安全、coach 型角色仍然需要 domain experience；但 Lovable 这种 AI 应用公司最怕把旧软件组织的节奏搬进来，所以他会主动寻找还没有被大公司流程塑形、愿意证明自己的年轻人。这个人才观和 Lovable 的业务吻合：模型能力每月变化，产品边界还没定型，最稀缺的是快速学习和重新组织工作的能力。

“I think experience can be a negative thing in some cases. You often want people who are super ambitious, who have a lot to prove, and who are more open-minded towards how you should work together in the team. For many roles, junior talent is super easy to get into the company. The best people you can hire at a young age would go home and become founders, and then you can't hire them anymore.” —— Anton Osika, 20VC, 2025-03-05

“For most cases, yes, I would hire someone who hasn't done what I'm asking them to do before. If it's engineering, you have to know software engineering, of course. For some roles you definitely want someone with a lot of experience who can coach and tell the more junior people what great looks like.” —— Anton Osika, 20VC, 2025-03-05

## GPT Engineer 的价值不在开源声量，而在 Anton 很早就把 LLM 放进 agent loop

Anton 对 GPT Engineer 的描述能解释 Lovable 为什么不是一个简单 prompt wrapper。他在 ChatGPT API 很早期就用“LLM in a for loop”的方式验证 agentic coding：让模型生成文件、运行代码、交付一个 snake game。这条线很重要，因为 Lovable 不是先看到市场热度再做 app builder，而是 founder 先通过一个周末的实验形成了对“模型可以接管软件工程多步骤任务”的体感。后面产品从 terminal 工具走向 browser，是同一个能力往更大用户群迁移。

“I had been playing with the precursors of ChatGPT as well, and a year before then I felt there was a massive wave coming from scaling up these models. I started writing: you can hook them up, put the large language model in a for loop, and have it do a lot of agentic things. When I came back to Sweden, it was obvious where to apply this: software engineering.” —— Anton Osika, 20VC, 2025-03-05

“I felt no one was sufficiently imaginative about what I was thinking, so I had to prove a point: with the current first versions of the ChatGPT APIs, you could build an agent that writes code. I drank a lot of coffee and crammed away. You write, create a snake game, and then you get a running snake game on your computer. That was the first version of what we're building now.” —— Anton Osika, 20VC, 2025-03-05

## Waitlist 被 Lovable 用成了用户研究阀门，而不是制造饥饿营销的工具

Anton 对 waitlist 的讲法很实用：它的功能是控制进人速度、筛选谁值得访谈、验证最有价值的 ICP。Lovable 早期不是把所有流量直接灌进产品，而是用 waitlist 做可控学习，把用户访谈拆成两种：一种观察用户怎么实际使用产品，另一种追问用户为什么需要这个产品、真实 pain point 是什么。这套方法让 Lovable 在爆发前先积累了对 aha moment 和 use case 的判断。

“Waitlists are useful because you can control exactly how many people you want to get on board and take user interviews with. Get sufficiently many people on the waitlist, find a good way to qualify who you want to talk to. You probably have a hypothesis on who you should sell into and who has the most value from your product; qualify those and talk to them.” —— Anton Osika, 20VC, 2025-03-05

“There are two types of user interviews. One is where we just see them use the product and see where they understand the product; that is more about user experience. The other is after they have tried the product a bit: we ask why they are interested, what problems they are facing in their business, and try to identify the biggest pain point they are actually looking to solve.” —— Anton Osika, 20VC, 2025-03-05

## Lovable 对团队结构的判断很克制：AI 放大个人从零到一，但成熟产品仍需要工程质量

Anton 没有把 Lovable 讲成“工程师会消失”。他的判断更细：做个人网站、原型、简单产品，AI 可以让一个人直接完成；一旦产品已有用户和复杂 codebase，AI 可能会搞坏系统，仍然需要懂质量和一致性的工程师介入。这个边界很重要，因为 Lovable 的企业叙事并不是“绕过工程团队”，而是把不确定的 idea 和早期探索从工程队列里分离出来，让工程师在更确定、更高价值的位置接手。

“If you want to create a personal website, it's super productive. You can do it; you don't have a team. A team just slows you down. Maybe you get input from a designer on how it should look. But once you have existing software with users and you want to iterate and change that software, AI might absolutely mess up your entire codebase. Then you want to work with a software engineer who knows how to keep quality and consistently keep quality in the product.” —— Anton Osika, 20VC, 2025-03-05

## Prompt box 是 Lovable 的第一层组织产品：先给用户一个可立即行动的入口

Lovable 的 aha moment 设计很朴素：打开不是 landing page，而是 prompt box。Anton 承认 Lovable 在缩短 time-to-aha 上仍然做得不够，甚至说 conversion 可能还能翻倍；但现有设计已经把用户从“读介绍”推向“直接动手”。这和 Lovable 的组织节奏一致：减少解释层，先让用户交互，再用产品反馈决定下一步。AI 应用的入口如果太像 SaaS marketing page，会浪费模型能力带来的即时性。

“Time to aha is very important. It's funny because you ask me this question and I think we are very bad at making the time to aha super short. We could double our conversion rates if we became better at speed to aha. But when you come to Lovable, you just see a prompt box. It's very inviting. Instead of getting to a landing page, you see a prompt box, and for the ones who enter a prompt, you get quite a quick first aha moment.” —— Anton Osika, 20VC, 2025-03-05

“There have to be many aha moments in Lovable. A software engineer is a very complex feature that we are doing well. My recommendation is: give the user something interactive with instant reward.” —— Anton Osika, 20VC, 2025-03-05

## Anton 把 culture risk 定义成 ownership 被 headcount 稀释

Lovable 增长很快，但 Anton 对加人非常谨慎。他担心的不是办公室氛围变差，而是每个人对产品、用户和团队运转的 ownership 被稀释。这个说法很值得留：高速度组织的文化不是价值观墙，而是成员是否会主动 role model 对产品和用户的 care。人多之后，如果这种 care 无法复制，组织会从 founder-led speed 变成普通公司流程。

“The most important thing for everyone in our company is to role model how much you care about the product, the users, the entire team, and how well the team works. Make sure other people care as much as you. That comes from a feeling of ownership of the culture and the team. If you're a lot of people, that gets diluted typically. That's what breaks, or that's what's harder.” —— Anton Osika, 20VC, 2025-03-05

## Lovable 不怕资本更多的对手，怕自己决策 loop 变慢

面对美国资金更厚的竞争者，Anton 没有把融资当主变量。他的回答是 execution，进一步拆到 decision loop：决策速度、沟通速度、少做一点事。这个判断很像 Lovable 的组织总线：竞争不是谁拿更多钱，而是谁能更快判断该不该做、把决定传到所有人、然后停止做那些单点看起来不错但分散注意力的事。对 AI 应用公司，机会太多，真正的熵来自内部想法过剩。

“You never have to raise; you can bootstrap most things. They can outspend you on talent, customers, and marketing. I'm not afraid of any of those. The only thing that matters is execution. If you can outperform on execution, then I'm scared.” —— Anton Osika, 20VC, 2025-03-05

“You can always improve how fast you take decisions and communicate those decisions so everyone is on the same page. I think it's about doing fewer things as well at Lovable. There are a lot of people with great ideas, and each in isolation is great, but you should only execute on so many at the same time.” —— Anton Osika, 20VC, 2025-03-05

## Claude 是 Lovable 的代码 workhorse，但 Anton 认为 foundation model 绑定会弱化

Anton 直接说 Lovable 使用 OpenAI、Gemini，代码主力是 Anthropic Claude。这对理解 Lovable 的技术栈很重要：它的差异化不在训练模型，而在 agent chain、产品上下文、用户入口和编排细节。更有意思的是，Anton 后面又说自己改变了对模型供应商绑定的看法：不需要 attached to one provider，所有模型都会很强。这说明 Lovable 的组织会尽量把模型 provider 当可替换能力，而把真正的 know-how 放在产品层和 workflow 层。

“Today we use all of OpenAI's models, Google Gemini, and the main workhorse is Anthropic's Claude model for writing the code.” —— Anton Osika, 20VC, 2025-03-05

“What have I changed my mind on in the last twelve months? You don't need to be attached to one foundation model provider. They're all going to be amazing. I know Claude is better for code and engineering today, but I think that will equalize.” —— Anton Osika, 20VC, 2025-03-05

## Enterprise 被 Anton 主动后置，因为 Lovable 的 North Star 仍然是 builder density

Anton 对 enterprise 的态度不是没有需求，而是先按 builder community 走到足够大。他把 North Star 定义为“让一百万最有才华的 builders 在 Lovable 上创造产品”，认为这个目标达成后，enterprise 会自然打开。这里有一个很清晰的时序判断：先把最好的人和最多的可运行产品沉淀到平台，再去接企业采购和治理。过早转 enterprise 会改变组织节奏，也会把产品拉进定制化。

“If we do enterprise, I want to do that really well, so we're holding off with doing enterprise for now. Our goal, the North Star metric, is to be the best place for builders to create products and get a million of the most talented builders on Lovable. If we succeed in that, that is a great entry into many other areas, including enterprise.” —— Anton Osika, 20VC, 2025-03-05
