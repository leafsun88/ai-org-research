# Bonus: Anton Osika on how Lovable’s creating a world of builders

## Lovable 的 mission 不是给工程师提效，而是让不会写代码的人从零到一

Anton 在这条 Series A 访谈里把 Lovable 的“为什么”说得很清楚：工程师提效当然有价值，但更大的 impact 是让 99% 不会写代码的人把自己的 idea 变成软件。这个判断把 Lovable 从 developer tool 拉到社会生产力工具：大量好想法并不来自工程师，真正的 bottleneck 是把创造力短接到可运行产品。它也解释了 Lovable 的界面为什么要足够简单到“mom and anyone on the street”能用。

“After thinking about how many people are making engineers more productive with AI, I realized the long-term outcome is that anyone will be able to build software thanks to AI. Going from zero to one for so many more people is going to have much more impact. Many of the unique ideas, the best ideas, don't come from engineers. If you can short-circuit their ability to take their creativity and put it out in the world, it's going to be much more impactful.” —— Anton Osika, Spotlight On, 2025-07-17

“The idea was to create a completely new type of interface where you can do everything that a human software engineer can do, but it's used by your mom and anyone on the street.” —— Anton Osika, Spotlight On, 2025-07-17

## Founder team 的选择标准是 high urgency 和极端 pragmatic

Anton 选择 Fabian 的描述里，组织信号比故事更重要：他想要的 cofounder 不是 title partner，而是一个极端 pragmatic、高 urgency、专注 build、能快速把大想法变成产品的人。Lovable 的早期速度来自这个 founding pair 的互补：Anton 负责 vision 和外部叙事，Fabian 代表工程推进和低废话执行。对 AI 应用公司，技术窗口短，founding team 如果没有这种 urgency，模型能力的红利会被普通产品节奏吃掉。

“When I thought there was something humongous you can do with how fast core AI capabilities are advancing, I thought about what type of personalities I want in the founding team. From working with Fabian, I knew he was extremely pragmatic, high urgency, focused on just building and doing that as fast as possible. I ended up on my bicycle outside where he lived, called him up, and said, Fabian, let's go on a walk. I explained the big idea of figuring out the interface of the future to build software products, and we decided to build Lovable.” —— Anton Osika, Spotlight On, 2025-07-17

## 早期 ICP 假设连续变化，最后由 capability unlock 接管用户定义

Lovable 早期先试 rapid prototyping，再试 internal tools，最后发现真正大的方向是 full product / full business。这里的关键是，Lovable 没有一开始就被某个垂类 ICP 绑住，而是在模型能力和产品能力一起到位后，让用户自己发现数百个 use cases。这种路径对 AI 应用很常见：当 capability unlock 足够强，传统 ICP segmentation 会滞后。组织要做的是快速教育不同 use case，而不是过早把产品收窄。

“We went through a few different core beliefs about the ICP. The first hypothesis was rapid prototyping: you want to show what a product could look like. Then another iteration was internal tools, because one place you go from zero to one is internal tools in businesses. Ultimately, the exciting thing is to build a full product, a full business on top of this.” —— Anton Osika, Spotlight On, 2025-07-17

“After we launched and the product worked really well, a lot of things came together in a way no one else had something even close. Then we didn't have to think about who was using it for what. There was this huge capability unlock, and users found hundreds of different use cases. That's what we continue to lean into and educate for.” —— Anton Osika, Spotlight On, 2025-07-17

## Lovable 的 product cadence 是少数 bold bet 拆小块连续发布

Anton 对速度的描述不是“多 ship”，而是先有少数 opinionated bold bets，然后拆成小块发布、收 feedback、再发布下一块。这套 cadence 介于 roadmap 和完全 chaos 之间：方向是 founder/team 下注，验证来自用户反馈，小块发布降低错误成本。它适合 Lovable 这种能力边界快速变化的 AI 产品，因为大而硬的 roadmap 容易过时，纯反应式又会被噪音牵着走。

“The most important thing is to make sure you have a bit of focus. You take a few opinionated bold bets on where the product is going. For each opinionated bet, you split it up into small chunks of things that you announce and get feedback on, and then you announce and launch the next thing.” —— Anton Osika, Spotlight On, 2025-07-17

## 每周让工程师完整跑用户 flow，是 Lovable 简化复杂产品的组织动作

Anton 把产品 simplicity 归因到团队工作方式：工程师每周不只是拿 planned tasks，而是坐下来跑完整使用流程，判断哪里可以更简单、更 delightful。这是很具体的 AI 产品组织动作。Lovable 后台复杂度很高，前台必须极简；如果工程师只接 tickets，复杂性会不断漏到用户界面。每周重新体验 flow，相当于把“非技术用户视角”制度化。

“Every week, engineers don't just take the planned tasks and do them. They sit down and go through the entire flow of using the product and think: is this actually the best way to do it? They try to polish, simplify, and make it as delightful as possible.” —— Anton Osika, Spotlight On, 2025-07-17

## Lovable culture 的核心是 care、team-first 和 high agency

Anton 对 culture 的定义不是福利或调性，而是每个人是否真的 care：care about what we build、care who joins、care how we communicate with customers、care about product details。第二层是 team-first：即使你有好想法，也要 build on top of others，让团队一起跑得快。这套文化非常贴合 Lovable 的小团队高速度：如果每个人只优化自己的 idea，组织会被局部最优拖散；如果每个人都把产品和团队当成自己的，公司才能在高不确定性下保持一致。

“The most important thing for building a generational and category-defining company is that everyone is there because they really care about what we want to build and want to build something big together. Everyone cares about who we bring into the team, that they share the same obsession, how we communicate with customers and partners, and the details of the product being perfect.” —— Anton Osika, Spotlight On, 2025-07-17

“The second part is how you work together as a team: a team-first mentality. Even if you know you have an amazing idea that everyone else should listen to, you build on top of what everyone is doing so you can run really fast together. From the beginning, we had mostly founder experience in the team, and people with founder experience often share deep care and high agency.” —— Anton Osika, Spotlight On, 2025-07-17

## Lovable 后来重新接纳 senior talent，因为 senior 的任务变成放大年轻高斜率人才

Anton 早期对 senior executives 很谨慎，来自上一家公司过早招高管的教训。但到 Lovable 规模上来后，他开始重新接受“select very senior people”，前提是他们理解文化、能保护年轻人影响力、能把 junior high potential 带起来。这是一个成熟信号：Lovable 不再只是 founder + junior generalists 冲锋，而是在补能 coach、能 scale、又不压低 agency 的 senior layer。

“In my last company, I think we hired executives too early. They can be difficult to evaluate because you're impressed by what they've done and what they've learned, and you're not evaluating the core traits of the individual. Now I've met more people who share the traits I'm looking for, and they have impressive backgrounds. I'm more positive towards adding select very senior people who care a lot about culture and understand the benefit of a team where young individuals have a lot of influence and are not just told what to do.” —— Anton Osika, Spotlight On, 2025-07-17

“We have quite a bunch of very young people and also a lot of very senior people. That mix is very good, because if you have a tight feedback loop between young people and very senior people, the young people learn much faster.” —— Anton Osika, Spotlight On, 2025-07-17

## Stockholm 的优势是相对人才池：Lovable 更容易成为 ambitious talent 的第一选择

Anton 承认从 Stockholm 做全球公司是 hard mode，但他同时指出欧洲有更多正在寻找 Lovable 这种机会的 ambitious talent。这个说法不是“欧洲人才更好”，而是相对供需：硅谷同一批人面对 OpenAI、Anthropic、xAI、Meta 的持续争夺，Stockholm 的顶级机会更少，Lovable 一旦成为区域最大磁铁，就能吸收一批 raw talent 和 founder-minded people。区域劣势被组织 momentum 部分抵消。

“Part of building from Stockholm compared to San Francisco is doing it on hard mode. The network is more limited in terms of people who've done it before, especially on tech, culture, hiring and managing. I'm here because I know you can do something global and write history from Stockholm.” —— Anton Osika, Spotlight On, 2025-07-17

“Talent is equally spread out in the world, the raw talent. There are more people looking for something like Lovable, super ambitious and looking for something like Lovable, in Europe, because here there aren't as many other places they would go to. So we have a larger pool, relatively speaking, of great people.” —— Anton Osika, Spotlight On, 2025-07-17

## Lovable 想带着 community 一起走到 AI native entrepreneur，而不是只卖一个工具

Anton 对下一步的定义是 empower anyone to build，并把关键 persona 说成 AI native entrepreneur：可以来自印度、波兰或任何背景，理解好产品怎么做，然后用 Lovable 翻译成能帮助人的业务。这个方向说明 Lovable 需要的不只是产品能力，还要教育、support、community 和 founder playbook。它不是纯软件工具，更像一个让新创业人群获得产品能力的生态入口。

“The way we talk about our mission is to empower anyone to build and through that unlock human creativity. One specific persona is the AI-native entrepreneur that comes from any background in the world. They might sit in India or in Poland, have a good understanding of how to build a great product, and use Lovable to translate that into a successful business that helps people.” —— Anton Osika, Spotlight On, 2025-07-17

“We're going to do that by making Lovable able to do more things, do everything it does fully reliably even though it's AI, and make sure we take the community with us: helping them, educating them, and supporting how they even build a successful business.” —— Anton Osika, Spotlight On, 2025-07-17
