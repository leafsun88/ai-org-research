# 20VC: Lovable CEO Anton Osika on $120M ARR in 7 Months

## Lovable 的资本观很清楚：钱不是约束，团队和信任才是约束

Anton 对 AI application company 的判断比融资新闻更重要。他没有把 Lovable 的竞争解释成谁融得更多，而是把约束放在 team、brand 和 user trust 上。这个口径和 foundation model company 不一样：模型公司要烧 compute，application company 更像一场人才和速度的组织竞赛。Lovable 已经融了很多钱，但 Anton 的重点仍然是“能不能持续吸引最适合这类产品的人，并让用户相信每次更新都没有破坏他们的工作流”。这能解释 Lovable 为什么在早期极强调 founder mode、产品速度和公开透明，因为这些东西共同服务一个目标：让一个小团队持续拿到比资本更稀缺的注意力、信任和人才。

“Is it a capital arms race, where whoever has the most money wins, or is it something else? I think it's an arms race to build the best team. And then there's an arms race to build the best brand and trust from your users. Capital can help, but for us it's not a constraint at all.” —— Anton Osika, 20VC, 2025-08-18

Anton 同时把 brand 和 trust 放在资本之后的第二个核心变量，说明 Lovable 的防御不是先从专利、模型或锁定合同开始，而是先从用户日常使用里建立“我愿意继续把产品和想法放在这个平台上”的信任。

## Anton 的招聘雷达押在 slope 上，不押在大厂履历和标准答案上

Lovable 的招聘标准不是“找看起来最像顶级工程师的人”。Anton 反复讲的是 slope：候选人能不能在对话中让他学到东西，能不能推动 conversation 变得动态，能不能进入一个高速变化的组织后继续变陡。这个判断很适合 AI application layer，因为这里的关键人才不一定是训练 foundation model 的十个人，而是能把模型能力、用户需求和产品体验快速接起来的人。Anton 甚至承认自己并不知道“完美工程师”是谁，所以 Lovable 的 hiring 更像寻找可塑性、判断力和 team fit，而不是按固定简历模板筛人。

“I like to think a little about slope. If I talk to someone and I learn a lot of things from them, and I notice that my conversation is very dynamic and exciting, that is usually a very good indicator. It means they are going to adapt to the organization, and their slope will be very high.” —— Anton Osika, 20VC, 2025-08-18

Anton 把 Meta 用天价合同抢模型训练专家和 Lovable 找 application engineer 分得很开：前者买的是少数人掌握的训练知识，后者需要的是能在产品、文化、用户理解之间高速适应的人。

## Founder mode 没有消失，开始长出一层 founder-type generalists

Anton 没有把公司变大后的结构问题回答成“保持扁平就好”。他承认 Lovable 仍然会由 founder mode 贡献最大 impact，但公司进入 growth stage 后，机会、噪音和外部请求会同时变多。于是他需要一层靠近自己的 protective layer，帮助判断什么该做、什么不该做。这层不是传统意义上的中层管理，更像一组 previous founder type generalists：他们贴着 Anton 工作，用快速反馈处理优先级，把 founder 的判断变成可扩展的过滤系统。这个动作很关键，因为 Lovable 之前的速度来自极短信息链；规模上来后，如果没有这层判断放大器，founder mode 会变成 founder bottleneck。

“I'm going to always operate with most of my impact coming from founder mode. But given that there are so many things thrown at me from all different directions, I do need a protective layer that introduces order. How do we prioritize all these incoming things? The team is made up of previous founder-type generalists working closely with me, and I work with them through quick feedback: this is not what you should be doing, this is what you should be doing.” —— Anton Osika, 20VC, 2025-08-18

Anton 说这层人和他一起工作，核心互动是快速告诉他“这不是你该做的事，那才是你该做的事”。这句话背后是一个组织设计：保护创始人注意力，同时尽量不牺牲早期的判断速度。

## Lovable 的 brand 不是市场部资产，而是每次产品更新后的用户反应

Anton 讲 brand 时举 Apple，不是在讲审美，而是在讲每一次 interaction 都会累积或消耗 trust。Lovable 的产品变化极快，模型能力也每月都在变，用户会不断遇到新能力、新限制和新 bug。对这类公司来说，brand 不是广告投放后的认知度，而是产品快速变化时用户仍然愿意相信你没有乱来。Anton 关心每次 rollout 后用户怎么反应，本质上是在把 brand 当成 product operating metric：产品速度越快，越需要用用户反应校准信任边界。

Anton 把 Apple 的细节和慢动作作为反例式参照：Lovable 不可能像 Apple 一样慢，但必须学会在高速 ship 时保留足够的 trust。

“A super concrete example is the Apple ecosystem, where they obsess about details. Maybe too much, so they move slowly, but that's what builds up trust and a very strong brand. That's what brand means for us as well in every interaction. Every time we update the product, how do we roll it out so that we really understand the users and their reactions to all the things we're changing rapidly in the product and in the company?” —— Anton Osika, 20VC, 2025-08-18

## Lovable 的 defensibility 来自用户在平台上持续累积的工作，而不是单点生成能力

Anton 对防御性的回答有一个很强的产品假设：如果用户在平台上持续创造价值，并且这些价值每天自动回到用户身上，用户就不愿离开。Lovable 今天是 technical cofounder，但他想把它扩成 general cofounder，覆盖 admin、finance operations、growth、customer communication 等环节。这个方向把 Lovable 从“prompt-to-app 工具”推向“创业和产品组织操作层”。如果这个判断成立，护城河不是某一次代码生成效果，而是用户把越来越多的公司活动放进 Lovable 后，迁移成本和工作流依赖一起上升。

Anton 对产品终局的描述不是只写代码，而是从 idea stage 一直陪用户到有 customers、做增长、优化产品和客户沟通。这里能看到 Lovable 的 TAM 扩张方式：不是只吃开发工具预算，而是吃掉早期公司里多个职能的工作流。

“If you want maximum defensibility, you need to build a product where, if you are on the platform, you don't want to leave because you have created so much value there and you get that value automatically every day. That's what Lovable is becoming. Today Lovable is your technical cofounder. We want it to be your cofounder in general, with all the admin, setting up your finance operations, and the things around building a company.” —— Anton Osika, 20VC, 2025-08-18

## AI startup 的早期操作系统是先高速飞起来，再补防御

Anton 用一个很粗粝但有用的比喻解释 AI startup：如果你开始有 traction，就先拼命 flap，后面每天都会有新的鸡被 cannon 打上天。这个表达背后是 Lovable 的早期 operating logic：AI 应用层的窗口变化太快，过早讨论 defensibility 可能会拖慢速度。先跑出用户、增长、brand 和产品手感，再把防御性补起来。对 Lovable 来说，这不是否认护城河，而是把护城河放到时序里：第一阶段是速度和增长，第二阶段才是平台化、企业化和 workflow lock-in。

“AI startups are like chickens shot out of a cannon up in the sky. If you start getting traction, it's all about flapping fast as a chicken, because there are new chickens shot out from cannons every day. If you keep flapping faster than the other chickens, you're going to do great. My recommendation is: execute fast, grow faster; when you're starting to get up there, then maybe start thinking a bit about defensibility.” —— Anton Osika, 20VC, 2025-08-18

这也解释了 Lovable 为什么愿意在早期承受较高模型成本和较低结构化流程：它更怕错过窗口，而不是一开始就把商业模型优化到漂亮。

## Lovable 的 unit economics 现在难看，但 Anton 在等收入结构从 build usage 迁到 platform value

访谈里 Harry 直接问了一美元收入有多少流给 Anthropic 和 OpenAI，Anton 没给具体比例，但承认 paid usage 里模型成本占重要部分。他的解法不是立刻优化模型，而是改变用户付费的原因：今天用户主要为 build 付费，未来要让用户因为平台价值持续留在 subscription 上。这里的关键不是毛利率今天是多少，而是 Lovable 能不能把价值从“生成一次产品”迁移到“持续经营一个产品或公司”。如果用户留在平台上做支付、增长、客户沟通和运营，模型 compute 就会从收入主体成本变成平台成本的一小部分。

Anton 还提到 Lovable 应用里已经有相当规模的 AI revenue flow，但用户连接模型 provider 的过程仍然复杂，Lovable 可以通过简化连接和降低底层成本拿走一部分 margin。这是很早期的平台税雏形。

“If you look at paid usage, I won't give you the exact numbers, but it is not everything. As our business develops, we're looking to get most of our revenue once you, as a user, say: I love this platform, I'm never leaving. Today, in the beginning, you're mostly paying to build. Over time, we want to create so much value that you stay on the subscription, and only a small part of the cost goes to AI compute.” —— Anton Osika, 20VC, 2025-08-18

“A few months ago we looked at how much revenue was flowing through AI from Lovable applications, and it was more than $10 million in ARR. All of that revenue needed the user to go through this complex process of setting up the connection to the model providers. We're looking at simplifying that. If we can reduce the underlying cost, maybe we can take a margin there as well.” —— Anton Osika, 20VC, 2025-08-18

## Lovable 选择先追模型能力边界，不急着把模型优化成便宜工具

Anton 对模型成本优化的态度很克制：现在模型每个月都在做新的事，Lovable 过早围绕今天的模型能力做优化，可能会把产品锁进旧边界。Lovable 更像是在“明天模型能做什么”上 build，而不是把今天的任务拆成最便宜的 routing。这个判断和普通 SaaS 成本优化逻辑冲突，但适合当下 AI 应用：当模型边界还在快速外扩时，最值钱的是快速把新能力产品化，而不是把旧能力榨干。

Anton 说 Lovable 有复杂的 agentic chain，会把用户请求、应用信息和不同模型串起来；小而快的模型处理 obvious task，深度 thinking model 处理新情况和复杂 debugging。这个系统观比“接哪个模型”更重要：Lovable 的产品能力来自模型编排、用户上下文和应用状态的组合。

“For us, it's too early to optimize for that because AI is, every month, doing new, completely different things. We just want to iterate really fast on what the AI is able to do, and not optimize the models for what it's doing today. When it's obvious what to do, it should cost almost no money and be super fast. But when it's a new situation, which building a software product often is, then it has to think much more.” —— Anton Osika, 20VC, 2025-08-18

“We have this very complex agentic chain where we pass the user's response and the application information through many different models. We use really fast and small ones, and for code writing we usually use Anthropic. Right now you can say, I want to use GPT-5, and that's better when you're solving a really hard debugging problem.” —— Anton Osika, 20VC, 2025-08-18

## Lovable 更怕 OpenAI 的产品能力，而不是 Anthropic 的开发者心智

当 Harry 问 OpenAI 和 Anthropic 谁更可能来打 Lovable，Anton 把 OpenAI 看得更重。原因很直接：Lovable 押的是成为 humans to AI 的 gateway，关键变量是 user experience，而 OpenAI 在这件事上做得比 Anthropic 更强。这个判断很有价值，因为它把竞争从“谁代码模型更强”拉回“谁更理解普通用户和产品入口”。Lovable 当然会用 Anthropic 写代码，但它真正担心的是一个更好的 AI interface 把用户入口拿走。

“What we're betting on is to be the gateway for humans and to be the best user experience for AI. So far, OpenAI is doing that better than Anthropic, so I see them as a more serious competitor. We usually use Anthropic for code writing. GPT-5 is better when you're solving a really hard debugging problem, but the model is still often too ambitious for our users.” —— Anton Osika, 20VC, 2025-08-18

Anton 对 GPT-5 的评价也体现这个视角：模型很聪明，但对 Lovable 用户经常太 ambitious。Lovable 的工作不是把最强模型直接塞给用户，而是判断模型能力、响应时延、用户预期和具体 use case 之间的 tradeoff。

## Lovable 的企业化会补销售团队，但 Anton 不想把公司变成传统 enterprise machine

Lovable 现在有很强的 enterprise pull，Anton 明确说会有 enterprise sales team，但他同时划了边界：这不是靠 wine and dine CEO 的 top-down enterprise sales，而是理解客户、帮助客户拿到产品价值。这个区别决定 Lovable 后续 GTM 的组织形态。如果它变成传统大客户定制公司，速度会被吃掉；如果它只做纯 self-serve，又可能接不住企业内部需求和治理要求。Anton 的答案是补 enterprise muscle，但尽量让它服务 product-led adoption，而不是反过来改造公司节奏。

Anton 还提到大公司里的个人和团队正在用 Lovable 把想法做成 working product，再决定是否交给工程团队实现。这类用法把 Lovable 放进企业需求生成链条，而不是单纯替代 IDE。

“Will Lovable have an enterprise sales team? Yes. Will it become an enterprise company? It will not become an enterprise company, but it will have its enterprise sales team. I'm not so nervous about it. To me, it is just talking to and understanding your customers, and making sure they have the tools to get value from the product. A top-down enterprise sales team hustling to wine and dine CEOs is not what we're going to do.” —— Anton Osika, 20VC, 2025-08-18

## Stockholm 给 Lovable 的优势是人才磁场和低流失，而不是欧洲浪漫主义

Anton 对欧洲优势的说法非常实际：Lovable 是 Stockholm 最大的人才磁铁，更容易吸收 underutilized talent，并通过更好的 culture 和 working style 放大表现。这里的重点不是“欧洲也能做 AI 公司”的情绪，而是区域竞争中的相对位势：在 San Francisco 或 New York，顶尖人才旁边永远有 OpenAI、更高 package 和更高流动性；在 Stockholm，Lovable 更容易成为第一选择。低 ego、team orientation、用更少做更多，也能让知识在团队里 compound，而不是被高流动性打断。

Anton 认为 Valley 的高 attrition 会伤害团队内知识复利：一个人 bad day 就可能被 OpenAI 更高 package 挖走。Lovable 在 Stockholm 反而可以把组织稳定性变成一种训练优势。

“We are the biggest talent magnet in Stockholm right now, which is amazing. It's much more difficult to be that in San Francisco or New York. We can pick up all the underutilized talent and 10x their performance by being in a 10x better culture and ways of working, with amazing colleagues. You have inherently higher attrition in the Valley; when you have a bad day, OpenAI offers you a bigger package, and that prevents the compounding of knowledge within teams.” —— Anton Osika, 20VC, 2025-08-18

## GPT Engineer 的教训是 sequencing，不是开源有没有价值

Anton 复盘早期 GPT Engineer 时很直接：当时 vision 清楚，但 sequencing 不清楚；Lovable 应该更早把精力全部放到浏览器里直接 build product 的未来形态上。这个反思不是否定 open source，而是在讲 focus。开源社区可以带来早期用户和声量，但如果它和最终产品方向只是 tangentially related，就会稀释速度。Lovable 现在反复讲 bottleneck，背后就是这个教训：每个阶段只找最限制公司的那个瓶颈，然后集中解决。

Anton 把长期瓶颈定义成能否找到 technical product engineers，带产品进入下一阶段并在多个方向同时创新；短期瓶颈则是让 AI 获得更多能力、让用户能在 Lovable 上完整搭建和经营公司。

“When we started, the vision was very clear. The sequencing was not so clear. We had this open-source community around a tool I made a few months before we started the company, GPT Engineer. I think we should have scrapped that completely and focused 100% on what the future looks like: opening your browser and just building your product there, which is Lovable.” —— Anton Osika, 20VC, 2025-08-18

“Open source can be very useful for many businesses, but from the perspective of maximal focus it was a bad idea to do two things that were too tangentially related. We talk a lot about doing one thing, finding the bottleneck for the company, and solving that bottleneck. For the long-term future, the bottleneck is how we identify the technical product engineers who will take the product to its next phase and innovate on many fronts at the same time.” —— Anton Osika, 20VC, 2025-08-18

## Anton 和 Fabian 的 cofounder fit 来自 polarity，而不是两个人都像创始人 CEO

Lovable 的 cofounder 结构里有一个有用的组织细节：Anton 是外向、愿意试新东西的一端，Fabian 更安静、更倾向于简化和等观点成型后再说。这种 polarity 让 Lovable 的速度不至于变成纯冲动，也让产品判断有一个反向制动器。Anton 提到低 ego 和 raw horsepower/adaptability，比“职责分工”更接近真正的 cofounder fit。对高速 AI 公司来说，创始人之间能不能互相 challenge、turn every stone，比表面 title 更重要。

Anton 还说 Fabian 需要继续专注 build product，所以 Lovable 对外仍然以 Anton 作为主要人格化界面。这是品牌策略，也是组织分工：一个人承接叙事和市场心智，另一个人保持产品推进。

“I'd love for Fabian to have more exposure, but I also want him to be focused on building the product, and that's what he focuses on. It's much easier for people to relate to Lovable if they see one person and keep seeing that one person; today, that is me. The most important thing in a cofounding pair is raw horsepower and adaptability. Fabian is quiet until he has really shaped an opinion; I'm on the other side, saying we should use this new crazy thing, and that polarity is productive.” —— Anton Osika, 20VC, 2025-08-18

## Lovable 的 2026 产品想象，是把 AI engineer 扩成公司生命周期的 cofounder

Anton 对 2026 的产品描述很大：从 idea stage 到 customers，再到 growth、product optimization、customer communication，Lovable 都要覆盖。这说明 Lovable 的目标不是做一个更好用的 app builder，而是逐步吃掉一个早期公司从产品到增长的操作系统。这个方向也解释了为什么 enterprise 会自然出现：大公司里的团队同样需要把想法变成 working product，并在内部推进资源决策。Lovable 如果能把这条链接住，它卖的就不是“生成代码”，而是“让组织里的非工程想法以可运行产品的形式流动”。

Anton 讲到未来应用都带 AI、支付和 checkout 要极其顺滑，这说明他对“应用是什么”的理解也在变：未来应用不是传统前后端加功能，而是 AI、支付、增长、用户沟通这些能力默认内建。

“At the end of 2026, Lovable is your perfect cofounder. You go to it with your idea from the idea stage, but also all the way up to growing your business once you have customers. It helps optimize the product for growth, optimize the product itself, and optimize your communication with customers through email or different marketing channels. It is one opinionated way to do the entire product life cycle.” —— Anton Osika, 20VC, 2025-08-18

## Anton 对 benchmark 的怀疑，其实是在提醒 Lovable 不能被 thumbs-up 这类指标 hack

Anton 讲 benchmark 会随着优化而失效，又把这个问题拉回 Lovable 自己：如果团队开始优化 thumbs up 或 message count，系统很容易学会让用户多点按钮，而不是让产品真的更好。这是一个很具体的 AI product management 风险。Lovable 的产品有大量 AI interaction，如果只看显性反馈，很容易被模型话术、UI 提示或用户短期情绪带偏。真正有价值的 evaluation 要贴近用户是否能完成任务、是否持续回来、是否把更重要的工作交给平台。

这个片段值得保留，因为它能解释 Lovable 为什么需要更强的 eval infrastructure 和 feedback loop：AI 应用不是传统 SaaS，很多指标会被模型本身反向影响，越优化越失真。

“Benchmarks turn more and more bullshit over time. There is something called Goodhart's law: when you start optimizing for a number, that number stops being a good measure of success, even if it was a great measure before. In Lovable, something like how many people click the thumbs-up button, or how many messages there are, means less if we start optimizing for it. We could make the system ask the human to click the button, and then we're hacking the metric.” —— Anton Osika, 20VC, 2025-08-18
