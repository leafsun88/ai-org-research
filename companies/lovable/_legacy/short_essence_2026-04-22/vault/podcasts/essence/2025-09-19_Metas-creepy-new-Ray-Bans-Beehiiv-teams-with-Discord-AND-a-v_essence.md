# TWIST: A Visit From Lovable CEO Anton Osika

## Lovable 到 9 月已经把 paying customers 从 2 万推到接近 20 倍

Anton 在 TWIST 里给出一个很强的增长线索：2 月左右刚到 20,000 paying customers，9 月已经 almost 20x。这个数字比 ARR headline 更适合说明扩散速度，因为它对应真实付费用户数量。更重要的是，他把增长和“99% non-coders”以及 enterprise speed 放在一起，说明 Lovable 的付费扩张不只来自 AI 尝鲜，而来自个人创业和企业内部团队两端同时采用。

“Last time we spoke in February, I think we had just hit 20,000 paying customers or something, and now we're almost at 20x that. We're still very early days on the product. What we're going to do is let the 99% who don't know how to code instantly turn their ideas into software. That's unlocking entrepreneurship and speeding up very large enterprises where product managers, designers and marketers can move without being bottlenecked by engineers.” —— Anton Osika, This Week in Startups, 2025-09-19

## Lovable 已经能让付费用户毛利为正，compute cost 没有压垮商业模式

Jason 追问 Lovable 是否单用户亏损，Anton 给出的回答很重要：free users 和 paying users 拆开看，paying users 已经 gross margin profitable。这不代表公司整体成熟盈利，但它反驳了“AI app revenue 全是烧 token 买来的糖水”这一类最粗糙的怀疑。Lovable 仍然要优化模型成本和免费用户漏斗，但它的付费层已经有基本 unit economics 支撑。

“We want to make it as cheap as possible to build and create things. Today we're in a very good place where we can very easily become profitable without big changes to how our business works. If you split up free users and paying users, we have gross margin profitability on the paying ones. That means we can acquire and grow users very rapidly without freaking out about our bank account.” —— Anton Osika, This Week in Startups, 2025-09-19

## Anton 几乎不想打价格战，因为核心变量仍是 generational product

被问到 pricing war，Anton 的回答很有 Lovable 味道：他大多数周甚至不怎么想定价，主要想的是把 customers love 的产品做成 generational company。这个口径不是说 pricing 不重要，而是说明 Lovable 当前仍处于能力和心智窗口期。对 AI 应用公司，过早把战略压成价格竞争，会让团队错过产品形态升级和工作流占位。

“I don't think much about pricing. You're thinking more about pricing in our conversation here than I do most weeks. We're just thinking about building a product that our customers love and making that into a generational product and a generational company on top of that. We want Lovable to be used by a billion developers or builders that don't have to write software code. There are 50 million developers today, and it should be the foundation where people build anything.” —— Anton Osika, This Week in Startups, 2025-09-19

## Security 被 Anton 定义成“没做好就不该 build”，并押注 AI reviewer 超过人类 reviewer

TWIST 里 security 讨论很深。Anton 把 security 说成 top priority，并用登月火箭类比：复杂系统里，人类会犯错，某些范围内计算机更可靠。这个比喻的含义是，Lovable 不可能靠用户自己懂安全来规模化，必须把 AI security review、扫描、guardrails 做进系统。Anton 还判断未来 18 个月左右，Lovable 平均安全水平会超过专家，这个判断很激进，但能体现产品方向。

“We take security as the top priority, because if you're building something that is not secure, you should not have built it at all. In the 60s, sending people to the moon, you could have put a human in the cockpit steering the rocket ship, but you realized you need a computer to run that mission because it is much more reliable in a certain scope. Humans may always make errors. We're going to see the same thing happen to computer security: you are not simply going to trust a human writing secure code; you need an AI system to write secure code.” —— Anton Osika, This Week in Startups, 2025-09-19

“Over the last few months, we've launched several security features that scan all the clients and code that the AI has written for you. I cannot say it is more secure than a security expert today, so I recommend looping in a security expert for life-and-death applications or very sensitive data. But for applications built fully in Lovable, on average it is going to be more secure in the coming 18 months.” —— Anton Osika, This Week in Startups, 2025-09-19

## Opinionated stack 是 Lovable 的可靠性选择，不是能力自我限制

听众质疑 Lovable 的 opinionated stack 会限制 capabilities，Anton 的回答很关键：Lovable 需要具备 95% SaaS 所需 primitives，真正 opinionated 的部分是这些 primitives 如何组合成技术架构。这个框架解释了 Lovable 为什么能服务非技术用户：它不是开放式代码环境，而是把常见 SaaS 架构封装成 AI 更容易决策和操作的空间。牺牲一部分任意性，换来可靠性、安全和一键能力。

“Lovable needs to have all the primitives on the software architecture side to create everything you need for 95% of SaaS companies. A Facebook-scale product may be hard to vibe code on Lovable, but how these primitives come together in the technical architecture is what's opinionated. As long as you have all the primitives, there should not be significant narrowing of what you can produce.” —— Anton Osika, This Week in Startups, 2025-09-19

“We're building the full platform where we make it as easy as possible for the AI to take decisions within our specific architecture for how applications are built. There is a strong synergy in adapting it to AI, polishing everything around it, and adding guardrails for the AI, while the big labs release new models every three months with greater capabilities. Us making it opinionated, unlike Claude Code and Cursor, is why it's becoming more reliable for non-technical people.” —— Anton Osika, This Week in Startups, 2025-09-19

## Lovable 的企业价值在于让 PM、designer、marketer 先 build，再让工程接最后一段

Anton 在 TWIST 里对 enterprise workflow 的表述很清楚：PM、designer、marketer 能几小时做出 prototype，给经理或客户试，再决定是否进入工程系统。这里的“节省 60% 时间”不是替代工程师，而是减少不确定性和反复解释。工程师拿到的不再是抽象 memo，而是已经验证过的 specification。这是 Lovable 对产品组织的直接改造。

“Product managers, designers and marketers can move much faster. Lovable is the best tool for them to prove out: here's my great idea. I'm not just going to talk about the idea; I'm going to build it in a few hours, show it to my manager, or try it with customers and say, let's launch this. Then you've saved 60% of the time, and engineers have this perfect specification to go implement in the system.” —— Anton Osika, This Week in Startups, 2025-09-19

## AI native 年轻人会用 Lovable 做 spec work 3.0，改变大公司招聘和内部创新

TWIST 主持人把 Lovable 连接到年轻人就业和“spec work 3.0”，Anton 顺着讲了自己公司里一个 20 岁 automation 人才的例子。这个片段很适合组织分析：AI native 年轻人的竞争优势不是多年经验，而是能主动把重复工作产品化、自动化、做成 demo。Lovable 这类工具会提高 entry-level worker 的表达能力，也会改变企业内部谁能推动改变。

“Young people who often learn faster and have more initiative-taking capability are going to come to companies and say: I can do all these new things that your staff currently cannot, because I grew up AI-native. I actually have this 20-year-old helping with automations in our company. I can't put the most senior engineers on automations, but the mission I'm telling him is: you have to automate Lovable. That's the goal for you: automate everything Lovable.” —— Anton Osika, This Week in Startups, 2025-09-19

## Lovable mafia 被 Anton 当成长期生态结果，但他用有限二级出售控制风险偏好

Jason 问 Lovable mafia 和员工流动，Anton 回到自己上一家公司 Depict 的经验：已经有多家 YC 公司从 Depict 出来。Lovable 也可能形成同样的创业扩散。但他对员工二级出售的处理很谨慎：允许拿一部分 chips off the table，降低个人风险厌恶，但限制总量，避免过早失去工作动力。这是高速估值上升公司很现实的组织问题，和人才留存直接相关。

“I used to say this in the last company I built, Depict, and from there we're seeing four YC companies come out, multiple valued at hundreds of millions of dollars. With Lovable, it is more in the future. We want to help our employees; taking some chips off the table should be done so you can think less about being risk-averse. In secondary sales, we limit the total amount.” —— Anton Osika, This Week in Startups, 2025-09-19

## Trillion-dollar Europe 叙事是 Lovable 的外部目标，也是内部证明场

Anton 在 TWIST 里把 Lovable 说成“Europe 的第一个 trillion-dollar company”的候选。这个数字本身可以争论，但它在组织上有功能：让团队用全球最大公司的标准要求自己，也把欧洲人才拉到同一个高目标下。它不是简单 PR；对一个从 Stockholm 起步、融资和用户增长都被全球关注的公司，极大目标可以成为招聘、文化和生态叙事的共同语言。

“I think Stockholm is very underrated. What we're set on is the first trillion-dollar company from Europe from here. We're seeing more unicorns minted in Stockholm than other European cities this year, and there is momentum building. Lovable should be a foundation where people build anything, and not just have this trillion-dollar outcome as a goal, but as a proving ground where ambition, talent and strong culture from European companies can be inspired to have large global goals.” —— Anton Osika, This Week in Startups, 2025-09-19
