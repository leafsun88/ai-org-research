# Rick Smith's Tasers and the Social-Control Economy

*Source: CounterPunch (Lee Hall)*

Arizona-based Axon Enterprise, Inc. (formerly Taser International) produces electroshock weapons. CEO Rick Smith's corporate slogan is *Protect Life*. Smith has gone so far as to suggest that using a Taser 10 is safer than playing volleyball.

## Key Criticism Points

**On TASER safety:**
The article cites Reuters' 2023 investigation examining Axon's corporate culture, noting that tasing "can be fatal." Examples cited: Kenneth Espinoza (handcuffed senior, repeatedly tased in squad car); Daryl Williams (heart condition, informed officers, repeatedly shocked, lost consciousness and died an hour later).

**On early development:**
Smith recalls running experiments on a living pig to tune the shock intensity: "And then we could ramp up or down the intensity using some pretty gross system adjustments. And just doing that experiment and then observing the muscle contractions of that pig, we were able to very quickly identify what we needed to change."

**On monopoly power:**
In 2018, Axon bought out NYPD bodycam supplier VieVu LLC. Axon fought FTC monopoly complaints all the way to U.S. Supreme Court (Supremes backed Axon on jurisdictional point; FTC dropped its case). By 2022, Axon claimed ~17,000 of 18,000 U.S. police agencies as clientele. Cities now pay far more for Axon's policing products without VieVu competition. 2023: cities (Howell NJ, Baltimore, Augusta ME) went to court to challenge Axon's monopoly (mixed results).

**On surveillance expansion:**
- Axon Body Workforce introduced into hospitals and medical offices — giving the company access to protected health information
- Police can get access to doorbell data unbeknownst to customers who paid to create it; Axon involved with Amazon's Ring doorbell cameras
- Seattle Police Department engaged Axon for drone surveillance; Axon's partner of choice is **Skydio, purveyor of reconnaissance drones to the IDF**

**On Draft One / AI:**
In 2024, Axon introduced Draft One — turns footage from its bodycams into police reports, using a ChatGPT variant. ACLU warns that AI can digest biases from data fed into it. 
- Utah enacted law (March 2025) forcing police to disclose any use of generative AI
- California enacted law (January 2026) barring police from using Draft One tools without retaining original AI-generated report and creating record-keeping protocol

The EFF has found: "Axon is tracking police use of the technology at a level that isn't available to the police department itself." The system is designed to be opaque. Consequences for lying "may be more lenient for a cop who blames it on the AI."

**On the drone controversy:**
After Uvalde, Smith announced Axon would roll out drone-based electroshock weapons for schools. This set off concerns in the Axon ethics board — potential privacy intrusions, racial injustice, additional hazards. The **majority of Axon's AI ethics board decided to resign**. "Which raises questions about why an ethics board would be formed—yet not consulted in advance of such a startling announcement from the CEO."

**On 911 takeover:**
"Next, watch as Axon takes over emergency call services and shapes responses with artificial intelligence. By promising quicker and stronger responses to calls, Axon is poised to amass a sprawling network of data that overlaps policing and social control."

The article concludes that Axon's growth is driven by social control and surveillance expansion, with insufficient oversight or accountability mechanisms.
