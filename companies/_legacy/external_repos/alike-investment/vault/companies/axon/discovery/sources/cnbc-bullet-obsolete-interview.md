# Axon Enterprise CEO describes how the company aims 'to make the bullet obsolete'

*Source: CNBC (Julie Coleman), Jim Cramer interview*

On Wednesday, Axon Enterprise's CEO Rick Smith shared insights with CNBC's Jim Cramer regarding the company's initiatives aimed at minimizing gun usage within law enforcement.

## Key Quotes

**On mission:**
> "Our goal is to render bullets unnecessary, specifically aiming to decrease gun-related fatalities in policing by 50% over the next decade. We plan to achieve this through tasers, body cameras, cloud-based software, and we now have a rapidly growing AI division."

**On AI adoption:**
Smith elaborated on how Axon leverages body camera recordings and AI to help police departments draft initial police reports, enhancing operational efficiency. He noted that **the AI services introduced this year are being adopted at a rate five times faster than any previous product in the company's history**.

**On drones:**
While AI is currently the company's focal point, he hinted at **drones being the next significant offering**.

**On Nordic countries:**
Smith praised Nordic countries for their exemplary use of taser technology. Police in those regions typically do not carry firearms during regular duties, making tasers a more suitable alternative.
> "I believe they will play a crucial role in our mission to eliminate the need for bullets. The Nordics are set to pave the way forward."

## Context

The company captured investor attention after announcing its Q4 2024 earnings, with stock prices surging **over 15%** during Wednesday's trading. Axon exceeded profit expectations, and its outlook was more optimistic than analysts had predicted, attributing the growth to strong demand for its taser products and its cloud services segment (40% of total revenue).
