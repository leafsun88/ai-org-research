# Government Documents Show Police Disabling AI Oversight Tools

### Departments aren't reviewing or disclosing AI-written police reports — which are now being used in plea deals.

*Source: Mother Jones (Tekendra Parmar)*

In April 2024, Axon released **Draft One**, an AI-powered software package that turns body camera footage and audio into police reports. Axon's AI system is a custom variant of OpenAI's ChatGPT.

Axon says it introduced safeguards, including:
1. Deliberately inserting "obvious errors" into draft reports to encourage human review
2. Requiring a minimum level of human editing before a report can be marked complete

**But records obtained through FOIA requests almost uniformly show police departments turning these safeguards off.**

## Key Findings

**Lafayette, Indiana pilot:**
- An Axon representative told the department the new AI-disclosure header "You can easily turn this off" — and the department did so
- Captain Brian Gossard confirmed to Axon that Draft One reports had been used in **plea deals**: "I can almost guarantee reports have been used in plea deals"
- When asked to identify AI-generated reports used in plea deals, the department said it was "unable to locate any list of cases"

**Fort Collins, Colorado:**
- Police similarly disabled footers acknowledging reports were produced using AI

**Adoption issues:**
- Less than a quarter of eligible officers in Lafayette were using the software
- An Axon staffer wrote: "AI models are *very* expensive for us to operate"
- Some agencies (Anchorage Police Department) ended their trial because it didn't produce significant time savings

**Scope of use:**
- South Jordan, Utah: software used for **900+ reports** (September 2024 to April 2025), from welfare checks to kidnapping and assault cases
- Fresno Police Department: software used for **3,000+ incidents** (December 2024 to April 2025) — and expanding

## Expert Commentary

**Andrew Ferguson, GWU Law Professor:**
> "This is a willful choice by the police department — an omission that has downstream consequences. There are judges, prosecutors, defense lawyers, and defendants relying on this document, assuming it comes from the sworn memory of the police officer."

Risks include AI hallucinations effectively constituting perjury: "Let's say the AI hallucinates — which happens on occasion — then there's essentially a lie put forth to the court that can't be easily revealed."

## Legal/Regulatory Response

- **Utah:** Passed law mandating police disclose any use of AI (first state to do so)
- **California:** State Assembly considering similar bill
- **Seattle's police watchdog:** Urged local ordinance regulating departmental AI use

## Axon's Disclosure in SEC Filings

> "Unexpected failures or inaccuracies in AI-driven systems could expose our customers to operational risks, particularly in high-stakes use cases such as law enforcement or public safety... the development, adoption, integration and use of generative AI technology remains in the early stages and consequently, our AI technology may contain material defects or errors."

Also acknowledged ACLU's concerns about algorithmic bias in its own SEC filings.
