# Deep Dive: Axon Enterprise ($AXON)

### A "stunning" business

*Source: Rebound Capital, Substack*

If you have worked at a large organization, you will intuitively understand how everyone wants to "play it safe." No one wants to take the risk of suggesting a new product and then it spectacularly blowing up in their face.

This is especially true in luxury and high-risk industries, where people rarely experiment with new products. What this means is that there are a few companies that very few people have heard of, with near-monopolies in these markets.

> If I use the cheaper alternative and it fails, I get fired. If I use the industry standard and it fails, it's a tragedy, but no one can blame me for choosing the best. —Every middle manager ever!

Some fascinating examples of these include:
- **YKK Group** is the world's largest manufacturer of zippers.
- **Intuitive Surgical** is the creator of the da Vinci Surgical System, with ~90% market share in robotic surgery.

The company we are covering today operates in a very similar high-stakes environment. Just as 'YKK' is the standard for zippers, 'Taser' is a proprietary eponym — the brand's name has become the generic name for the category.

Axon is a pioneer and a leader in the development of non-lethal electric weapons and safety systems for police departments and other government agencies.

US law enforcement agencies exhibit extreme aversion to the performance risks associated with replacing a Taser, as a malfunctioning electric gun can lead to injury or death to police officers.

The result? A quasi-monopoly for Axon.

## Business Overview: Creating a Security Ecosystem

Axon's scale is underpinned by its 2024 financial performance, including revenue of $2.1B, with ~$1.3B derived from recurring revenue and ~25% Adjusted EBITDA margins.

The strength of their software segment can be gauged by the software business's **Net Revenue Retention of ~124%.**

Net Revenue Retention (NRR) measures the percentage of recurring revenue retained from an existing customer base over a set period, including expansion revenue from upsells and cross-sells, net of churn and downgrades. So with an NRR of 124%, Axon's customers are growing their spend by 24% YoY on a net basis.

Axon generates revenue from the following major categories:

- **Hardware & Devices:** The most important revenue driver here is the Taser, which accounted for ~35% of Axon's revenue in 2024. Axon also sells connected video devices and sensors for police use, as well as in-car cameras. They have recently started selling Drones for security operations.
- **Software & Services:** The management has successfully converted Axon from a single product company into a security ecosystem that sells software, productivity tools, real-time intelligence, situational awareness, and digital evidence management solutions.

The major adjacent product categories that the company has expanded into are:

- **Digital Evidence Management:** Axon Evidence is the world's largest repository of public safety data.
- **Productivity:** This consists of Axon Records and AI-based solutions, which help police officers save time by writing their reports for them and completing paperwork using AI.
- **Real Time Operations Solutions:** Axon Respond uses data from multiple sources like video feeds, 911 calls, and other area-specific intel to provide a complete picture of evolving situations.

All this has led to a growing TAM, and Axon now derives ~$1.3B in recurring revenue from its security system.

The transition reflects management's strategic vision, **successfully transforming the revenue model from selling Tasers once every few years to a high-margin, annual recurring revenue model built on bundling software and services**.

This meant that the company has consistently grown at an incredible 25% CAGR over the past 10 years, with software revenue growth touching 50% CAGR!

## What went wrong (recent drawdown context)

Even with all this, the company entered a 38% drawdown with the stock dropping 14% following its latest earnings call.

- **Tariffs:** Axon's margins got compressed due to the rising cost of hardware from suppliers in China, Taiwan, and Vietnam. The hardware gross margin dropped to 52.1% in Q3, down from 54.5% a year prior.
- **Heavy R&D expenses:** The company had a 50% YoY increase in R&D spending due to the $58 million in stock-based compensation. This spike meant that earnings growth did not keep up with revenue growth.
- **High expectations:** The near-monopoly business meant that Axon went into the earnings with a ~200 P/E ratio. This lofty valuation meant that any disappointment in results or guidance could trigger an outsized reaction.

## No Competitor

Pay attention to how Axon describes its competition in the Taser or the 'Conducted Energy Devices' segment — in their 2024 Annual Report, they are unable to name a single competitor directly in the CED segment. They just talk about other alternatives used for firearms.
