# Veeva: Durable Vertical Cloud Platform

This deep dive examines Veeva Systems, a vertical cloud platform company specializing in life sciences software solutions.

## Founding and Early Philosophy

Founder Peter Gassner established Veeva in 2007 with just $3 million in seed funding, maintaining a disciplined approach to profitability that defied conventional venture capital wisdom. Despite investor pressure to spend more, Gassner prioritized sustainable unit economics. He raised just $3 million in the seed round to found Veeva in 2007, and raised another $4 million in 2007 before going for IPO in 2013.

The company achieved positive cash flow after burning only $3 million and went public in October 2013 with a +23% GAAP operating margin — unusual for pre-IPO tech companies.

## Business Overview

Veeva provides cloud-based solutions to the life sciences industry, including:

- CRM Platform: Enables drug sample tracking, healthcare affiliation management, and mobile presentations for pharmaceutical sales representatives
- Vault: A regulated content management solution launched in 2011 for clinical trial workflows and promotional material compliance

Revenue grew from $210 million in 2013 to $2.4 billion in 2023, representing an 11x increase. Despite a 33% decline from its 2021 peak, the stock has appreciated 5x since the IPO.

## Key Themes

The analysis covers:
- Business economics and capital-light model
- Competitive dynamics against point solutions in each vertical
- Capital allocation philosophy (minimal M&A, organic growth focus)
- Valuation assumptions for long-term holders
- The unique challenge of maintaining growth in a highly penetrated vertical

## Gassner's Philosophy

The founder's approach stands out in the SaaS world:
- Profitability from Day 1 mindset
- Organic growth over acquisitions
- Product excellence as the primary competitive weapon
- Long-term customer relationships over short-term revenue optimization
- Conservative financial management with zero debt
