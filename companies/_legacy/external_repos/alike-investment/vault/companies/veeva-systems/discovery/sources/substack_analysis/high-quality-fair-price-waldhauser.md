# Veeva Stock: High-Quality Company at a Fair Price

Stefan Waldhauser presents an investment thesis for Veeva Systems, a cloud software specialist serving the pharmaceutical and biotech industries. The piece argues the company represents compelling value after significant price declines from 2021 peaks.

## Company Overview

Veeva, founded in 2007 by former Salesforce employee Peter Gassner, went public at $20 in 2013. The stock doubled on its first trading day and reached over $330 by 2021 before declining nearly 50%.

## Market Position

Veeva is the world's leading software specialist for the life sciences industry, operating within a $20 billion total addressable market with approximately 12% current penetration. The vertical market focus creates substantial competitive moats: such solutions are very expensive to replace, and the average life of a Veeva installation is over 15 years.

## Business Model

Subscription revenue comprises 80% of income, with the remainder from implementation services. The company has transitioned into a multi-product organization with Development Cloud and Commercial Cloud solutions.

## Financial Performance

FY24 revenue reached $2.364 billion with 10% growth. Key metrics:
- 72% gross margins
- Over 35% non-GAAP operating margins
- Free cash flow margins exceed 45%
- Rule-of-40 score around 60%

## Valuation Assessment

Despite a P/E ratio exceeding 50, Waldhauser considers valuations attractive on enterprise value-to-free cash flow metrics (21x) and EV-to-sales (near single digits). The combination of high-quality business characteristics with compressed multiples creates a compelling setup.

## Investment Thesis

The author expects 20% annual cash flow growth and projects share prices will exceed historical highs of $330 by 2030. The key drivers include:
- Continued penetration of the $20B TAM
- New product launches reaching maturity
- Clinical trial software adoption accelerating
- AI capabilities enhancing product value
- Strong balance sheet enabling opportunistic capital allocation
