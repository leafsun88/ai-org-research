# Veeva Systems Q1 2025 Earnings Analysis

Veeva Systems delivered strong Q1 FY2026 results, with revenue of $759.0M (+16.7% YoY) beating estimates by 4.3%. The company achieved non-GAAP EPS of $1.97, surpassing expectations by 13.2%. Management raised full-year FY2026 guidance to $3.09-$3.10B.

## Financial Highlights

### Revenue & Profitability
- Revenue grew to $759.0M, up 16.7% annually and 5.3% sequentially
- Gross margin expanded to 79.2% (non-GAAP), gaining 3.5 percentage points
- Operating margin declined to 30.8%, down 9.3 percentage points year-over-year
- Net margin improved to 30.1%, up 7.4 percentage points

### Key Metrics
- Subscription revenue reached $634.8M, accelerating 18.9% YoY
- Net new ARR hit $104.8M, surging 110.3% YoY
- Billings totaled $731M, up 16.1% annually
- CAC payback improved significantly to 7.4 months (down from 14.5 months)

## Business Segments

**Subscription Services** ($634.8M, +18.9% YoY): Gross margin of 88.1% reflects improved operational efficiency and strong demand for cloud-based solutions.

**Professional Services** ($124.3M, +6.8% YoY): Gross margin expanded to 33.6%, though sequential normalization expected.

## Product Performance

**Vault CRM:** Surpassed 80 customers, with approximately half being migrations and half new logos. Management projects reaching ~200 customers by FY2027. Four Top 20 pharma firms have publicly committed, with one rolling out to ~20,000 users globally.

**Clinical Products:** eTMF achieved penetration with 19 of Top 20 pharma customers. EDC adoption spans 9 of Top 20, though implementations remain in early ramp stages.

**Data Cloud - Crossix:** Grew 30%+ YoY, driven by usage-based pricing and broader digital media adoption.

## Strategic Initiatives

**AI Strategy:** Management estimates AI capabilities could drive 15% industry efficiency gains by 2030. Early applications include CRM agents for pre-call planning and safety agents for case creation.

**Horizontal CRM:** A dedicated team is developing enterprise CRM solutions targeting large organizations, with first customer expected by year-end FY2026.

**Operating Efficiency:** S&M, R&D, and G&A all declined as percentages of revenue, reflecting disciplined cost management.

## Market Outlook

Management acknowledged macro uncertainties including U.S. drug pricing reforms, trade tariffs, and biotech funding constraints. However, the subscription model and mission-critical product suite provide stability. No material pipeline impact observed.

## Assessment

**Strengths:** Strong revenue growth, margin expansion, exceptional ARR acceleration, and positive customer sentiment support continued momentum.

**Challenges:** Operating margin compression warrants monitoring, and EDC ramp remains slower than desired.
