# [$VEEV] Veeva Systems: Fantastic Business, Steep Multiple

## Overview

Veeva Systems is the leading cloud software provider for the life sciences industry, serving approximately 1,500 customers including roughly 47 of the top 50 global biopharmaceutical companies. Since its 2013 IPO, the company has grown revenue over 13-fold at a ~26% CAGR while maintaining best-in-class operating margins around 40% on a non-GAAP basis.

The company has invested heavily in new applications over the past four years that are reaching maturity and beginning to capture meaningful market share. These markets collectively represent roughly $1 billion opportunities. With its products now spanning the commercial and R&D functions of customers, Veeva is exceptionally positioned to deploy artificial intelligence across the life sciences industry.

## Key Investment Merits

### Extreme Stickiness

Veeva's software demonstrates exceptional customer retention due to:
- Industry-specific design addressing complex regulatory requirements across different geographies
- Mission-critical status — customers cannot operate without certain Veeva solutions
- System-of-record positioning that increases switching costs significantly
- Integrated suite design encouraging standardization across applications
- Highly trusted brand reputation, particularly in conservative areas like safety and quality

The company has not faced credible new entrants since its founding approximately 20 years ago. Competing solutions would need to be roughly twice as good to gain traction.

### Product Excellence

Founder Peter Gassner emphasized from the company's inception that each application must be "best of breed" on a standalone basis. The company invests for 4+ years to achieve market-leading products. This approach makes sales easier, improves professional services delivery, and enhances overall company morale.

### Customer Goodwill

Veeva demonstrates customer-friendly behavior through:
- No price increases on existing customers from 2007 through 2023 — sixteen years without subscription rate hikes
- Conversion to public benefit corporation status in 2020
- Purposefully understaffing sales teams to encourage long-term relationship building
- Annual contract structures requiring customers to recommit annually

### Distinguished Management

Peter Gassner has led Veeva since its founding, with senior team members averaging roughly ten years tenure. Track record includes:
- Building a ~$48 billion market cap company on just $7 million of venture capital
- Achieving 26% revenue CAGR over 11 years post-IPO
- Reaching mid-20% GAAP operating margins and low-40% non-GAAP margins

## Product Overview

### Commercial Systems

Veeva's first product was a life science-specific CRM launched around 2007 on the Salesforce platform. Today it dominates with roughly 80% market share of global pharma sales representatives.

Commercial Cloud includes: CRM, PromoMats, MedComms, Approved Email, Events Management, Align, Crossix, Service Center, Campaign Manager.

Data Cloud products include: OpenData, Link, Pulse, Compass Patient, Compass Prescriber, Compass National.

### Drug Development Systems

Beginning in 2012, Veeva introduced software for the R&D function. Development revenue represents roughly 50% of total revenue, expected to reach approximately two-thirds by 2030.

Clinical Operations Suite: eTMF (2012), CTMS (2017), Study Startup (2015), Site Connect (2020), Payments (2020), RTSM (2021), Study Training (2022), Disclosures (2023), OpenData Clinical (2023).

Clinical Data Management Suite: EDC (2016), CDB (2018), eCOA (2022).

Regulatory & Safety Suite: Submissions (2013), Submissions Archive (2015), Submissions Publishing (2017), Registrations (2015), Safety (2019), SafetyDocs (2019), Signal (2020), Safety Workbench (2023).

Quality Suite: QualityDocs (2013), QMS (2016), LIMS (2021), Training (2018), Validation Management (2021), LearnGxP (2021), Batch Release (2023).

## Five-Year Growth Opportunity

Management provided a 2030 revenue target of approximately $6 billion (roughly $4 billion from R&D, $2 billion from commercial) at the 2024 investor day. The company has previously achieved two such long-range targets ahead of schedule.

### TAM Discussion

Veeva estimates a $20 billion TAM across current application areas:
- Commercial and Clinical (Ops + Data): ~$7 billion each
- Quality: ~$4 billion
- Regulatory & Safety: ~$2 billion

### Key Product Opportunities

**EDC/CDB:** 9 of top 20 pharma committed for all new clinical trials. Contracts ramp over 3-5 years.

**RTSM and eCOA:** RTSM roughly equivalent in size to EDC. eCOA similarly sized. Both areas where competitive landscape is relatively weak.

**Safety:** Extreme conservatism in this area where system failures are catastrophic. Veeva currently serves 20% of top 20 pharmas, with momentum accelerating.

**LIMS:** Largest opportunity within Quality suite. No top 20 pharma customers yet, but essential application.

## Beyond Five-Year Opportunity

**Compass Data Products:** Largest single-product opportunity but toughest competitor (IQVIA). Veeva differentiating through superior patient data, faster daily data refresh, and integration with dominant commercial software suite.

**Commercial Reacceleration:** Migration from Salesforce to Vault platform by 2030 eliminates royalty payments and opens MedTech market (~25% of life sciences industry).

**Horizontal Applications:** Announced early-stage horizontal enterprise CRM development at November 2024 investor day. Pursuing AI-native design principles.

## AI Opportunity

### Strategic Advantages for AI
- Customer lock-in with sticky, vertical-specific systems
- Multiple integrated applications enabling AI agents to access diverse data
- Proprietary internal and external data assets
- Deep life sciences expertise
- In-house consulting capability

### AI Implementation Timeline
- 2015: CRM Suggestions (embryonic AI)
- 2019: AI in Safety product
- Oct 2021: TMF Bot for document classification
- 2023-2024: Application bots, Direct Data API, partner program
- Nov 2024: MLR Bot, CRM Bot, CRM Voice Control
- Feb 2025: Direct Data API (100x faster data access)
- Apr 2025: All applications to feature AI Agents and AI Shortcuts

### AI Market Opportunity
McKinsey estimates GenAI could produce $60-100 billion annually for life sciences. Conservative TAM expansion of $15 billion could double Veeva's current $20 billion TAM.

Management projects Veeva AI could increase life sciences efficiency by 15% over 3-5 years through 2030.
