# Veeva Update - December 2025

MBI Deep Dives discusses Veeva Systems' stock performance in 2025. Software stocks broadly have underperformed, with the IGV tech-software ETF lagging both SPY and QQQ by approximately 10-14 percentage points year-to-date. Veeva specifically has experienced a sharper decline, falling from +45% YTD in late October to just +5% YTD.

## Financial Performance vs. Estimates

The author previously modeled approximately $3.13 billion in revenue for Veeva in FY'26, while the company guided for $3.17 billion. More notably, the author projected $780 million in GAAP EBIT but observed that Veeva's last-twelve-month GAAP EBIT already reached $859 million, driven by operating margins running approximately 400 basis points higher than modeled.

Actual EBIT in FY'26 may turn out to be approximately 15% higher than estimates despite the stock remaining largely flat since the original deep dive analysis.

## Key Observations

- The stock has experienced significant volatility despite strong fundamental execution
- Operating margins have expanded faster than expected
- Revenue guidance exceeds prior estimates
- The disconnect between operational performance and stock price creates potential opportunity for long-term investors

## Context

The analysis references reviewing Veeva's October Investor Day, recent earnings calls, and sell-side transcripts to assess recent developments. The broader software sector weakness has created valuation compression across the category, with Veeva caught in the downdraft despite outperforming its own financial targets.
