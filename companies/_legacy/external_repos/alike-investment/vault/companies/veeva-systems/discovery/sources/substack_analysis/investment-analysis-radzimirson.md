# Veeva Systems (VEEV) - Investment Analysis

This investment analysis examines Veeva Systems, a subscription-based SaaS company serving the life sciences industry.

## Company Background

Veeva was founded in 2007 by Peter Gassner, an industry veteran with 18 years of experience at IBM, PeopleSoft, and Salesforce. Gassner's founding philosophy differed from typical startup culture: you don't have to burn billions to be a successful growth company.

## Business Model Strengths

Veeva operates as a vertical specialist rather than horizontal player. Two primary solutions:

1. **Commercial Cloud** - CRM built on Salesforce technology for sales and customer management
2. **Veeva Vault** - Content and data management platform

Pandemic-driven healthcare digitalization created significant tailwinds. Additionally, increasing regulations from the FDA and European Union Medical Device Regulation (EU MDR) have made compliance urgent for medical device companies.

## Competitive Advantages

- High switching costs in a heavily regulated industry
- Economy of scale from vertical focus
- Pricing power due to limited competition
- 1000+ customer base with 121-124% net revenue retention rates

## Financial Performance

- ~25% revenue CAGR
- 40% free cash flow to sales ratio
- Debt-free balance sheet
- Expanding profit margins
- Geographic diversification (60% US, 40% international)

## Risk Factors

1. **Legal dispute** - 2017 IQVIA intellectual property lawsuit
2. **Expansion execution** - New market entries (cosmetics, packaged goods) carry implementation risk
3. **Salesforce partnership** - CRM agreement expiring, though relationship appears stable

## Investment Conclusion

The author expresses confidence in Veeva's long-term trajectory, noting the company's growth story is likely just getting started. Plans to maintain the position long-term as a reliable compounder.
