# Company Deep Dive: Veeva Systems

MazwoodCap analyzes Veeva Systems (VEEV), a cloud-based software provider serving the pharmaceutical and life sciences industry. The piece opens with a retrospective on missed investment opportunities — the author spotted VEEV in 2016 during a high-return-on-capital screen but took no action, only finally investing in June 2020 at $206.78 per share.

## The Business

Veeva operates two main product divisions: Commercial Cloud (49% of revenue) for customer relationship management, and Vault (51% of revenue) for content management across commercial and R&D functions. The company was expanding into Data Cloud and other regulated industries like CPG and cosmetics.

## Financial Performance

At the time of writing, Veeva demonstrated impressive metrics: $40B market cap, $1B in revenue, 30% revenue growth. The analyst notes it was among very few $40B+ companies maintaining such growth rates. Free cash flow margins and profitability metrics were strong, with management generating efficient customer acquisition.

## Leadership & Competition

Founder Peter Gassner, previously SVP of Technology at Salesforce, led the company. IQVIA emerged as the principal competitor, though it significantly dwarfed Veeva in revenue while showing stagnant growth and inferior margins — making competitive positioning favorable for VEEV.

## Valuation Concerns

The author acknowledges steep valuation multiples relative to growth peers like Twilio, Stripe, and Paycom, questioning whether growth justifies premium pricing.

## Forward Outlook

Expansion into consumer markets and regulated industries beyond pharma offered additional growth avenues, suggesting Veeva can transition from growth juggernaut to quiet compounder. The company's vertical specialization creates deep moats that horizontal SaaS players cannot easily replicate.
