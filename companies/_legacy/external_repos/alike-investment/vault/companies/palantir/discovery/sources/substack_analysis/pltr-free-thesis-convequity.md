# Public Free PLTR Thesis - Series 1

Convequity presents a comprehensive analysis of Palantir Technologies (PLTR), positioning it as a potential future tech giant comparable to Microsoft's rise in the 1980s-90s.

## Philosophical Foundation

The analysis traces PLTR's origin to Peter Thiel's vision of combining human decision-making with machine capabilities, initially inspired by PayPal's fraud detection success. CEO Alex Karp's academic background in structural functionalism shaped the company's focus on serving institutional needs.

## Competitive Advantages

PLTR's primary strengths include infrastructure-agnostic software platforms (Gotham for government, Foundry for commercial), rapid deployment capabilities reduced from months to 14 days, and a human-machine optimization philosophy distinct from competitors pursuing pure AI solutions.

## Market Opportunity

Management estimates TAM at $119 billion ($56bn commercial, $63bn government), though analysts suggest actual opportunity may exceed $300 billion when considering broader enterprise software spending patterns.

## Financial Metrics

- FY20 Revenue: $1.07 billion (44% growth)
- 132 customers with significant expansion potential
- CAC payback period improved to 25 months in Q3 2020
- Contribution margins expanding as deployment efficiency increases

## Valuation

Two DCF approaches yielded intrinsic values of approximately $47-$49 per share, suggesting meaningful upside potential despite high current multiples.

## Risk Factors

The authors identify scaling challenges in recruiting talented FDEs and volatility in customer acquisition costs as primary risks to their thesis.
