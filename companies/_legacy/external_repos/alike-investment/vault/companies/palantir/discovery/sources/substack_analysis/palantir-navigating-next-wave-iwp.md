# Palantir (PLTR): Navigating the Next Wave – A Deep Dive Fundamental & Technical Review

Balancing explosive growth, improving margins, and an actionable trade plan amid a fresh pullback.

Palantir Technologies has captured investor attention through government contracts and AI ambitions. Following a recent 9% pullback, the analysis examines whether the uptrend remains intact, if fundamentals support current valuations, and where traders should position next.

## Key Takeaways

**Fundamentals:**
- Revenue grew 25% year-over-year to $3.8 billion in FY24
- Maintains industry-leading 75% gross margins
- Operating losses narrowing; net loss decreased approximately 40%
- Cash position of $3.5+ billion with zero net debt
- Positive free cash flow achieved in FY24
- Projections show 35% revenue growth for FY26 with ~20% EBITDA margins

**Technical Analysis:**
Weekly Ichimoku and Bollinger Bands show bullish signals, though daily and intraday charts display weakening momentum. Key support sits at $123.80 (monthly pivot convergence), with secondary support at $108.37 (100-day moving average).

**Trade Strategy:**
Scale entries near $124–$120 with stops below $115. Target resistance at $150 (weekly upper Bollinger Band) and Fibonacci extensions between $160–$172. Confirmation requires daily closes above $124 with bullish RSI and Ichimoku signals.

## Fundamental Deep Dive

The company demonstrates robust growth with both enterprise and government segments gaining traction. Adjusted EBITDA margins turned positive at approximately 10% in FY24, with forecasts predicting expansion to 20% by FY26. Operating cash flow achieved $150 million while free cash flow reached $50 million positively.

## Technical Framework

Weekly analysis reveals price above bullish Kumo clouds with Tenkan exceeding Kijun levels. Daily charts show price testing lower Bollinger Band midlines while remaining above Kijun support. Intraday oscillators display oversold conditions on 2-hour and 30-minute timeframes, suggesting potential for relief bounces.

Support hierarchy: $123.80 → $108.37 → $83.39 (200-day average)
Resistance levels: $141.74 (monthly R1) → $150 → $160

## Conclusion

Fundamental bulls lean on revenue accelerations and margin take-up while technical traders monitor for daily confirmation signals to resume the broader uptrend.
