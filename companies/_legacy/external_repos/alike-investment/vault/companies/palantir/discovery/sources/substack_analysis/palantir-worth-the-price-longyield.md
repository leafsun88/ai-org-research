# Palantir Technologies (PLTR): Worth the Price?

LongYield analyzes whether Palantir's steep valuation is justified given its recent financial performance. The company reported 70% revenue growth in Q4 2025 and guided for 61% growth in 2026, alongside a major $10 billion U.S. Army contract.

## Key Business Fundamentals

Palantir operates three primary software platforms. Gotham serves government intelligence and military applications, analyzing fragmented data sources. Foundry extends these capabilities to commercial enterprises in manufacturing, healthcare, and finance. The newer Artificial Intelligence Platform (AIP), launched in 2023, integrates large language models with Palantir's infrastructure, enabling organizations to deploy AI on proprietary data.

The commercial segment shows explosive growth, particularly through AIP boot camps — intensive training sessions converting to contracts at extraordinary rates. U.S. commercial revenue grew 137% in Q4 2025, with remaining deal value reaching $4.38 billion.

## The Rule of 40 Metric

Palantir achieved a Rule of 40 score of 127, combining approximately 70% revenue growth with 57% free cash flow margins. This represents the highest score ever recorded by a public software company, exceeding Salesforce's peak performance of roughly 50 and Snowflake's best quarter of approximately 80.

## Valuation Concerns

Trading at roughly 200x trailing earnings, Palantir stands among the most expensive software stocks. Analyst price targets range from $50 to $260, reflecting genuine disagreement about whether the company represents legitimate premium quality or speculative momentum.

## Government Business Moat

Government contracts provide significant competitive advantages. These infrastructure contracts for U.S. military and intelligence agencies create substantial switching costs measured in institutional knowledge and classified system integration. The geopolitical environment supports accelerating investment in AI-enabled defense decision systems.

## Critical Risk Factor

The Department of Government Efficiency's federal contract audits create headline risk. While core government contracts appear mission-critical and embedded, unexpected contract reviews could significantly impact a stock priced for flawless execution.

## Valuation Scenarios

At 80x earnings (still premium), Palantir would need sustained 50%+ revenue growth for 4-5 consecutive years while maintaining margins. The 2026 guidance appears on track, but years three through five remain uncertain.

## LongYield Assessment

The publication acknowledges Palantir as one of the best software businesses ever built, recommending ownership for concentrated technology portfolios based on business quality. However, the extreme valuation requires perfect execution across multiple years without competitive disruption from Microsoft, Google, or ServiceNow — conditions historically unmet by any company at 200x multiples.
