# Behind the Scenes: My 'Architect Protocol' for Analyzing Stocks (Case Study: Palantir)

Nabzdyk presents a systematic investment analysis framework called the "Architect Protocol" designed to combat confirmation bias. The article demonstrates this methodology through a comparative analysis of Palantir Technologies (PLTR) and Cognyte Software.

## The Architect Protocol Framework

The protocol operates in four phases:

**Phase 0: The Hunt** - The analyst independently selects investment targets without AI suggestions, ensuring conviction originates from personal market observation.

**Phase 1: The Briefing** - AI generates "Owner's Questions" that bypass marketing narratives and examine core business metrics like margins and capital allocation.

**Phase 2: The Gauntlet** - Deep work using primary documents (10-K, 10-Q filings). Analysts manually calculate financial metrics rather than relying on AI summaries.

**Phase 3: The Sparring Match** - Adversarial testing where AI assumes the role of devil's advocate, attacking the preliminary thesis with counter-arguments.

**Phase 4: Invalidation Rules** - Analysts define specific numerical "kill conditions" that trigger exits, removing emotion from selling decisions.

## Key Findings on Palantir

Nabzdyk's analysis revealed:

- **Gross Margin:** 80.24% (supporting software monopoly characteristics)
- **Operating Margin:** 10.83%
- **Rule of 40 Score:** 62.17 (elite efficiency)
- **Stock-Based Compensation:** 24.13% of revenue — a "hidden cost" that artificially inflates cash flow metrics

### Critical Issue: SBC Manipulation

The analysis identified that Stock-Based Compensation added back to operating cash flow masks true economic costs. When treated as real expense, Palantir's free cash flow margin drops from 40% to 16% — substantially changing the investment thesis.

### Moat Assessment

Using proprietary NabzdykRatings:
- **Moat Score:** 4.9/10 (Moderate)
- **Quality Score:** 5.8/10 (Good)

While Palantir demonstrates product lock-in and switching costs, high customer acquisition costs prevent elite-level ratings.

## The Verdict

Despite identifying superior business fundamentals compared to Cognyte, Nabzdyk concluded: **AVOID**

**Rationale:**
- DCF valuation: Fair value $21 vs. current price $176
- Current valuation demands perfection
- Margin of safety absent
- Philosophy prioritizes discipline over chasing momentum

The analyst explicitly stated his willingness to miss the opportunity rather than chase a stock without a margin of safety.

## Core Insight

The article demonstrates that rigorous analysis can validate a company's moat while simultaneously rejecting the investment opportunity based on valuation discipline — a nuanced conclusion more valuable than a simple buy/sell recommendation.
