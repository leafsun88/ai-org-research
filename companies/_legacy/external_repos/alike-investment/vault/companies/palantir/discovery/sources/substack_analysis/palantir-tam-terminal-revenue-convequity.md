# Notes - Palantir's TAM, Terminal Revenue Potential, & Valuation

This analysis examines Palantir Technologies through the lens of AI agents, estimating the company's total addressable market and terminal revenue potential. The research reconciles top-down TAM calculations with traditional bottom-up valuation approaches.

## Key Findings

**Current Performance:** Palantir achieved $828 million in 4Q24 revenue, beating guidance by $59 million. US commercial revenue surged 64% year-over-year with 20% quarter-over-quarter growth, while government revenue expanded 45% annually.

**Strategic Advantage:** The company's core competitive edge lies in building customized ontologies for each enterprise client. These structured knowledge frameworks enable effective enterprise LLM implementation, which remains challenging across the industry.

**Real-World Impact:** PLTR focuses on deep relationships with major enterprises rather than broad customer acquisition. The analysis cites examples like manufacturing optimization (Warp Speed) and insurance processes reduced from two weeks to three hours through 78 AI agents.

## TAM Analysis

The researchers employed two methodologies:

**Method 1 - Bottom-Up:** Modeling a baseline 200-employee company, they calculated that AI agents could generate $36 million in additional annual profit through 3x productivity gains in non-revenue roles and 2x gains in revenue-generating positions. Scaling across 500,000 mid-sized companies globally yields a $4.5 trillion TAM.

**Method 2 - Simplified Approach:** Using 1 billion global knowledge workers averaging $60,000 in salary plus overhead, with agents reducing labor costs by 40%, the calculation suggests $6 trillion in value creation, of which vendors capture $1.5 trillion.

**Terminal Revenue:** If Palantir captures 5% of the $5 trillion estimated TAM, terminal revenue could reach approximately $250 billion, plus additional tens of billions from non-agent software.

## Valuation Framework

The DCF model employs a 25-year forecast period rather than the standard 15-year approach, reflecting the unprecedented scale of opportunity. Key assumptions include:

- FY27-FY30 CAGR of 45% for continued growth acceleration
- US Commercial revenue reaching $19 billion by FY30, growing at 40%
- FCF margins expanding from 40% (FY24) to 50% (FY49)
- Terminal growth rate of 3% by year 25 (FY49)

**Valuation Result:** The analysis produces an intrinsic value of $186 per share versus approximately $106 current price, implying 76% upside.

## Critical Perspective

The authors explicitly note this represents an academic exploration rather than a base-case recommendation. They acknowledge that most investors instinctively apply conventional growth decay models, and cite historical examples like Microsoft and NVIDIA where markets initially underestimated long-term potential. However, they emphasize uncertainty while arguing that if AI agents do become a ubiquitous enterprise necessity and Palantir maintains technical leadership, such revenue levels become plausible rather than absurd.
