Over three years have passed since I first wrote about Duolingo stock on my blog in fall 2022 and added it to my portfolio (read more here; originally published in German only). Like most stocks from the 2021 IPO class, Duolingo stock fell well below its initial offering price of $102 in 2022. At the time of my initial post, Duolingo shares were trading at $73, more than 60% below their September 2021 peak of $200, having lost two-thirds of their value.

A lot has happened since I first invested. The company performed fantastically, increasing its revenue from $250 million to over $1 billion between 2021 and 2025.

Consequently, Duolingo shares surged by over 600% to reach $500 between January 2023 and May 2025!

Amid the euphoria surrounding Duolingo’s impressive Q1 2025 results, I urged caution. My conclusion at the time was that:

“Due to its high valuation, Duolingo is one of the most speculative stocks in my portfolio. Since I bought it three years ago, the share price has more than quintupled… I would advise you to wait for a major pullback before investing, as this is a highly volatile stock. In a negative market environment, a stock like this can quickly lose half its value, even if the company continues to perform well. You should therefore consider taking (partial) profits from time to time if you have been a proud Duolingo shareholder for years, as I have.”

Unfortunately, my concerns about the high valuation have proven to be true. Duolingo shares have really taken a beating in recent months, losing about two-thirds of their value since their May peak!

It’s time to take another close look and answer the question of whether Duolingo shares are worth buying again.

Could Duolingo’s legendary share price rise repeat itself?

In this article, I explore the reasons behind the stock’s significant value loss and my approach to managing my diminished Duolingo position, which was initially reduced through profit-taking and subsequently impacted by price declines.
