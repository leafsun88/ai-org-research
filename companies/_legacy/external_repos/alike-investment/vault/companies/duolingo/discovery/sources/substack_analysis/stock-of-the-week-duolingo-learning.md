This week’s spotlight is on Duolingo. Despite surpassing $1B in revenue and crushing Q4 expectations, the stock plummeted over 20% following its latest report as management announced a deliberate pivot to sacrifice short-term profits for long-term scale. CEO Luis von Ahn is betting $50M in foregone revenue that a freer experience will double his user base to 100M daily actives by 2028. Is this a visionary move to widen a behavioral moat or a defensive surrender to the rising tide of AI competitors? Does a 70% drawdown from ATH offer a generational entry point for quality investors or are we looking at a classic value trap? Let’s find out!

Recent news

Duolingo exceeded expectations in its Q4 2025 by reporting $283M in revenue (up 35% YoY) and reaching a record 52.7M daily active users

The company officially surpassed $1B in revenue for the first time, signaling its successful transition into a large-scale education platform

Management issued a disappointing 2026 forecast, guiding for revenue growth of only 15% to 18% and bookings growth of just 10% to 12%

Profitability is expected to take a temporary hit as adjusted EBITDA margins are projected to contract from nearly 30% in 2025 down to approximately 25% in 2026

A major strategic pivot is underway to reach 100M daily active users by 2028, even if it requires sacrificing short-term revenue by removing monetization friction for free users

The company plans to move high-end AI features, such as Video Call with Lily, to lower-priced tiers to boost engagement and defend against AI-native competitors

Significant investments are being channeled into non-language subjects like math, music or chess to turn the app into a multi-subject learning hub

The board authorized a $400M share repurchase program to return capital to shareholders and signal confidence during the recent stock price weakness

The stock price experienced a massive drawdown, falling over 20% in late February to hit a low near $101, as investors recalculated the company’s valuation under slower growth expectations

Sales and marketing expenses are projected to outpace revenue growth in 2026 as the company ramps up spending to defend its market share against AI-native competitors and support its aggressive expansion into new subjects like Math and Music

Marketing efficiency is showing signs of pressure as sales and marketing expenses grew by nearly 48% in 2025 (outpacing revenue growth), suggesting that the company is having to spend more heavily to acquire users in mature markets
