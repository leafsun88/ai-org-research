# Andrew Bialecki — Founder/CEO of Klaviyo

## Background
- Co-founder and co-CEO of Klaviyo (co-CEO since Jan 2026, previously sole CEO)
- Technical founder background
- Built Klaviyo from bootstrap to $10B+ public company, burning only ~$15M in venture capital before IPO
- Based in Boston — deliberately built company outside Silicon Valley

## Leadership Philosophy

### Capital Efficiency & Patience
- Deliberately delayed fundraising to maintain control and build capital-efficient business
- Philosophy: build sustainable business, not chase rapid growth at any cost
- Only raised minimal VC before reaching scale — rare in SaaS

### Product-First Thinking
- Believes in building product so good customers can't help but talk about it (word-of-mouth driven)
- Emphasizes data-driven decision making over following industry trends
- Now fully focused on AI vision and AI-first products (since Jan 2026 co-CEO split)

### Organizational Views
- Values lean teams that move quickly regardless of company size
- Emphasizes pain of siloed leadership; advocates tightly integrated product and tech
- Cultivates entrepreneurship inside Klaviyo
- Encourages AI-first mindset across entire organization
- Curious about reducing friction and moving faster in complex environments

### Culture & Talent
- Focuses on finding the best talent
- Values curiosity and active builders (especially in AI)
- Scaling culture is a deliberate priority

## Co-CEO Structure (Dec 2025 Announcement)

### Division of Responsibilities
- **Bialecki**: AI vision, AI-first product development (technical/product side)
- **Chano Fernandez**: Go-to-market, operations, general administration (business/scaling side)

### Rationale
- Company growing so fast that single CEO model was becoming a bottleneck
- Bialecki wanted to go 100% deep on AI product strategy
- Fernandez brings enterprise scaling experience (ex-Workday co-CEO, ex-SAP)
- Fernandez had been on Klaviyo board for 2 years, served as Interim Executive Officer
