## 3. Competitive Landscape: Mobvista vs. AppLovin vs. Unity

### Market Position
- AppLovin: Dominant player, $200B+ market cap, closed ecosystem (MAX + Adjust + AXON)
- Unity Ads: Struggling after ironSource merger integration issues
- Mobvista/Mintegral: Rising challenger, strong in Asia and emerging markets
- Other competitors: Liftoff, ironSource (now Unity), Google AdMob

### Mobvista's Competitive Edge
- Stronger presence in China and Asia-Pacific markets
- Lower cost structure as China-based operation
- AI-powered smart bidding competing on performance metrics
- Topped AppsFlyer's Performance Index (16th edition)

### Competitive Risks
- AppLovin's first-party data advantage in privacy era
- Scale gap: AppLovin at ~$200B market cap vs. Mobvista much smaller
- Unity still has deep game engine integration advantage
- Google/Meta control most of the overall mobile ad market

### Market Context
- Global mobile ad market: ~$400-500B (2024 estimate)
- Mobile gaming ad spend increasingly concentrated in top platforms
- Privacy regulations (ATT, GDPR) reshaping competitive dynamics

## 4. Controversies & Risks

### Mintegral Sale Saga (2024-2025)
- Dec 2024: Mobvista explored selling Mintegral, Bain Capital showed interest
- Stock surged ~190% in 2024 on sale speculation
- Feb 2025: Sale stalled on valuation gap with Bain Capital
- Other interested parties: Affirma Capital, CDH Investments
- Nov 2025: Sale halted entirely
- Shares plunged on news of politically motivated potential asset sales

### Regulatory / Geopolitical Risk
- US executive orders targeting Chinese tech companies processing personal data
- Company explicitly cited regulatory risk as reason for considering divestiture
- China-US tensions creating structural uncertainty for cross-border ad platforms
- Revenue head in US = direct exposure to US regulatory actions

### SDK / Technical Issues
- Mintegral joined IAB Europe's Transparency and Consent Framework (TCF V2.2)
- Updated SDKs to support Google User Messaging Platform (UMP)
- No major SDK controversies found in 2025-2026 (earlier 2020 SDK data collection controversy existed)
