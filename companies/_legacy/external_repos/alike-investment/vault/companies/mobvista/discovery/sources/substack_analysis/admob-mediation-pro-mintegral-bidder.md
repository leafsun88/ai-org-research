# What Is AdMob Mediation Pro and Who Is It For?

## Context

AdMob Mediation Pro was released by Google in early 2025 to address the needs of large, more sophisticated publishers. This article reviews its features from six months of hands-on experience.

## Key Features

### User Value Segmentation

Historically, mobile mediation platforms segmented users by country. AdMob Mediation Pro's segmentation tool allows segmenting users by eCPM from their first impression, enabling a mediation setup that moves away from traditional geo-based structure.

Example mediation group structure:
- $60 - $MAX
- $20 - $70
- $10 - $25
- $5 - $12
- $min - $7
- Backfill

Within each mediation group, you can enable both header bidding partners and the same networks running on waterfall price floors. For example, you can run the Mintegral bidder alongside multiple Mintegral waterfall instances. This significantly increases the likelihood of maintaining higher average eCPMs.

### Unlimited AdMob Waterfall Placements

When a publisher relies exclusively on header bidders, eCPMs tend to decline over time. Publishers can counter this by introducing bid floors or reintroducing waterfall placements. AdMob Mediation Pro allows configuring unlimited AdMob waterfall placements within user value segment mediation groups.

### Improved Account Management

AdMob Mediation Pro typically includes dedicated account managers who oversee fewer publishers, giving them more time to be proactive and hands-on.

## Practical Considerations

Setting up for a single Android app has never taken less than seven hours of continuous work. A publisher typically needs at least six mediation groups segmented by eCPM, each with five to ten bidders alongside ten to fifteen waterfall placements.

## Results

When executed correctly with hybrid setups including both bidders and waterfall placements from at least five networks, this setup has shown uplifts in the range of ten to fifteen percent ADARPDAU, particularly on video inventory.

## Mintegral Relevance

Mintegral is cited as a key bidder network in the hybrid mediation setup, demonstrating its importance in the mobile ad monetization ecosystem alongside Google's own demand.
