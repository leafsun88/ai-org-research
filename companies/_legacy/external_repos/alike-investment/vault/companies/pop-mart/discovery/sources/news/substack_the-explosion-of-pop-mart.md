Let’s discuss Pop Mart, and the Labubu craze that’s taken the internet by storm!

A couple of weeks ago, I did a Q+A on my Instagram and someone asked me what I thought of Labubus. My answer was that I didn’t understand the trend of putting them on your birkin or purse, as I don’t like bag charms, especially not large ones! However, I do understand the love of the toy itself, and I will explain why.

I’ve been collecting toys, blind boxes, and plushies for two decades. It started with Kidrobot blind boxes from Urban Outfitters in the mid 2000’s. If you don’t know, Kidrobot is a company that makes toys and plushies similar to Pop Mart. They had different characters and series, but arguably their most popular series at the time was called Yummy World, which consisted of characters in the form of different foods. They had blind boxes and plushies, and I was OBSESSED. They still make these today, but they’ve changed them over the years. I’ve also collected Bearbricks, Kaws, Sanrio, you name it. My dad lived in Asia for 6 years, so you can imagine the toys I brought home after every trip!

When I lived in New York, my best friend and I would go to little Japanese grocery stores to try and find more blind boxes, or a cute keychain! I had to stop buying larger plushies because I ran out of places to store them. They ended up taking over my whole bed, and now, sadly, they sit in boxes, or in my closet. I still sleep with my Kidrobot black and white cookie plush that I’ve had since 2013, as well as a Gudetama plush that fits perfectly in the crook of my arm. At one point, I had all of my collectibles and figurines on display on shelves. However, I have a cat now, so I’m unable to do that. If you have a cat, you’ll understand! They knock everything off shelves and tables.

*My black and white cookie plush when I first bought it in 2013. It looks much sadder now, lol.

The amount of joy that buying a new toy brings is unmatched. I get why kids throw tantrums if they can’t get a new toy at the store (kidding…kind of). Buying a new blind box gives you something to hope for, and something to look forward to! Usually, there is a character that you are hoping to pull, and opening a blind box to see that you actually got the one you wanted feels like a drug! It sounds silly, but don’t knock it until you try it.

*This was the one I wanted, yay! (you can see my cat already trying to steal it)

*This was my number two choice, so overall I was still very happy with this pull!

*Sea Salt Coconut and Soymilk! My top two choices from the macaron series. 

I think, given the uncertainty of the world right now, especially in this country, having something to look forward to or hope for, even if it’s small, is essential. We’ve gone back to basics in the form of toys. We’re literally so excited to open the LABUBU we’ve been hoping for, or the CRYBABY that we take with us everywhere we go. (I’m still hoping for She’s Alice!) I won’t be putting my Pop Mart toys on my purse as a bag charm, but I will put them in my purse! I will take them with me to get coffee, or run an errand, or even put them in my car, because why not? They are cute, and they spark joy. I think Marie Kondo would approve!
