# Pop Mart 2025 Annual Results & Stock Analysis

## 2025 Financial Highlights

Pop Mart International Group (9992.HK) delivered extraordinary 2025 results:
- **Revenue**: 37.12 billion yuan ($5.37B), +184.7% YoY — first time crossing 30B yuan threshold
- **Net profit**: 13.08 billion yuan ($1.89B), +284.5% YoY
- **Gross margin**: 72.1%, historical high
- **Adjusted net profit**: 13.08 billion yuan, +284.5%

## LABUBU IP Dominance

THE MONSTERS series (anchored by LABUBU):
- Revenue: 14.16 billion yuan, 38.1% of total sales (+365.7% YoY)
- LABUBU units exceeded 100 million sold annually
- Plush toys grew +560.6% YoY, becoming the largest product category for the first time
- IP concentration rose from 23% (2024) to 38.1% (2025)

## Stock Market Reaction

Despite stellar financials, the stock experienced severe selling:
- Share price dropped 22.5% to HK$168.30 on March 25, 2026 — steepest since April 2025
- Market cap erased approximately HK$89 billion ($11.4B) over two consecutive trading days
- HK$65.57 billion evaporated in a single session

## Analyst Views

**Morningstar (Jeff Zhang)**:
- 2025 revenue and earnings likely missed market consensus estimates
- Material Q4 slowdown amplified concerns about IP durability
- Dividend payout reduction from 35% to 25% viewed negatively
- Conservative 2026 guidance weighs on sentiment

**Morgan Stanley**:
- Modeled ~13% Labubu sales growth for 2026
- 2026 revenue forecast: 47.88 billion yuan
- 2026 net income forecast: 15.42 billion yuan

## Q4 2025 Deceleration Signals

- Q4 revenue: ~11 billion yuan, down 9.5% QoQ from Q3
- Q4 YoY growth: ~120%, significant deceleration from Q3's 245-250%
- Sequential decline raised structural concerns beyond seasonal factors

## Other IPs Performance

- **Molly**: 2.9 billion yuan, far below market expectation of 4.6 billion yuan
- **Crybaby**: Also missed expectations
- **Star People (星星人)**: Bright spot — grew from 120M to 2.06B yuan
- New IPs (SUPER TUTU, After-School Merodi, KeyA): Lukewarm reception in e-commerce

## Secondary Market Weakness

LABUBU collectible prices declining in secondary markets:
- Some regular editions falling below retail prices
- Suggests cooling of speculative demand

## 2026 Strategic Initiatives

1. **Global expansion**: European HQ in London; Americas store count from 64 to 100+
2. **New markets**: Middle East, South Asia, South America
3. **Entertainment**: Sony Pictures partnership for LABUBU film (directed by Paul King)
4. **Diversification**: Pop Mart City Park Phase 1.5 (summer 2026), home appliances (April 2026), POP BAKERY dessert brand
5. **Growth target**: CEO Wang Ning guided "no less than 20%" growth for 2026

## Balance Sheet

- Cash position: Over 17 billion yuan available
- Bank debt: Zero
- Resources adequate for global expansion and IP development

## Key Investment Questions

1. Can Pop Mart incubate next 10B-yuan IP franchise after LABUBU's natural decline?
2. Will global store expansion drive incremental growth or cannibalize online?
3. Does the shift from 185% to 20% growth guidance signal structural slowdown?
4. How sustainable is the 72.1% gross margin as product mix shifts?
