# Pop Mart Goes Global: The Cult of Labubu

**Author:** Arya / Genuine Impact | **Date:** 2025-05-28

## International Expansion (2024 Data)

- Americas revenue: +900% YoY
- Europe revenue: +600% YoY
- International markets: ~40% of revenue, projected 65% by 2027 (JPM)
- 2024 total revenue: ~$1.8B, gross margins 66.8%
- Market cap reached HK$313.2B (~$40B USD) on May 27, 2025

## IP Platform Model (Not Just a Toy Company)

Three core mechanisms:
1. **IP Creation**: Discovers independent artists, commercializes characters
2. **IP Monetization**: Blind boxes, retail, licensing revenue streams
3. **IP Operation**: Sustained fandom through narrative development

Key IP performance:
- Molly: RMB 2.09B (~$290M) in 2024
- The Monsters (Labubu): projected growth from RMB 3B (2024) → RMB 14B (2027)

## Distribution Innovation

- Roboshops (automated vending): RMB 698M (~$96M) in 2024
- Offline China channels: RMB 3.83B (~$530M)
- Global pop-up retail and flagships

## Cultural Impact

- UK in-store Labubu sales paused due to crowd control
- Chinese customs launched smuggling crackdowns on resellers
- Labubu eclipsed Hello Kitty in search volume

## Strategic Differentiation vs Disney

Unlike Disney's high-stakes content pipelines, Pop Mart uses agile small-scale blind box trials + rapid feedback loops. This flywheel attracts emerging artists, creating self-reinforcing competitive moat.

## Institutional Validation

- JPMorgan: Overweight rating, 152% YoY overseas growth projected for 2025
- Adjusted net profit: RMB 3.4B (~$470M), up 185.9%
