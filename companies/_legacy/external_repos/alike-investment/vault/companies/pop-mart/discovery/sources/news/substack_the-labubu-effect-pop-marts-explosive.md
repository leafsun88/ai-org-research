What if I told you a fuzzy monster with sharp teeth just turned a $5 billion toy company into a $37 billion empire?

Sounds ridiculous, right?

But Pop Mart International Group’s market capitalization hit $37.06 billion in January 2026. Six years earlier? Just $5 billion.

Meet Labubu: a sharp-toothed, fuzzy creature that became the difference between mediocrity and meteoric growth.

This isn’t some feel-good story about viral marketing or getting lucky with influencers. This is a hard-nosed case study in how a single product line can transform a company’s entire trajectory. But only when the conditions align perfectly. And only if you don’t screw up the execution.

The Breaking Point

Here’s where things get wild.

In the first half of 2025 alone, Pop Mart’s revenue reached RMB 13.7 billion. That’s more than the company’s entire 2024 revenue. In just six months. Net profit? Up 350% year-over-year.

And here’s the kicker: Labubu collectible toys now represent an estimated 60-70% of total revenue.

The numbers are almost absurd. By mid-2025, Labubu sales had exploded 1,200% from their 2023 baseline. Production capacity? Scaled from 300,000 units per month in early 2024 to 10 million units by 2025.

Let’s put this in perspective:

Pre-Labubu revenue in 2018: $74.81 million

Post-Labubu peak revenue in 2025: $2.50 billion annualized

That’s 33x growth in six years. From one product line.

What Led to This Moment

Pop Mart partnered with Labubu creator Kasing Lung in 2019. The initial product line launched quietly. Just another niche collectible in Pop Mart’s growing portfolio.
