# Is The Trade Desk's Growth Engine Starting to Stall?

Sergey's analysis raises concerns about decelerating momentum at The Trade Desk ($TTD), a leading independent demand-side platform in programmatic advertising.

## Revenue Deceleration Pattern

Recent quarters show a concerning trend. After Q4 2024 missed estimates, Q1 2025 rebounded with a strong 7.1% beat. However, Q2 2025 delivered only a 1.8% beat with cautious forward guidance. The author warns that if Q3 is surpassed by the same margin as Q2, reported growth could slow to +16.2%, well below prior momentum.

## Valuation Position

The company trades at a forward EV/Sales multiple of 6.7, below its median of 14.3. Despite growth deceleration concerns, it maintains a forward P/E of 23.7 with a PEG ratio of 1.2, suggesting reasonable valuation relative to expectations.

Key valuation metrics:
- EPS growth forecast for 2026: 20.1%
- P/E ratio: 25.5
- PEG ratio: 1.2
- Revenue growth forecast: +18.2% for 2025, +16.8% for 2026

## Operational Strengths

- Maintains 95%+ customer retention across 27 consecutive quarters
- Recognized leader by Forrester Wave, IDC MarketScape, and Frost Radar
- Strong balance sheet with $1.68 billion in cash, minimal debt
- Kokai platform showing 20+ point performance improvements
- Connected TV continues to be the fastest-growing segment
- International expansion outpacing domestic growth

## Key Risk

Management's conservative guidance raises questions about actual underlying growth sustainability. The critical factor remains whether Q3 results demonstrate stabilizing momentum or confirm structural slowdown fears.

## Beat-and-Raise Pattern Analysis

Historical pattern shows TTD typically beats guidance by 3-5%. Recent quarters:
- Q4 2024: First miss in 33 quarters (-2.1%)
- Q1 2025: Strong recovery (+7.1% beat)
- Q2 2025: Modest beat (+1.8%)
- Q3 2025 guidance: Conservative, implying 16-17% growth

The narrowing beat margin is the primary concern for growth-oriented investors. If the company cannot return to consistent 20%+ growth beats, the stock's premium multiple may be permanently impaired.
