# The Trade Desk ($TTD) - Should You Buy the 39% Dip or is the Thesis Falling Apart?

Following Trade Desk's Q2 earnings release, shares plummeted 39% despite results that matched consensus estimates. The author, who holds TTD as a significant portfolio position, analyzes whether this sell-off is justified or represents a buying opportunity.

## Key Financial Results

TTD reported $694 million in Q2 revenue, reflecting 19% year-over-year growth — the company's first quarter below 20% growth in its public history. Adjusted EBITDA reached $271 million with a 39% margin, down approximately 200 basis points year-over-year. Net income grew only 3% to $203 million, while free cash flow improved to $117 million (17% margin).

## Long-Term Investment Thesis

The author remains bullish on several structural factors:

1. **Open Internet Advantage:** TTD operates as an independent demand-side platform serving the open internet against walled gardens like Meta and Google, providing advertisers with transparency, flexibility, and cross-channel reach.

2. **Streaming Acceleration:** Connected TV advertising continues accelerating, with Nielsen data showing streaming TV viewing up 71% over four years while traditional broadcasts declined 39%.

3. **Technological Innovation:** Platform upgrades like Kokai demonstrate differentiation, showing improvements including 43% improvement in reaching target audiences and 73% improvement in cost per acquisition.

4. **Financial Health:** TTD maintains $1.7 billion in cash with virtually no debt, enabling continued share buybacks.

## Concerns and Headwinds

### Competitive Pressures
Amazon's expanding DSP capabilities represent mounting threats through vertical integration and direct advertising capabilities.

### Macro Uncertainty
Management guidance assumes a stable environment, but tariff policies create unpredictability affecting large multinational advertisers.

### Valuation Reset
While shares dropped from 50x to 31x forward earnings, the author adjusted FY25 revenue growth expectations downward to 17%, with EPS growing only 5% annually.

## Organizational Changes

The sudden CFO transition — Laura Schenkein departing in favor of Alex Kayyal — initially spooked investors. However, the author views this as manageable given Kayyal's experience at Salesforce.

The extension of TTD's dual-class share structure preserving founder Jeff Green's control is characterized as potentially reassuring for long-term strategy execution.

## Valuation and Forward Outlook

At 31x earnings with expected high-teens to low-twenties growth, the author calculates a PEG ratio of 1.24x — 55% below TTD's five-year average. Assuming a conservative 35x forward multiple by end-2027, the target price stands at $93, suggesting 24% annualized returns.

## Investment Conclusion

The author rates TTD as a Buy below $66 with a 2027 target of $93. The assessment emphasizes that this is a massive overreaction by the market, driven by temporary macro headwinds rather than fundamental deterioration. Historical patterns show TTD shares recovering after similar 30-40% corrections when lofty expectations prove temporarily unmet.
