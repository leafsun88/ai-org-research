# The Trade Desk in 2026: Putting the Puzzle Pieces Together for 20%+ Growth

Matthew Blake, Founder and CIO of Sycamore Capital Management, argues that The Trade Desk's recent stock decline is unwarranted, contending that Wall Street analysts have misdiagnosed the company's 2025 challenges and wrongly attribute them to competitive threats from Amazon.

## On Competition

Blake contends that Amazon's primary advertising efforts compete with Google Search rather than TTD's demand-side platform. He notes that of Amazon's ~$70 billion advertising revenue, approximately 95% comes from sponsored listings, with only low single-digit percentages directed toward their DSP. The overlap with TTD's open internet focus is minimal.

## Temporary Headwinds

The analyst identifies three primary causes for 2025's growth deceleration to ~18-19%, rather than structural competitive decline:

1. A problematic Kokai platform transition in late 2024
2. Organizational restructuring that damaged near-term execution
3. Macro uncertainty and tariff concerns affecting major advertisers' spending

## Management Confidence

TTD returned approximately $968 million to shareholders through buybacks in 2025 YTD, representing roughly 3.5% of outstanding shares. Blake interprets aggressive capital returns as management's confidence in undervaluation.

## 2026 Growth Catalysts

Blake projects a re-acceleration to 20%+ growth driven by:

1. **Completed Technology Transitions:** Kokai migration fully complete, performance improvements compounding
2. **New JBPs:** 80 new Joint Business Plans in the pipeline, driving enterprise adoption
3. **International Expansion:** Currently 13% of revenue versus 60% of TAM — massive runway
4. **New Product Innovations:** Reaching market maturity and contributing to revenue
5. **Easing Macro Conditions:** Easier year-over-year comparisons and stabilizing ad budgets

## Valuation

Blake values the stock at 20x forward earnings and 14x adjusted EBITDA, suggesting meaningful upside potential if growth accelerates as predicted. At current prices, the risk/reward is highly asymmetric in favor of long-term investors.

## Key Argument: Amazon Is Not the Threat

The most important insight in Blake's analysis is the detailed breakdown of Amazon's advertising business. He demonstrates that Amazon's DSP (which directly competes with TTD) represents only a small fraction of Amazon's total ad revenue. The bulk of Amazon's advertising comes from search-based sponsored product listings on Amazon.com, which do not compete with TTD's open internet display, CTV, and audio advertising.

This distinction is critical because many Wall Street analysts have cited Amazon as a primary competitive threat to TTD, potentially leading to mispricing of the stock.
