# TTD -73% from ATH: What the Market Is Missing

Sergey's deep dive analysis examines The Trade Desk (TTD), a programmatic advertising platform that has experienced a significant valuation decline. Despite surface-level concerns about slowing growth and margin compression, the author argues the market is overlooking meaningful operational strengths.

## Valuation & Growth

The stock trades at a forward EV/Sales multiple of 5.3, well below its historical median of 14.1. While reported Q3 revenue growth decelerated to 17.7% YoY (from 27.4% in Q3 2024), excluding political advertising reveals underlying growth stabilization at approximately 22%.

## Economic Moat

TTD maintains a strong competitive moat built on:

- Economies of scale with EBITDA margins above 40%
- Network effects through data aggregation and AI optimization
- Strong brand positioning as the neutral alternative to walled gardens
- High switching costs with 95%+ customer retention

## Business Performance

- Connected TV represents ~50% of revenue and grows faster than company average
- Kokai AI platform adopted by ~85% of clients, delivering 26% better CPA metrics
- 180 active Joint Business Plans (JBPs) drive roughly 50% of total revenue
- International growth outpaced North America for 11 consecutive quarters

## Financial Health

- GAAP net income profitable with improving margins
- $1.445 billion in cash versus $376 million debt (untapped revolver)
- $2 billion in share buybacks since 2023
- Stock-based compensation at 16% of revenue (SaaS industry average)

## Market Leadership

The Trade Desk ranks as a leader across major industry assessments: Forrester Wave, IDC MarketScape, and Frost Radar for DSPs.

## What the Market Is Missing

The author argues several factors are being underappreciated:

1. Political advertising created a tough comp in Q3/Q4 — underlying organic growth is stronger than headline numbers suggest
2. CTV penetration is still in early innings with streaming ad tiers expanding
3. Kokai's performance improvements create a compounding flywheel effect
4. International expansion represents a massive untapped opportunity
5. The balance sheet provides significant flexibility for buybacks and investments

## Investment Decision

Sergey increased his TTD position to 2.5% of portfolio, viewing the 73% decline as a valuation reset opportunity. The critical variable will be whether the company continues exceeding guidance on underlying revenue growth (excluding political advertising).
