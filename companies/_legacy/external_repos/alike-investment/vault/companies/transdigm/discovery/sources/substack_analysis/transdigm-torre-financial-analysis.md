# Market, Earnings, and TransDigm Group (TDG)

## Market Overview

The major U.S. stock indices experienced choppy trading over the two-week period, with trade and tariff policy uncertainty affecting specific sectors. Year-to-date performance shows the Nasdaq leading at +11.53%, followed by the S&P 500 at +9.88% and the Dow Jones at +7.06%.

Key economic indicators included a PCE price index reading of 2.9% for July, remaining above the Federal Reserve's 2% target. Federal Reserve Chairman Jerome Powell acknowledged risks of overtightening monetary policy for the first time at Jackson Hole.

## TransDigm Group (TDG) Analysis

### Company Overview

TransDigm manufactures engineered aerospace components with over 115 distinct business units. Founded in 1993 through a leveraged buyout, the company went public in 2006 and pursues private-equity-like returns for shareholders.

### Business Model Strengths

The company operates across three segments:
- Power & Control (~50% of sales)
- Airframe (~48% of sales)
- Non-aviation (~2% of sales)

A critical competitive advantage lies in being the sole-source supplier for many proprietary components. This position grants significant pricing power in the lucrative aftermarket segment, which comprises 56% of revenue but approximately 75% of EBITDA. The aftermarket business proves essential because grounded commercial aircraft — worth roughly $100 million — can cost up to $150,000 daily in expenses and lost revenue.

### Financial Performance

- Revenue CAGR since 2006: 18%
- TTM revenue growth: 13% year-over-year
- Gross margin: 60%
- EBITDA margin: 51%
- Return on Invested Capital (ROIC): 19.2%
- Net debt: ~$21 billion (4.9x EBITDA)

### Valuation & Outlook

Shares trade at 19.8x EV/NTM EBITDA with a free cash flow yield of 1.9%. The significant gap between EBITDA yield (5%) and FCF yield reflects substantial interest payments on the company's leveraged capital structure.

Analyst projections anticipate sales growth of 7-10% CAGR and EBITDA growth of 8-12% CAGR. Depending on growth assumptions and exit multiples, potential five-year annualized returns range from approximately 9% to 17%.

TransDigm addresses only 3% of its addressable $57 billion maintenance market opportunity, suggesting considerable growth potential. The company benefits from secular tailwinds including aircraft delivery backlogs and aging fleets requiring increased maintenance.

Recent stock weakness stemmed from management guidance indicating slower-than-expected OEM sales, primarily due to delays from Boeing and Airbus. However, technical support appears firm around $1,200 per share.
