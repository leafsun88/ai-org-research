## 3. Roper Technologies vs Constellation Software (CSU)

### Similarities
- Both are serial acquirers of niche vertical market software businesses
- Both avoid growth-through-product-development model
- Both focus on mission-critical, embedded software with high switching costs
- Roper is CSU's closest U.S. counterpart

### Key Differences
| Dimension | Roper (ROP) | Constellation (CSU) |
|-----------|-------------|---------------------|
| Deal Size | Larger deals ($800M-$1.86B each) | Smaller, higher volume |
| Structure | More centralized | Highly decentralized (6 operating groups) |
| ROIC | Strong but lower | Sustained high teens (best-in-class) |
| P/S Ratio | ~7x | ~5.5x |
| OCF Growth | Strong | Best-in-class |
| Dilution | Some (buybacks offset) | Minimal |
| Geographic | Primarily US | Global (200+ acquisitions) |
| Revenue | ~$8.5B | ~$10B+ |

### Competitive Dynamic
- Roper increasingly encroaching on CSU's space as it leans further into software + AI
- CSU has deeper bench in small deal sourcing and integration
- Roper has higher margins (40% EBITDA) partly due to larger deal sizes in more profitable niches
