# Neil Hunn — Roper Technologies CEO Research
**Date**: 2026-04-08

## Background
- President & CEO since 2018
- Member of Business Roundtable
- Led transformation from diversified industrial conglomerate to asset-light vertical software portfolio
- Succeeded Brian Jellison (who passed away in 2018), widely regarded as the architect of modern Roper

## CEO Philosophy & Strategy

### Acquisition Framework
Neil Hunn evaluates acquisitions through three core questions:
1. Is it a business in a niche market?
2. Is it in a leadership position in its niche?
3. Does it have an ability to invest in itself to grow?

### Operating Model
- Describes Roper as "a durable, steady vertical market software compounder"
- Characterized the business as "roughly $8.5B revenue, 40% EBITDA margins, 30%+ FCF margins"
- Strategy: compound cash flow through niche software acquisitions
- Divested industrial assets, strategically acquired high-margin software companies
- Decentralized operating model — portfolio companies run independently

### Capital Deployment Track Record
- 2024: $3.6B deployed across vertical software acquisitions (Procare $1.86B, Transact $1.60B)
- 2025: $3.3B deployed (CentralReach $1.65B, Subsplash $800M, bolt-ons)
- 2026 capacity: ~$6B for M&A or buybacks
- Focus on "high-quality, market-leading technology businesses that enhance long-term cash flow compounding"

## Recent Public Appearances

### Morgan Stanley TMT Conference (Feb/Mar 2026)
- Participated with Jason Conley (EVP & CFO)
- Topic: Embracing AI for Growth
- Described AI strategy: central AI accelerator team sharing technical expertise across portfolio
