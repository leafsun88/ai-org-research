# Carvana: Groupthink

The author presents a contrarian analysis of Carvana, arguing that overwhelmingly bearish market sentiment may represent groupthink rather than careful analysis. With most seeming to assume Carvana is going to declare bankruptcy shortly, the investment takes the position that the market has become dangerously one-sided in its pessimism.

## Key Financial Position

Carvana maintains substantial liquidity despite challenges. The company possesses approximately $2 billion in unpledged real estate assets and securitization interests that could be monetized if necessary. Management is aggressively cutting costs and improving profitability to minimize cash burn.

## Fourth Quarter Performance

Retail units declined 23% year-over-year, which the author characterizes as reasonable given self-imposed profitability measures including inventory reduction and advertising cuts. The significant drop in gross profit per unit was primarily attributable to two non-recurring items: vehicle write-downs from deflationary pressures and timing differences in loan sales.

## Three-Step Recovery Path

Management outlined a progression: achieving adjusted EBITDA breakeven, reaching substantially positive unit economics, then restarting growth initiatives. The author suggests breakeven is achievable by late 2023, with positive unit economics and growth resumption following in 2024-2025.

## Forward Outlook

The analysis projects improving metrics across 2023 quarters, with particular strength expected in first-quarter loan sales generating unusually high Other GPU figures. The author models recovery scenarios suggesting CVNA shareholders could realize significant multiples from current levels if execution continues.

The piece concludes that this represents either a complete loss scenario or a 10x or 20x or more opportunity depending on business trajectory.
