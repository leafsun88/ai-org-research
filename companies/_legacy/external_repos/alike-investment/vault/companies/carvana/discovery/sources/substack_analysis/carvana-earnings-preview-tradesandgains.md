# $CVNA Earnings Preview: Can Carvana Clear the Bar After a 27% Drawdown?

Carvana is reporting Q4 2025 earnings after market close on February 18, 2026, amid significant stock pressure and high market scrutiny.

## Key Context

The stock has declined approximately 27% over the past month, while options markets are pricing in a potential 15-16% post-earnings move. The Street expects solid execution: consensus estimates call for earnings per share around $1.10-$1.16 and revenue of $5.2-$5.3 billion, representing roughly 47% year-over-year growth with unit volume projected in the mid-150,000 to mid-160,000 range.

## The Transformation Narrative

The company has shifted from existential survival concerns to a profitability and margin durability story. In Q3 2025, Carvana reported earnings of approximately $1.03 per share (missing consensus of $1.33) but delivered revenue of $5.65 billion, beating expectations with 55% year-over-year growth.

## Operational Framework

Management's operational framework emphasizes:
- Aggressive unit expansion
- Improving gross profit per unit
- Lowering per-unit SG&A costs
- Balance sheet stabilization
- Free cash flow improvement

However, rising capital expenditures -- approximately $96 million year-to-date versus $67 million previously -- signal expansion ambitions while pressuring near-term cash generation.

This earnings report will determine whether the company's transformation gains credibility with investors.
