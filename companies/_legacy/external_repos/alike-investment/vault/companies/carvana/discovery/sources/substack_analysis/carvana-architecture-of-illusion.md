# The Architecture of Illusion: Carvana, the Garcia Dynasty, and the Three-Channel Fragility

How a Convicted Felon's Private Empire Burns Billions to Fund Record Public Profits While Subprime Defaults Silently Surpass 2008

## Overview

The article presents a forensic analysis of Carvana Co., examining why the stock collapsed despite impressive FY2025 financial results. Carvana reported $20.322 billion in revenue and $2.237 billion in adjusted EBITDA -- representing 49% and 62% year-over-year growth respectively.

## Three Interconnected Risk Channels

The author argues three interconnected factors -- rather than isolated issues -- explain the market's sharp reversal:

1. **The Garcia family ecosystem's subsidy architecture** -- Complex related-party transactions between Carvana and DriveTime (owned by Ernest Garcia II) that may artificially inflate reported profitability

2. **Subprime auto loan delinquencies exceeding 2008 crisis levels by 45%** -- Carvana's heavy exposure to subprime borrowers creates systemic risk as delinquency rates surpass levels seen during the Great Financial Crisis

3. **A $500 million annual cash interest obligation cliff on $3.93 billion in restructured debt** -- The transition from PIK (payment-in-kind) interest to cash interest creates a significant new cash drain

## Key Claims

The author claims to source all assertions from public filings, court records, and regulatory documents. The analysis identifies five information asymmetries, specific risk conditions, and frameworks for understanding correlated channel risk within concentrated governance structures.

The piece concludes that institutional positions are being constructed based on this convergence of risks.
