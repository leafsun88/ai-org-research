# Carvana (CVNA) Is Worth Zero - The Subprime Scam

This investigative report argues that Carvana's "record profitability" is fundamentally illusory, driven by accounting gimmicks rather than genuine business performance.

## Key Arguments

### Profitability Illusion

The authors contend that roughly 93% of Carvana's year-to-date net income derives from Gain on Loan Sale accounting. The company originates subprime auto loans -- targeting borrowers requiring almost 50% of their entire annual income for vehicles -- then immediately sells these loans to generate upfront gains while transferring credit risk elsewhere.

### Cash Flow Deterioration

Despite headline profits, operating cash flow has collapsed by $252 million year-over-year. Vehicle inventory surged 44% in nine months, consuming $676 million in cash as inventory depreciates while sitting unsold.

### Garcia Family Dynamics

Ernest Garcia II, CEO Ernie Garcia III's father, is a convicted felon from the 1990 Lincoln Savings scandal. The family has executed systematic insider selling -- $5 billion total, with Garcia II alone dumping $1.4 billion in 2025 while only one other insider purchased $200,000 worth of shares.

### Debt Servicing Crisis

Total debt stands at $5.06 billion, with Payment-in-Kind interest compounding annually at 12-14%. A debt service cliff approaches as the company must now pay cash interest obligations it cannot support operationally.

### Legal Exposure

Multiple fronts threaten the business: state regulatory actions threatening operational licenses, securities litigation that survived dismissal motions, and consumer class actions alleging title registration failures.

The report characterizes Carvana as engaging in unsustainable financing -- relying on continued capital market appetite for subprime auto securitizations that could freeze suddenly.
