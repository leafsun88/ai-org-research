# Carvana Just Printed $2.2 Billion in EBITDA -- So Why Did the Stock Crash 20% Overnight?

## Overview

Carvana reported record fiscal 2025 results with $20.3 billion in revenue and $2.24 billion in adjusted EBITDA, yet the stock plummeted 20% after-hours. This paradox reflects investor concerns about margin sustainability rather than demand fundamentals.

## Key Financial Results

The company achieved impressive volume metrics: 596,641 retail units sold, representing 43% year-over-year growth. However, Q4 adjusted EBITDA of $511 million fell short of consensus expectations around $536 million. The shortfall stemmed from declining gross profit per unit, which dropped $255 sequentially to $3,076, driven by elevated reconditioning expenses that management indicated would persist into Q1 2026.

Full-year profitability reached an 11% adjusted EBITDA margin, positioning Carvana as the most profitable public American auto retailer by this metric. The company reported net income of $1.90 billion, though approximately $685 million represented a non-cash tax benefit from releasing a deferred tax asset valuation allowance.

## Balance Sheet Strength

Carvana's financial position improved significantly, with cash reserves of $2.6 billion and a net debt-to-EBITDA ratio of 1.5x, reflecting the company's strongest-ever balance sheet. Total debt ranges between $4.6 billion and $5.6 billion depending on floorplan facility inclusion.

## Market Context & Challenges

Used-car pricing benefited from a 2.9% year-over-year increase in the Manheim index. However, headwinds emerged: subprime delinquencies reached record levels at 6.9%, and average monthly payments approached $550, signaling affordability constraints for price-sensitive buyers.

## Forward Guidance Concerns

Management projected "significant growth" in units and EBITDA for 2026 but declined to provide specific targets. This vagueness, combined with the margin miss, contributed substantially to investor disappointment and the sharp stock decline following earnings release.
