# Carvana Deep Dive ($CVNA)

What do you have to believe for a 30% CAGR over 10 years?

## Investment Thesis

This comprehensive investment analysis examines Carvana's potential as a disruptive force in the used car industry. The author argues that the company follows a proven retail disruption pattern -- higher upfront costs offset by superior efficiency and lower prices at scale.

The analysis contends that Carvana can capture approximately 7% market share by 2032 while generating sustainable 3% free cash flow margins. This would support a projected 30% compound annual return over the decade.

## Market Opportunity

The used car sector represents roughly $880 billion in annual sales across 43,000 dealerships. Notably, the top 8 players cumulatively only have ~6.5% market share. This fragmentation creates significant consolidation potential for a well-positioned competitor.

## Competitive Advantages

Carvana's vertically integrated model delivers measurable customer benefits: approximately $1,500 savings versus traditional dealers, access to 75,000 inventory units versus 250-300 locally, and transaction completion within 10 minutes instead of 40 minutes spent on typical dealership paperwork.

## Financial Projections

The base case models indicate Carvana achieving $4,000-4,500 in gross profit per retail unit by 2032, with operational leverage driving SG&A down to manageable levels. The analysis includes bull and bear scenarios accommodating various growth assumptions.

## Current Challenges

The author acknowledges three pressures: Omicron-related logistics disruptions, affordability constraints from inflation and rising rates, and debt concerns from the ADESA acquisition. However, he contends these obstacles appear surmountable given Carvana's cash position and demonstrated operating leverage.
