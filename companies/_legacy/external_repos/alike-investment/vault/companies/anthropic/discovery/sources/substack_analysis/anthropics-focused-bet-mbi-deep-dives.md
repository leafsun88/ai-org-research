# Anthropic's Focused Bet, Portfolio Change

## Model Performance & Benchmarks

Anthropic recently released Claude Opus 4.5, which surpassed Google's Gemini 3 Pro in several benchmark categories. The article distinguishes between two models: Sonnet (efficient, cost-effective) and Opus (premium reasoning for complex tasks).

The author raises concerns about benchmark reliability, invoking Goodhart's Law: when a measure becomes a target, it ceases to be a good measure. AI companies may be optimizing specifically for benchmarks rather than genuine capability.

## Real-World Validation

More meaningful signals come from actual customers. Cognition AI's blog post about rebuilding their "Devin" AI software engineer demonstrates Sonnet 4.5's practical improvements: planning performance up 18%, end-to-end scores up 12%, with faster multi-hour sessions. Customer perspective carries more weight than abstract benchmarks.

## Anthropic's Strategic Focus

Anthropic has become a "20-bagger" (20x valuation increase) in under two years, rising from $18 billion to $350 billion valuations. The company differentiates through extreme strategic focus on coding and computer use, deliberately sacrificing mathematical reasoning to pursue near-term economic impact.

## Why Coding?

According to Sholto Douglas from Anthropic, coding represents the optimal tractable problem domain because:
- It enables faster AI research acceleration through automation
- Massive market demand exists among eager early adopters
- Software demand vastly exceeds supply
- Coding is uniquely measurable with unit tests and containerization
- Unlike self-driving (requiring first-try success), failed code attempts can iterate repeatedly

The focused bet is clearly succeeding.
