# The Growth Miracle and the Six Fractures: Anthropic at $380 Billion

## The Fastest Company in History

Anthropic's $30 billion Series G on February 12, 2026, at $380 billion post-money valuation. At 27x revenue, this isn't traditional enterprise software valuation — it's a derivative bet on achieving what Dario Amodei describes as "a country of geniuses in a datacenter."

## Revenue Growth

- $1 billion (December 2024)
- $4 billion (July 2025)
- $9 billion (December 2025)
- $14 billion (February 2026)
- This represents sustained 10x annual growth — unprecedented for enterprise technology at this scale

## Claude Code Growth

- Zero to $2.5 billion annualized billings in nine months
- Business subscriptions quadrupled in six weeks post-January 1
- Over 500 customers spend $1 million+ annually (vs. roughly a dozen two years prior)
- Eight of Fortune's ten largest companies are Claude customers

## Six Structural Fractures (Risk Analysis)

The article examines whether six structural fractures justify a multiple demanding near-perfect execution through 2028:

1. Unit economics divergence
2. Customer concentration paradoxes
3. Three simultaneous distribution barriers that closed in January 2026
4. A $4.5 billion legal exposure naming founders personally
5. Pentagon constraints on the safety-oriented brand
6. Competitive convergence eroding technical advantages

## The Margin Bridge Problem

Revenue at 40% gross margins operates as fundamentally different business than revenue at 77% gross margins. The gap between Anthropic's current position and 2028 targets represents perhaps the most aggressive margin expansion assumption embedded in any private technology valuation.
