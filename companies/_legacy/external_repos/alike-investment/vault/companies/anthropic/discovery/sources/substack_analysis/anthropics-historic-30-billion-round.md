# Anthropic's Historic $30 Billion Round

## Funding Overview

Anthropic closed the second-largest venture funding round in history — a $30 billion Series G at a $380 billion valuation. This came just five months after the company's $13 billion Series F at a $183 billion valuation, effectively doubling valuation in under six months. Since its 2021 founding, Anthropic has raised $63.7 billion across all funding rounds.

## Revenue Trajectory

- Zero revenue in January 2023
- $100 million by January 2024
- $1 billion by January 2025
- $14 billion in run-rate revenue by February 2026

Attributed to its position as the intelligence platform of choice for enterprises and developers.

## Market Impact

- Claude Code: 4% of GitHub public commits already generated by the tool, with projections reaching 20% by year-end 2026
- Ramp data: Anthropic adoption grew from 16.7% to 19.5% (one of its largest monthly gains), while OpenAI adoption declined from 36.8% to 35.9%

## AI Safety Concerns

Mrinank Sharma, a senior Anthropic safety researcher leading the Safeguards Research Team, resigned, stating that throughout his two-year tenure, he repeatedly saw how hard it is to truly let values govern actions and warned that the world is in peril from artificial intelligence.

This resignation carries particular significance given Anthropic's founding mission. CEO Dario Amodei co-authored the "Concrete Problems in Safety" paper in 2016 and founded Anthropic to address AI alignment alongside scaling efforts.

## Memory Chip Supply Constraints

The semiconductor industry reports severe memory chip supply contraction. AI infrastructure demands have consumed NAND, DRAM, and high bandwidth memory manufacturing capacity. Standard DRAM spot market pricing surged 600%. Supply-demand imbalances projected to persist until new fabrication facilities operationalize in early 2028.
