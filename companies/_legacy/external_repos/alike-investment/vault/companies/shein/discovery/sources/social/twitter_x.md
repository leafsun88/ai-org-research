# SHEIN Twitter/X Discussion Summary

## De Minimis Rule Impact (Major Theme)

### Policy Change
- Trump administration ended the de minimis rule (duty-free imports under $800)
- New 120% levy or flat fee of $100/package (rising to $200 in June) layered on existing 145% tariff on Chinese exports
- Makes cross-border low-value parcel shipments uneconomical for SHEIN/Temu model

### User Demand Impact
- Temu U.S. DAUs dropped 52% (May vs March) per Sensor Tower via CNBC
- SHEIN U.S. DAUs down 25% in same period
- Packages now face customs clearance adding days/weeks to delivery
- Buyers may see $10-$20 per-package duties

### Analyst Commentary
- @ruima (Rui Ma, TechBuzz China): "It's not the removal of de minimis itself causing disruption" - the layered tariff structure is the real problem
- @lmatsakis (Louise Matsakis): "SHEIN and Temu have been working for the last few years to vastly decrease their reliance on de minimis, bc they know consumers don't want to wait a week or 10 days for stuff"
- @Techmeme: SHEIN prices spiked in April, settled 12.5% above pre-tariff levels by late May, jumped again in July

### Platform Adaptation Signals
- Both SHEIN and Temu building local fulfillment/warehousing in US
- Shifting from pure China-direct to hybrid model
- Pricing advantage over Amazon shrinking but companies implementing countermeasures
