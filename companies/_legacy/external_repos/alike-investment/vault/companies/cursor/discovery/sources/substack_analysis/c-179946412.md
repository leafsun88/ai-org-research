Product Growth

Cursor is the fastest company ever to reach $1B ARR

And it did so with 0 full-time PMs:

It's wild.

But everyone is taking the wrong lesson from this. 

Cursor scaled without traditional PMs, because they’re building a developer tool for developers. 

The model breaks the second you’re building for non-technical users. 

You see, product decisions require: 

Understanding customer jobs

Translating between the technical + user needs

Prioritizing across conflicting stakeholder demands

Maintaining strategic coherence as the team grows

Cursor gets away with fuzzy roadmaps and fluid roles because every person in those concentric circles (staff, beta users, early adopters, enterprises) can evaluate a live prototype and tell you exactly what’s wrong. 

Most companies don’t have that luxury:

Your developers are disconnected from your users

Your go-to-market team needs a sales roadmap

Your stakeholders don’t understand API latency

Your support team needs documentation

Your compliance team needs audit trails

Your users can’t code

The Cursor model works when the product is the development environment. For everything else, you need someone to find what’s: 

a. technically possible

b. what’s commercially viable

c. what customers actually want

 That’s the PM role.

Marty Cagan explained it best: youtube.com/watch?v=J0F…

Peter Yang broke this news in an interview with Cursor's head of design: youtube.com/watch?v=bdh…

My take?

Copying Cursor’s structure without Cursor’s context is how you end up with features nobody asked for and engineers burned out from scope creep.
