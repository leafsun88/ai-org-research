## Founding Story

### Origin (2022)
The four founders initially pursued AI tools for mechanical engineering and CAD software autocomplete. However, they pivoted after recognizing the CAD market was stagnant and limited by data constraints.

### The Pivot
By mid-2022, they shifted focus to software development after identifying gaps in GitHub Copilot's capabilities. Key insight: Copilot was a plugin tool, not a purpose-built IDE for the AI era.

### Technical Decision
Rather than building an IDE from scratch, they forked Visual Studio Code — enabling rapid development while maintaining familiarity for existing developers. This architectural choice proved crucial for adoption.

### Launch & Acceleration
- Graduated from OpenAI's accelerator program (2023)
- Launched Cursor in March 2023
- Reached $100M ARR by January 2025 (20 months)
- No traditional marketing spend — pure product-led growth

### Key Challenges (2026)
- Competition from Anthropic (Claude Code) with wholesale model pricing advantage
- Talent retention under pressure — engineers departing for xAI, lost head of engineering
- Industry paradigm potentially shifting from IDE-based to agent-based workflows
- Building proprietary models while managing rapid growth
