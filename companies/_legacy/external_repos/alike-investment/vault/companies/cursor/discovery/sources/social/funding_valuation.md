# Cursor (Anysphere) — Funding, Valuation & Revenue

## Company Overview

Anysphere, the company behind Cursor AI code editor, was founded in 2022 by four MIT classmates. It is one of the fastest-growing SaaS companies in history, reaching $1B ARR in approximately 24 months from launch.

## User & Adoption Metrics

- 1 million+ daily active users (December 2025)
- 50,000+ businesses powered
- 360,000+ paying subscribers (March 2025)
- 67% of Fortune 500 companies using Cursor
- 150 million lines of enterprise code generated daily
- Half of Fortune 500 adopted by June 2025

## Business Model & Revenue Mix

### Pricing (Individual)
- Hobby: Free (limited)
- Pro: $20/month (unlimited Tab completions, agent access)
- Pro+: $60/month (3x usage)
- Ultra: $200/month (20x usage, priority)

### Pricing (Team/Enterprise)
- Teams: $40/user/month
- Enterprise: Custom pricing
- Bugbot (PR Review): $40/month

### Revenue Composition Shift
- Enterprise customers now account for ~60% of revenue (up from primarily individual developers)
- Average enterprise contract: $19,200-$152,600/year
- Average individual customer: ~$276 (early 2025)
- 4,000-5,000 enterprise inquiries in February 2025 alone

## Financial Health

- Single-digit monthly cash burn rate
- $1 billion in cash reserves (as of December 2025)
- CEO Michael Truell: "No plans to IPO soon"

## Key Risks

1. **Model dependency**: Reliant on third-party foundation models (Anthropic Claude, OpenAI) — retail cost vs. competitors' wholesale access
2. **Competition from model providers**: Anthropic (Claude Code) and OpenAI building direct coding tools
3. **Talent retention**: Engineers departing for xAI and others; lost head of engineering
4. **Market shift**: Industry perception moving toward autonomous agents over IDE workflows
5. **Security incidents**: May 2025 malicious npm packages; April 2025 AI hallucination in support bot

## Strategic Response

- Developing proprietary model "Composer" — outperforms Anthropic Opus 4.6 on certain benchmarks
- Deepening enterprise relationships (60% of revenue)
- Expanding beyond code editing toward full software creation platform
- CEO maintains vision of building "a long-lasting, independent, generational company"

- Global developer tools market: $6.6B (2024) → projected $22.6B by 2033 (14.5% CAGR)
