# Founder/CEO Research: Zomato (Eternal)

## 1. Deepinder Goyal (Founder, Vice Chairman)

### Background
- Founded Zomato in 2008 (originally Foodiebay)
- IIT Delhi graduate
- Led company from restaurant listing to food delivery to quick commerce to Eternal conglomerate
- Stepped down as Group CEO effective February 1, 2026

### Leadership Transition (Jan 2026)
- Announced resignation as CEO on January 21, 2026
- Elevated Albinder Dhindsa (Blinkit founder) as successor
- Remains on board as Vice Chairman and Non-Executive Director
- Stated he plans to pursue "higher-risk initiatives outside the remit of a listed company CEO"

### Post-Zomato Ventures
- **Temple** (brain-monitoring wearable startup): Raised $54M at ~$190M post-money valuation (Feb 2026)
  - Investors: founder friends and early Zomato backers
  - Source: TechCrunch, Feb 27, 2026
- **Continue Research**: Committed $25M personal capital (Oct 2025) — human lifespan extension

### Shareholding
- Holds 3.83% stake in Eternal (as of June 2025), down from 4.18% (369.47M shares)
- No insider trading allegations found in search
- Stake valued at over INR 10,000 crore

### Key Decisions Under Goyal
- Blinkit acquisition for $568M (2022) — now valued at $13B
- Rebranding from Zomato to Eternal Limited (2025)
- Aggressive quick commerce investment (INR 4,300 crore total into Blinkit)
- OpenAI strategic partnership for AI integration across platforms

## 2. Albinder Dhindsa (New CEO, effective Feb 1, 2026)

### Background
- IIT Delhi graduate + Columbia MBA
- Co-founded Grofers (later renamed Blinkit)
- Sold Blinkit to Zomato in 2022 for $568M
- Previously at The Boston Consulting Group

### Leadership Style
- Data-driven, efficiency-first approach
- Prioritizes logistics optimization and unit economics
- Favors measurable outcomes and course correction when models fail
- Contrast with Goyal: execution-first vs experimentation-led

### Strategic Priorities as CEO
1. **Quick Commerce Expansion**: 2,027 dark stores (Q3 FY26) to 3,000 target by FY27
2. **Category Expansion**: Electronics, beauty and make-up, pet care, toys and games
3. **Marketing Investment**: 4x YoY marketing spend increase, 40% QoQ in Q2
4. **Profitability Discipline**: Balancing aggressive growth with unit economics focus
5. **Operational Excellence**: Logistics optimization across Zomato + Blinkit + Hyperpure + District

### Market Reaction to CEO Change
- Stock was around INR 236.50 on April 1, 2026
- Analysts largely positive: execution-focused leader for operational phase

## 3. Key Organizational Signal: Founder Succession

### Pattern Analysis
- Goyal stepping down at age ~40 after 18 years is notable
- Not pushed out — voluntary, with clear succession plan
- Successor is an operator/executor, not a visionary — signals company entering "scaling phase"
- Goyal pursuing moonshot ventures (brain tech, longevity) — pattern similar to Bezos to Jassy at Amazon
- Risk: institutional knowledge loss, culture shift from startup energy to corporate execution
