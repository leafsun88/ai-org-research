## Organizational Structure

- **Holding company model**: Booking Holdings as parent, with semi-autonomous brand subsidiaries
- **Key subsidiaries**: Booking.com (dominant), Priceline, KAYAK, Agoda, OpenTable, Rentalcars.com
- **Decentralized brand management**: Each subsidiary has its own CEO
- **Centralized functions**: Finance, legal, strategy at holding level
- **Connected Trip integration**: Cross-brand product unification driving tighter coordination

## Culture Mechanisms

1. **Customer-centric philosophy**: Fogel asked team what culture they wanted; landed on "customer-centric"
2. **Trust-based leadership**: Fogel trusts domain experts, doesn't pretend to know everything
3. **Data-driven**: Booking.com historically one of most A/B testing-intensive companies globally
4. **Adaptive decision-making**: Emphasis on rapid decisions based on real-time signals
5. **Employee engagement**: Won awards for engagement levels

## Key Observations

- **Strength**: Fogel is a 26-year veteran — deep institutional knowledge, continuity
- **Strength**: CFO upgrade from fintech world signals strategic pivot
- **Risk**: Holding company model can create silos between brands
- **Risk**: Connected Trip requires cross-brand coordination that holding structure may resist
- **Opportunity**: Board addition of Sievers signals technology investment acceleration
