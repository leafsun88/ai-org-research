# Glenn Fogel — CEO Profile Search — 2026-04-06

## Background
- **Education**: Harvard Law School (JD), Wharton School (BS Economics)
- **Joined Booking**: February 2000 (pre-Priceline era)
- **CEO since**: January 2017
- **CEO of Booking.com**: Additionally since June 2019
- **Not a founder**: Career executive, but 26-year tenure = quasi-founder depth

## Key Quotes & Philosophy

### On Leadership
- "A CEO's job is not about taking the game-winning shot; rather, a CEO's job is to create the environment where players can thrive."
- First to admit when something is not his area of expertise — trusts experts on his team

### On Strategy (Connected Trip)
- Vision: Everything seamless, everything easy, everything updates automatically
- AI as enabler of connected trip — agentic AI to reduce friction
- Multi-vertical approach: flights (+37%), activities, car rentals alongside core accommodation

### On AI
- Sees parallels between AI era and dot-com boom
- AI is key to personalization at scale
- 2026 focus: deploying agentic capabilities for unified, personalized experience
- Not scared of AI disruption — believes Booking's data moat is defensible

### On Practice
- Advocates "deliberate practice beats just putting in hours"
- Adaptive leadership: rapid decisions based on real-time signals from customers and teams

## Decision-Making Style
- **Consultative**: Trusts domain experts, collaborative
- **Long-tenured**: 26 years of institutional context = deep pattern recognition
- **Customer-obsessed**: Built culture around customer-centricity
- **Pragmatic**: Not a visionary revolutionary — more of an evolutionary builder

## Strengths
- Extraordinary tenure provides deep institutional knowledge
- Survived multiple travel industry disruptions (9/11, GFC, COVID)
- Successfully pivoted from Priceline "name your price" to global OTA
- Connected Trip vision is coherent and data-backed

## Weaknesses
- Not a founder — lacks the "reality distortion field" of founder-CEOs
- More of an operator-optimizer than a disruptive visionary
- AI strategy feels reactive (countering disruption) not proactive (creating disruption)
- Holding company structure may limit his ability to drive rapid organizational change
