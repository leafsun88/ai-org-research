## Consensus Investment Thesis (Across Substacks)

### Business Model Strengths
- Digital-only model with ~85% lower cost-to-serve vs traditional banks
- $5 CAC per customer with 80-90% organic acquisition (word of mouth)
- LTV/CAC ratio over 30x — exceptional unit economics
- Technology built from scratch (Clojure-based cloud stack), no legacy systems
- Monthly voluntary churn rate of just 0.06%

### Financial Trajectory
- Revenue growing 40%+ YoY on FX-neutral basis
- ARPAC expanding from $4.90 (2021) toward $15+ monthly for mature cohorts
- Net Promoter Scores of 90 (Brazil), 94 (Mexico) — industry-leading
- Efficiency ratio improved from 78% (Q4 2021) to 19.9% (Q4 2025)
- NPL rates 25-30% better than competitors due to proprietary credit models

### Competitive Position
- Largest credit card issuer in Brazil (~22% market share)
- 131M customers across Brazil, Mexico, Colombia
- Former Capital One executives built proprietary credit models analyzing 11,000+ data points
- Competitors (Itau: 120K employees) have fundamentally higher cost structures

### Growth Catalysts
- U.S. national bank charter (OCC conditional approval Jan 2026)
- Mexico banking license (CNBV approved April 2025)
- Brazil full banking license expected 2026
- AI-first strategy (Hyperplane acquisition, nuFormer credit model, Precog)
- Insurance, investments, small business lending expansion

### Key Risks Identified
- Valuation premium (25x P/E vs 11x banking average)
- Brazil macro/FX exposure (Real depreciated 40%+ since 2016)
- Credit cycle risk as personal loan book grows
- Competition from MercadoPago (66M), PicPay (65.6M), and digitizing incumbents
- Execution risk in U.S. market entry

### Valuation Range (Substack Consensus)
- Bear case: $12-14 (credit deterioration + multiple compression)
- Base case: $17-21 (continued execution at current trajectory)
- Bull case: $25-30+ (successful U.S. entry + ARPAC expansion)
- Morgan Stanley envisions $100B valuation by end of 2026
