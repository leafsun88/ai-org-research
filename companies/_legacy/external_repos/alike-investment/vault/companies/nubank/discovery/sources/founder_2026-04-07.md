# Nubank Founders/CEO Profile — David Velez & Cristina Junqueira

## David Velez (CEO & Founder)

### Background
- Colombian-born, Stanford MBA
- Pre-Nubank: General Atlantic (NYC), Sequoia Capital (LatAm), Morgan Stanley
- Founded Nubank in 2013 in Sao Paulo, Brazil
- Net worth: ~$11.3B (Forbes)
- Sequoia's first investment in Latin America

### Recent Leadership Changes (2025)
- **March 24, 2025**: Velez resumed direct operational control of Nubank, described as "returning to the driver's seat" of the $55B fintech
- Signaled a sharpening of focus on operational agility and new market expansion
- 2025 focus areas: core market execution + international growth

### Key Podcast Appearances (2025-2026)
1. **In Good Company (Nicolai Tangen, Oct 2025)** — Discussed customer obsession, scaling from startup to major financial institution, global digitalization opportunity
2. **Deep Purpose (Ranjay Gulati, May 2025)** — Leadership, company culture, philanthropy commitment
3. **CNBC Squawk Box (Dec 2025)** — U.S. expansion plans, competition, learning from Brazil's digital payments
4. **20VC (Harry Stebbings)** — Scaling Nubank to $25B, founder-to-CEO transition, "Responsibility Framework" for driving efficiency
5. **Morgan Stanley Exceptional Leaders** — Long-form interview on strategy and vision
6. **Sequoia Crucible Moments** — Nubank founding story and early pivotal decisions

### CEO Cognitive Patterns (from interviews)
- **Customer obsession as north star** — Consistently references NPS scores (90 Brazil, 94 Mexico) as most important metric
- **Responsibility Framework** — Velez's management philosophy: clear ownership drives efficiency, distinguishes what makes a great founder different from a great CEO
- **Long-term thinking** — Explicitly discusses multi-decade horizon for building financial infrastructure
- **Disruption from first principles** — Built entire tech stack from scratch rather than adapting legacy systems
- **Philanthropy commitment** — Plans to give away majority of wealth (Giving Pledge context)
- **Humility + ambition** — Acknowledges "what makes a great founder does not make a great CEO" — shows self-awareness about leadership evolution

### Public Statements (2025-2026)
- On U.S. expansion: Exploring beyond LatAm, including possible U.S. entry (Jan 2025)
- On AI: Divided AI strategy into customer-facing (personalization, Precog) and business ops (collections, fraud)
- On competition: "The market is large enough for multiple players" but execution and cost structure matter
- On digitalization: "U.S. can learn a lot from Brazil" on digital payments (CNBC Dec 2025)

## Cristina Junqueira (Co-Founder & CEO, Nu U.S.)

### Background
- Brazilian, Itau Unibanco veteran (cards division)
- Co-founded Nubank with Velez in 2013
- 3% stake in Nubank — one of world's youngest self-made female billionaires
- Moved from Sao Paulo to Miami in 2025 with family

### Current Role (2025-2026)
- **CEO of Nu's emerging U.S. business** — leading the entire U.S. market entry
- Overseeing OCC bank charter process (conditional approval received Jan 29, 2026)
- Building U.S. brand from scratch — describes it as "returning to startup fundamentals"

### Key Appearances (2025-2026)
1. **Fortune Interview (Feb 2026)** — Detailed U.S. expansion strategy
2. **CNBC Changemakers (2026)** — Named to "Women Transforming Business" list
3. **Bloomberg (Jan 2025)** — Disrupting banking industry

### Strategic Vision for U.S.
- **Initial target**: 800K-5M customers — LatAm immigrants and frequent travelers
- **Long-term ambition**: "We want to build for the average American, whoever that is" — NOT positioning as a "Latino brand"
- **Brand strategy**: Nubank became official brand partner for Mercedes' F1 team for global awareness
- **Key insight**: "The biggest thing in the U.S. will be building the brand—cutting through the noise"

### Leadership Style
- Brand architect — built Nubank into "the most valuable brand in Brazil" and "the most loved brand in Brazil"
- Product-oriented: originally led credit card product development
- Hands-on operator: personally relocated to Miami for U.S. launch
- Public-facing: comfortable with media, active speaker

## Founder Dynamic Assessment

### Complementarity
- **Velez**: Strategy + capital markets + global vision + investor relations
- **Junqueira**: Product + brand + operations + market execution
- Clear role division: Velez runs LatAm core, Junqueira leads U.S. greenfield

### Red Flags
- None significant detected
- Velez's "return to driver's seat" in March 2025 suggests possible prior delegation issues, but framed positively

### Green Flags
- Both founders still deeply operational after 12+ years
- Willingness to relocate (Junqueira to Miami) for strategic priorities
- Sequoia's Doug Leone personally cited as "best investor" — long-term VC alignment
- Consistent messaging across multiple forums and years
