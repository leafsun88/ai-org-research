# Is Lemonade a Buy? Fundamentals meet Technicals: Lemonade

## Revenue Performance

Lemonade demonstrates stable growth with a 54% CAGR from 2020-2024, characterized as insane growth that should satisfy shareholders.

## Profitability Concerns

The company exhibits negative trajectories across several metrics. Net income deteriorated from -$122M (2020) to -$202M (2024). Operating income similarly shows unprofitable operations, though management demonstrates improving cost control with margins becoming better each year.

## Free Cash Flow Trajectory

A positive development: FCF turned positive after reaching -$173M in 2022, signaling management's capability in cash flow management.

## Management Alignment

Insider ownership stands at approximately 18.4%, exceeding the benchmark threshold. CEO Daniel Schreiber maintains roughly 2.7% ownership through approximately 2M shares of 74M total outstanding shares.

## Margin Analysis

Current margins remain deeply negative: -38% for both operating and net profit margins, though this reflects the company's growth phase.

## Capital Allocation Metrics

- ROIC has improved substantially, indicating sound investment decisions
- Goodwill remains quite low, reflecting organic growth rather than acquisition-driven expansion
- Capex stays steady, reducing FCF impact as the company scales

## Stock-Based Compensation Impact

SBC destroys their FCF completely, rendering free cash flow worthless currently. However, growing FCF should eventually outweigh SBC expenses. The company has never executed share buybacks since IPO.

## Valuation Metrics

Forward P/S trades slightly above its historical median, suggesting potential overvaluation. Forward EV/Sales similarly indicates the stock may currently be overvalued based on this metric.

## Key Takeaway

Mixed picture: Lemonade shows exceptional revenue growth and improving operational efficiency, yet profitability remains elusive and current valuations appear stretched relative to historical norms.
