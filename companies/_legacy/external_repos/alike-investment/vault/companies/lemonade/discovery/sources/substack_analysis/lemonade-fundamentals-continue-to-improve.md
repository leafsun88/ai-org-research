# Lemonade: Fundamentals Continue to Improve

## Overview

This analysis examines Lemonade's Q2 2025 earnings, positioning the company on a path toward becoming a "Singularity Scaler" — a business achieving exponential operational improvements through technology.

## Key Performance Indicators

### Growth Metrics
- In-force premiums (IFP) grew 29% year-over-year
- IFP growth is accelerating, signaling stronger customer resonance
- Operating expenses remained relatively flat despite revenue expansion
- Gross loss ratio declined to 67% this quarter

### European Operations
The European segment demonstrated exceptional momentum with $43 million in IFP, representing over 200% growth and marking the eighth consecutive quarter of triple-digit expansion.

## AI-Driven Competitive Advantages

### LoCo Technology
Management highlighted their proprietary LLM-first no-code insurance application builder that enables them to build products and launch regions in hours instead of weeks. This contrasts sharply with competitors requiring large local teams and legacy infrastructure.

### Reinsurance Strategy
Management reduced quota share reinsurance from 55% to 20%, signaling confidence in their underwriting capabilities. This decision indicates management believes their pricing ability and risk assessment are rapidly improving.

## Financial Path Forward

### Current State
Free cash flow per share remains negative but shows convergence toward positive territory as loss ratios improve.

### Future Drivers
1. Operational scale and leverage expansion, particularly evident in European markets
2. Renewal policy benefits combined with cross-sell expansion (50% of growth derived from cross-selling)

## Business Philosophy

Two critical characteristics:
1. **Innovation Stack:** Years of focused iteration creating competitive moats
2. **Customer Value Sharing:** Management actively passes efficiency gains back to customers through competitive pricing

CFO Timothy Bixby noted the company tends to give the benefit of unit economics improvements back to the customer where market dynamics permit.

## Valuation Assessment

Trading at approximately 6 times sales, considered reasonably valued despite recent appreciation. The author maintains no current position but suggests continued close monitoring given trajectory improvements.

## Author's Perspective

Initially harbored skepticism about management's AI strategy, believing they should have pursued proprietary data advantages rather than building proprietary models. However, fundamental improvements are now validating the chosen approach.
