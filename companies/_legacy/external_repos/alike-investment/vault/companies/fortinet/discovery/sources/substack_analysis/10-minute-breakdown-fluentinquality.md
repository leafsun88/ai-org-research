# 10-Minute Breakdown: Is Fortinet Worth Buying Right Now?

This investment analysis examines Fortinet (NASDAQ: FTNT), a cybersecurity firm founded in 2000 that specializes in network security solutions centered around its FortiGate firewall platform.

## Key Business Metrics

Revenue breakdown:
- Product sales: 36.3%
- Security subscriptions: 35.8%
- Technical support services: 27.9%

Total revenue in 2024: $5.96 billion, up 12.3% from 2023. Service revenue reached $3.85 billion, up 19.8%.

## Management & Capital Efficiency

Founders Ken Xie and Michael Xie maintain significant ownership stakes (8.3% and 7.4% respectively), creating strong alignment with shareholders. The company demonstrates exceptional capital efficiency:
- ROCE of 36.6%, the best among competitors
- Improving year-over-year ROIC metrics
- Disciplined SBC management relative to peers

## Financial Health

- Substantial cash and short-term investments
- Strong free cash flow conversion at approximately 32% of revenue
- Improving debt metrics
- Expanding unearned revenue indicating growing long-term customer contracts and recurring revenue stability

## Valuation Assessment

DCF analysis suggests fair value around $83.80 per share at 16% growth assumption. Current pricing implies market expectations of 20%+ FCF growth, which the author deems aggressive given stabilizing margins.

## Investment Conclusion

While acknowledging Fortinet's strong fundamentals and cybersecurity market tailwinds, the analyst would require entry within the $85-$90 range to meet their 10% annual return threshold. Currently placed on watchlist rather than portfolio.

## Competitive Comparison

Fortinet's capital efficiency stands out among cybersecurity peers:
- Higher ROCE than Palo Alto Networks and CrowdStrike
- Lower SBC as percentage of revenue
- Better free cash flow conversion
- More balanced hardware + software revenue model providing pricing flexibility
