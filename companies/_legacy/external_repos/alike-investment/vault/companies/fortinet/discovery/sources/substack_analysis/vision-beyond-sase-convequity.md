# Fortinet: A Vision Beyond SASE

Convequity presents an analysis of Fortinet's strategic positioning in the networking and security landscape, arguing that the company transcends traditional SASE (Secure Access Service Edge) frameworks.

## Fortinet's Competitive Advantage

The analysts highlight Fortinet's distinctive approach through custom silicon development. Their ASIC chips combined with proprietary hardware and FortiOS enable integrated networking and security within single FortiGate appliances, reducing complexity and operational expenses compared to vendors requiring service chaining across multiple systems.

## SASE Evolution to SASO

Convequity proposes shifting from SASE to SASO (Secure Access Service Omni), reflecting the reality that converged networking and security should be implemented wherever it makes most sense economically. This acknowledges hybrid IT environments where some organizations maintain substantial on-premises infrastructure alongside cloud deployments — a model Gartner's original SASE framework underemphasized.

## Three-Mile Framework

The analysis extends beyond first-mile considerations (WAN Edge), examining:

- **First Mile:** SASE/SASO implementation where Fortinet excels through integrated hardware-software approach
- **Middle Mile:** Network interconnectivity where Fortinet pursues proprietary point-of-presence (PoP) expansion
- **Last Mile:** Application delivery where Fortinet maintains established capabilities

## Valuation Assessment

Using DCF methodology with conservative assumptions — 45% terminal free cash flow margin and 10% FY26-FY29 CAGR — analysts project approximately 50% upside potential. Relative valuation analysis indicates Fortinet trades at attractive multiples compared to Palo Alto Networks and CrowdStrike when normalized for growth and profitability metrics.

## Market Context

The article traces Gartner's delayed recognition of Fortinet's SASE capabilities, attributing this partly to institutional biases favoring cloud-delivered solutions. Recent vendor consolidation trends vindicate Fortinet's long-standing integrated architecture strategy.

## Strategic Implications

Fortinet's approach represents a fundamentally different architectural philosophy from pure-cloud competitors:

1. **Hybrid-First:** Acknowledges that most enterprises will maintain hybrid environments for decades
2. **Hardware Advantage:** Custom ASIC chips provide performance and cost advantages that cloud-only vendors cannot match
3. **Platform Convergence:** Single OS (FortiOS) across all form factors simplifies operations
4. **PoP Expansion:** Building own middle-mile infrastructure for cloud-delivered services
5. **AI Integration:** Leveraging hardware performance advantages for real-time AI-powered threat detection
