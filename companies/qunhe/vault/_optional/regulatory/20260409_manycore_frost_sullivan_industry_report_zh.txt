Verifications


• The competitive landscape of China’s spatial design software market is relatively concentrated, with the top three players
  accounting for over 50% of the market share. International competitors such as Autodesk, Inc. (“Autodesk”) hold a significant share
  of the market; however, in recent years, a clear trend of domestic substitution has emerged. Chinese companies, including the
  Issuer and Guangzhou Zhongwang Longteng Software Co., Ltd. (“ZWSOFT”), together command a market share of approximately
  33.0%. Among them, the Issuer has achieved a 23.2% market share, marking an initial realization of domestic substitution in
  China’s spatial design sector.

• Autodesk was founded in 1982 and is headquartered in California, United States. Its principal business is vertical industry design
  software. The company is listed on the NASDAQ under the ticker symbol “ADSK”. It primarily offers four major product lines:
  Architecture, Engineering and Construction (AEC); AutoCAD and AutoCAD LT for 2D & 3D design (general-purpose industrial
  design software); Product Design and Manufacturing (MFG); and Media and Entertainment (M&E).

• Autodesk’s Competitive Advantages vs. the Issuer
      ➢ Product: AutoCAD, launched by Autodesk in 1982, is widely applied across various domains. In contrast, the Issuer focuses
        on spatial design software, enabling a deeper understanding of and alignment with user needs.
      ➢ R&D Investment: The Issuer continuously drives innovation through research and development, maintaining an industry-
        leading R&D expense ratio of approximately 45–73% over the past three years, compared to Autodesk’s 24–25%.
      ➢ Regional Advantage: With a strong presence in China, the Issuer (Kujiale) ranked No.1 in the Chinese market in 2024,
        offering products that better cater to local user preferences and requirements. Autodesk, by comparison, does not prioritize
        the Chinese market, with the Asia-Pacific region accounting for only 18% of its revenue.
      ➢ User Friendliness: The Issuer provides a simple, intuitive interface with a low learning curve, enabling non-professional
        users to adopt its software quickly. Autodesk, however, primarily targets professional designers in industrial and architectural
        fields, where adoption often requires longer training and more complex operations.
